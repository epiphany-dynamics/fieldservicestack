FieldEdge has been around long enough to have a real track record, and at this point, the reviews are clear: it's genuinely solid software for small HVAC, plumbing, and electrical shops. It's not perfect. But if you're running a team of 3-15 techs and your biggest pain is scheduling chaos and QuickBooks reconciliation headaches, it fixes both.

I've spent time with this platform and dug through 304 Capterra reviews and 87 G2 reviews to give you a straight read on whether it's worth the money in 2026.

## What Is FieldEdge?

**FieldEdge is a cloud-based field service management platform built specifically for trades businesses, including HVAC, plumbing, electrical, locksmith, and appliance repair. It connects office and field teams through scheduling, dispatching, invoicing, and a mobile app. Small-to-midsize operations with roughly 3-20 technicians are its primary audience, not enterprise shops.**

It's not trying to be everything to everyone. FieldEdge focuses on the core workflow: job comes in, tech gets dispatched, work gets done, invoice goes out. That sounds simple, but plenty of FSM tools botch at least one of those steps. FieldEdge gets most of them right for smaller operations.

The QuickBooks integration is probably the single biggest reason small businesses choose it. If you're already running QuickBooks and you're tired of double-entry between your accounting software and your scheduling tool, that sync alone makes it worth evaluating.

## FieldEdge Features in 2026

**FieldEdge covers the full job lifecycle: smart dispatching, a mobile app for technicians, customer history and service agreements, built-in payment processing, and basic workflow automations. It handles the lead-to-invoice process without requiring you to stitch together multiple tools. Advanced reporting and deep customization are not its strength, which Capterra reviewers reflect with a 3.9/5 Features score.**

**Scheduling and Dispatching**

The dispatch board is the heart of the platform. FieldEdge assigns jobs based on technician skills, location, and availability. Real-time rerouting helps when things go sideways mid-day, which they always do. Route optimization cuts drive time, which means more jobs completed without burning out your techs.

**Mobile App**

Technicians get customer history, job details, and time tracking on their phones. They can collect payments on-site and use upsell prompts during the visit. Two-way texting keeps customer communication in one place instead of scattered across personal phones. I've seen this feature alone reduce the "I never got that message" complaints at small shops.

**Customer Management and Service Agreements**

Full customer history is available from any device. MarketingEdge handles automated follow-ups: service reminders, email campaigns, renewal outreach. If you're running maintenance agreements, this keeps things from slipping through the cracks when your office is busy.

**Payments and Invoicing**

Built-in FieldEdge Payments handles processing without a third-party add-on. The QuickBooks sync eliminates most manual reconciliation. Most small shops lose hours every week on that, and FieldEdge largely fixes it.

**What's Missing**

Advanced reporting is thin. If you want deep labor tracking, custom dashboards, or granular job costing, FieldEdge is going to leave you short. The 3.9/5 Features score on Capterra says it all: the basics are handled well, but power users hit a wall fast.

## FieldEdge Pricing: What You'll Actually Pay

**FieldEdge uses three pricing tiers: Select for small shops, Premier for growing teams, and Elite for larger operations. Pricing is quote-based and not listed publicly, so you have to contact them for a number. Capterra reviewers rate Value for Money at only 3.9/5, and users across G2 and Capterra consistently flag it as expensive relative to what you get.**

| Plan | Best For | Focus |
|------|----------|-------|
| Select | Solo operators and small shops | Basic scheduling, dispatching, mobile access |
| Premier | Growing teams | Advanced scheduling, automations, team collaboration |
| Elite | Larger operations | Full office and field tools, integrations |

No free trial is mentioned in current reviews. You book a demo, get a custom quote, and go from there. That's standard at this price point, but it makes comparison shopping harder. When you talk to them, ask specifically about per-user costs, what's included at each tier, and whether annual pricing saves you anything meaningful.

My honest read: FieldEdge is priced like a mid-market product but delivers a small-business product. Compared to ServiceTitan, the price gap narrows. Compared to FieldPulse, you're paying a premium that needs to be justified by specific needs, mainly the QuickBooks depth and HVAC-specific dispatching.

## FieldEdge Pros and Cons: What Real Users Say

**Based on 304 Capterra reviews and 87 G2 reviews as of 2026, FieldEdge earns a 4.2/5 on Capterra and 4.0/5 on G2. An 80% recommendation rate from Capterra users is a genuine signal that the core product works. The consistent complaints center on cost, intermittent bugs, and limited features for shops that are trying to grow.**

### What Users Like

The ease of use comes up constantly. G2 reviewers specifically call out the gentle learning curve. New users pick it up without weeks of training, which matters when you're adding a dispatcher mid-season and don't have time for a six-week onboarding process.

Scheduling gets consistent praise. The dispatch board is described as intuitive, and smart dispatching actually works in practice. For most small shops, that's the biggest day-to-day win.

QuickBooks sync appears in almost every positive review. It connects with QuickBooks Online and holds up in daily use. That eliminates a significant source of weekly manual work for shops that are otherwise doing double-entry.

Workflow efficiency is the overall theme. The 80% recommendation rate comes from users who say the platform lets small teams handle more jobs without adding headcount.

Customer support scores 4.4/5 on Capterra, one of the higher-rated categories. The experience is inconsistent though, and that shows up in the critical reviews too.

### What Users Don't Like

Bugs and reliability are the top complaints. Connectivity issues and bugs that take too long to get fixed come up repeatedly across both G2 and Capterra. Not a dealbreaker for most users, but a recurring frustration.

The cost-to-value ratio bothers people. Value for Money sits at only 3.9/5 on Capterra. Multiple reviewers describe the price as "unjustified" given the feature limitations, especially for anything beyond basic scheduling.

Outside of QuickBooks, integrations are thin. If you need to connect to other business tools, plan on friction and manual workarounds. One G2 reviewer specifically calls out the "lack of capabilities in one dashboard."

Scalability is the deal-breaker for growing shops. Advanced reporting, custom dashboards, and labor tracking are weak spots. Features scores only 3.9/5 on Capterra, and a YouTube review specifically flags labor tracking as a gap. Fine for daily ops; struggles when your needs get more complex.

Support consistency is a real issue. Despite the decent Capterra number, G2 reviewers flag poor customer support frequently. The quality of help you get seems to vary a lot depending on the issue and the timing.

## How FieldEdge Compares to ServiceTitan and FieldPulse

**FieldEdge sits between budget tools and enterprise platforms. ServiceTitan beats it on reporting, labor tracking, and handling larger operations. FieldPulse undercuts it on price with a more modern interface. FieldEdge's edge is its QuickBooks integration depth and the relative speed of getting a small team up and running without heavy implementation costs.**

| Software | Beats FieldEdge On | Falls Short On | Best Fit |
|----------|--------------------|---------------|----------|
| ServiceTitan | Reporting, labor tracking, automations, enterprise scale | Cost, complexity, onboarding time | Large HVAC/plumbing operations with aggressive growth targets |
| FieldPulse | Price, modern UI, flexibility for small teams | HVAC-specific feature depth, QuickBooks integration | Budget-conscious SMBs in general field service |
| Housecall Pro | Broader integrations, lower entry cost | Specialized dispatching for HVAC/plumbing | General field service, non-HVAC trades |

If you're running 20-plus techs and need detailed job costing and payroll reporting, ServiceTitan is worth the added cost and complexity. If you're a 3-person plumbing shop watching expenses, FieldPulse deserves a serious look before you commit anywhere. FieldEdge fits the middle: established small business, HVAC or plumbing focus, already committed to QuickBooks.

## Who Should Actually Use FieldEdge?

**FieldEdge is the right fit for HVAC, plumbing, electrical, and similar trades businesses with roughly 3-20 technicians that are already using QuickBooks and need scheduling software that doesn't require a consultant to get running. It's a poor fit for businesses planning rapid growth or needing advanced reporting, labor tracking, and broad integrations.**

I'd recommend FieldEdge to a shop if:

- They're running an established HVAC or plumbing operation with 5-15 field techs
- They're currently managing dispatch on a whiteboard or in a spreadsheet
- They're already on QuickBooks and don't want to disrupt their accounting setup
- They need something a new dispatcher can learn in a week, not six weeks of onboarding

I'd tell them to keep looking if:

- They're planning to double headcount in the next 12-18 months
- They need strong labor tracking and detailed job costing reports
- They want a broad integration ecosystem beyond QuickBooks
- They're prioritizing price above everything else (FieldPulse is worth a serious look first)

The software does what it says. It's not cheap, and it's not flashy. But for the right shop, it solves real problems that are costing real money every week.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is FieldEdge worth the money in 2026?**

For small HVAC, plumbing, or electrical shops already using QuickBooks, it's defensible. The 80% recommendation rate from 304 Capterra reviewers is a genuine signal. But at only 3.9/5 for Value for Money on Capterra, it's not an obvious yes. Compare it to FieldPulse before signing anything, especially if you're cost-sensitive or don't specifically need the QuickBooks depth.

**How much does FieldEdge cost?**

FieldEdge doesn't publish pricing publicly. It uses three tiers: Select, Premier, and Elite, all quote-based and scaled to team size. Users across G2 and Capterra consistently describe it as expensive. Plan to negotiate, ask about annual pricing discounts, and get a clear breakdown of what's included at each tier before you sign. There is no publicized free trial.

**How does FieldEdge compare to ServiceTitan?**

ServiceTitan is more capable on reporting, labor tracking, and managing larger teams, but it costs more and takes longer to implement. FieldEdge earns a 4.2/5 on Capterra while ServiceTitan scores higher overall. For a smaller shop where simplicity matters more than feature depth, FieldEdge is a reasonable tradeoff. For a 20-plus tech operation with serious growth plans, ServiceTitan is worth the investment.

**Does FieldEdge integrate with QuickBooks?**

Yes, and it's the strongest part of the platform. FieldEdge integrates with QuickBooks Online, and the sync is consistently praised across G2 and Capterra reviews. Outside of QuickBooks, the integration story is thin. If you need broad third-party connectivity, plan your expectations accordingly: the "lack of capabilities in one dashboard" is one of the most common complaints in G2 reviews.
