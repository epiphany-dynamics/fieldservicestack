<p><strong>Jobber is a legitimate, mature field service management platform trusted by over 200,000 service professionals in HVAC, plumbing, landscaping, electrical, and cleaning. Plans start at $49/month for solo operators and reach $249/month for teams up to 15 users (billed annually). It handles the full job lifecycle from quote to payment, with reliable QuickBooks Online sync and a solid mobile app.</strong></p>

<h2>Jobber as a Company: Stability and Track Record</h2>

<p><strong>Jobber launched in 2011 out of Edmonton, Alberta, and has grown into one of the dominant platforms for small and mid-size residential service businesses. They've raised over $370 million in funding and processed more than $10 billion in homeowner payments. That's a real company with a real track record, not a bootstrapped side project that disappears when the market shifts.</strong></p>

<p>When I evaluate any software company for a trade business, the first question is whether they actually understand the industry or just think they do. Jobber focuses almost entirely on residential and light-commercial service verticals: HVAC, plumbing, electrical, landscaping, cleaning, pest control, general contracting. Their feature decisions reflect that focus. They're not trying to build one platform for hospitals, manufacturers, and roofers simultaneously. That specificity is a genuine advantage.</p>

<p>They've also been around long enough to build a product that holds up. This isn't a startup on version 1.4 that crashes during a busy Monday dispatch window. Jobber ships regular updates, maintains a public changelog, and offers support by phone, chat, and email from people who can actually help, not a chatbot that routes you in circles. For a solo plumber or a 5-truck HVAC company, knowing you can call someone when something breaks before a big install day matters more than any feature comparison chart.</p>

<h2>Key Features: What Jobber Actually Does Well</h2>

<p><strong>Jobber's strongest features are the ones you use every day: scheduling, quoting, invoicing, and automated client communication. The full loop from estimate approval to payment collection is polished and actually works in the field. Where it shows limits is in inventory management, GPS fleet tracking, and deep financial reporting, gaps that matter more at larger scale.</strong></p>

<h3>Scheduling and Dispatch</h3>

<p>The calendar view is the thing you'll live in. Drag-and-drop scheduling, color-coded by tech or job type, with push notifications to the crew when assignments change. For a 3-5 truck HVAC company, this replaces a whiteboard and a group text chain. That sounds basic because it is, but there are a lot of small operations still running on exactly those tools, and the switch alone changes how organized your dispatch feels by the end of week one.</p>

<p>Route optimization is available on Connect and Grow plans. It won't replace a dedicated routing platform if you're running 20-plus stops, but for a landscaping crew doing 8-10 residential accounts per day it trims drive time without requiring a separate subscription. Worth having, even if it's not the headline feature.</p>

<h3>Quoting and Invoicing</h3>

<p>Online quotes are where Jobber earns its reputation. Build a quote, send it by text or email, the client approves it from their phone without a callback. When the job's done, converting that approved quote to an invoice takes two clicks. Payment link goes out automatically. The client pays from wherever they are.</p>

<p>For a solo plumber doing 8-10 service calls a day, that workflow is the difference between getting paid within 48 hours and chasing someone for three weeks. The invoice chase is where a lot of small service operations quietly bleed cash, not from bad work, but from bad follow-through on paper. Jobber fixes that specific problem reliably.</p>

<h3>Client Hub and Communication</h3>

<p>Every client gets a self-service portal where they can view job history, approve estimates, and pay invoices. Appointment reminders fire automatically by text and email before each job, which cuts no-shows. Post-job review requests can be automated on a configurable delay. None of this requires manual effort once it's set up.</p>

<p>This is not a sales CRM with lead pipelines and deal stages. What it does is make existing customers feel like they're working with a professional company, which has a measurable effect on repeat bookings and referrals, especially when you're competing against larger regional operators who've invested in customer experience infrastructure.</p>

<h3>Mobile App</h3>

<p>Available on iOS and Android. Techs see their schedule, pull up job details and client notes, attach before-and-after photos, collect signatures, and mark jobs complete from the site. The app works offline and syncs when signal returns, which matters in basements, rural properties, and anywhere cell coverage is unreliable. That's a real-world consideration a lot of software reviewers don't test for.</p>

<p>The app is stable. It doesn't log techs out randomly or freeze during checkout. That sounds like a minimum expectation, but several competitors in this price range haven't cleared that bar consistently.</p>

<h3>QuickBooks Integration</h3>

<p>Two-way sync with QuickBooks Online. Invoices, payments, and client records flow between both platforms. You'll want to map your chart of accounts carefully during initial setup, but once it's configured it runs in the background without constant babysitting. QuickBooks Desktop users get a worse experience: more manual export/import work and less reliable sync. If you're still on Desktop, that's worth factoring into your decision before you commit.</p>

<h2>Pros and Cons</h2>

<p><strong>Jobber's strengths are usability, the quote-to-payment workflow, and a mobile app that holds up in real field conditions. The weaknesses are inventory tracking, fleet GPS depth, financial reporting granularity, and scalability past 15 users. For most 1-15 person service businesses, the strengths cover the gaps by a wide margin.</strong></p>

<h3>What Works</h3>

<ul>
  <li>Clean interface that a non-technical owner can learn in a few days without a dedicated trainer</li>
  <li>Automated client communication genuinely reduces no-shows and speeds up invoice payment cycles</li>
  <li>Online booking captures jobs while you're already on other calls</li>
  <li>Mobile app is stable, works offline, and doesn't lose job data when signal drops</li>
  <li>QuickBooks Online sync is reliable for standard day-to-day accounting use</li>
  <li>Phone, chat, and email support from people who know what a dispatch board is</li>
  <li>14-day free trial with actual functionality, not a sandboxed demo that hides the real product</li>
</ul>

<h3>What Doesn't</h3>

<ul>
  <li>No built-in inventory or parts tracking, a real gap for HVAC and plumbing shops managing van stock</li>
  <li>GPS fleet tracking is surface-level and not a substitute for a dedicated fleet tool</li>
  <li>Job profitability reporting lacks the granularity that businesses with tight cost-per-job accounting need</li>
  <li>Pricing becomes a custom conversation past 15 users, which limits forward planning</li>
  <li>QuickBooks Desktop users get a noticeably degraded experience compared to QBO users</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Jobber fits service businesses with 1-15 employees best, specifically in HVAC, plumbing, electrical, landscaping, cleaning, and pest control. Solo operators who need to professionalize fast and small crews battling dispatch chaos or slow collections will see the most immediate impact. Operations with 20-plus techs or complex job costing requirements will start hitting its ceiling.</strong></p>

<p>Solo operators still running on texts and spreadsheets: Core at $49/month changes how your business feels to operate within the first couple of weeks. Automated invoicing and online booking alone pay for the subscription on the first or second job. The bigger return is time, specifically the hours you stop spending on follow-ups, reminders, and chasing payments that already should have been collected.</p>

<p>Running 3-8 trucks with a dispatch board that's a mess and invoice cycles that drag out past two weeks: Connect is where you land. Route optimization, two-way texting, and QuickBooks sync are the features that change day-to-day operations at that scale. Those aren't checkbox features. They solve the actual problems that eat time at a 5-truck operation.</p>
