Pricing starts around $398/month and most real installs run $600-$1,500/month once you add features. <h2>What Is ServiceTitan?</h2>

<p><strong>ServiceTitan is an all-in-one field service platform covering scheduling, dispatch, CRM, invoicing, payroll, marketing automation, and reporting. Built specifically for residential and commercial trades - HVAC, plumbing, electrical, roofing, pest control - it's the most feature-complete FSM platform available in 2026. The pricing reflects that.</strong></p>

<p>ServiceTitan launched in 2012, built by two founders who grew up in HVAC families. That origin story actually matters here. The dispatch board, the call booking workflow, the service agreement management - this stuff was designed by people who understood what a no-show at 7am on a Monday in July actually costs you. It's not a generic project management tool dressed up in field service clothes.</p>

<p>By 2026, they've expanded considerably: acquired complementary platforms, built out an equipment financing integration, launched AI-assisted scheduling, and grown a contractor marketplace. If you evaluated ServiceTitan back in 2022 and passed, it's worth another look. The product has changed.</p>

<p>That said, the core question - is ServiceTitan worth it - still comes down to your business size and what you actually need. If you're piecing together QuickBooks, a Google Calendar, and a shared spreadsheet for dispatch, ServiceTitan will feel like a revelation. If you're a solo plumber doing 8-10 calls a day, it's going to feel like flying a 747 to the grocery store. The <a href="/guides/field-service-management-buyers-guide">field service management buyer's guide for 2026</a> is worth reading if you're not sure where ServiceTitan fits relative to the broader market before you sit through a demo.</p>

<h2>Key Features</h2>

<p><strong>ServiceTitan covers drag-and-drop dispatch, GPS fleet tracking, flat-rate pricebook with good-better-best pricing tiers, service agreement tracking, marketing automation with campaign ROI reporting, and detailed business dashboards. The tech-facing mobile app is genuinely polished. It's one of the few platforms where techs don't immediately resent the app they're handed on day one.</strong></p>

<h3>Scheduling and Dispatch</h3>
<p>The dispatch board is what most people reference first when they talk about ServiceTitan. You see every tech, every job, every open time slot in real time. Drag to reassign, check estimated drive times, flag who's running behind. For a 15-truck HVAC crew managing 40-plus calls on a July day, this is the difference between controlled chaos and actual chaos. Every dispatcher I've talked to who has used it says it cuts down on the phone back-and-forth significantly - not a little, a lot.</p>

<h3>Tech Mobile App</h3>
<p>Techs pull up job details, customer history, photos from prior visits, the pricebook, and payment collection from the same screen. The good-better-best option presentation is built in, and the data consistently shows it lifts average ticket values when techs actually use it. GPS check-in and check-out are automatic, which helps with payroll accuracy and holds techs accountable without making it feel oppressive.</p>

<h3>Marketing and CRM</h3>
<p>Most FSM tools stop at job management. ServiceTitan goes further by tracking customer acquisition source, supporting email and SMS campaigns, and integrating with Google Ads to connect ad spend to actual booked revenue. If you're spending $5,000 a month on marketing and genuinely have no idea which channel is driving jobs, that's a problem ServiceTitan actually solves. Most platforms don't even try.</p>

<h3>Reporting</h3>
<p>Revenue by tech, revenue by job type, first-call completion rate, marketing ROI, conversion rate from estimate to sold job. The reporting suite is comprehensive enough that some owners find it overwhelming at first. That's not a knock. After 60-90 days of real data, you'll know exactly which three or four numbers actually run your business.</p>

<h3>QuickBooks Integration</h3>
<p>ServiceTitan syncs with both QuickBooks Online and Desktop. Invoices, payments, and customer records push over without manual entry. Like any integration, it needs correct initial setup and you'll occasionally troubleshoot sync issues, but it's stable enough that most shops drop their duplicate-entry workarounds within the first month.</p>

<h2>ServiceTitan Pricing in 2026</h2>

<p><strong>ServiceTitan doesn't publish pricing publicly. Based on community-reported data, the Starter plan runs around $398/month, Essentials around $598/month, and larger full-feature builds typically land between $1,000-$2,000/month. Setup fees of $1,000-$5,000 are standard. Annual contracts are required. Marketing automation, GPS, and payroll add-ons cost extra on top of the base plan.</strong></p>

<p>The lack of public pricing is a calculated move. They want to see your business before quoting, which lets them price relative to your size and budget. This is common in enterprise SaaS, but it's still annoying. It also means two shops with similar needs may have gotten meaningfully different quotes based on who they talked to and how the demo went.</p>

<h2>Who Is ServiceTitan Best For?</h2>

<p><strong>ServiceTitan is best for HVAC, plumbing, electrical, and pest control businesses with 5 or more techs and at least $1M in annual revenue. Multi-location and franchise operators get outsized value from its centralized management tools. It's a poor fit for solo operators and small crews who need straightforward job management without the enterprise overhead.</strong></p>

<p><strong>2-4 techs, under $500K revenue:</strong> ServiceTitan will overwhelm you and strain your budget. The onboarding alone takes weeks your team can't spare when you're already dispatching every job manually. For this stage, there are solid tools at $50-$150/month that cover the essentials well. The <a href="/guides/cheapest-field-service-software">cheapest field service software options for 2026</a> is worth reading before committing to enterprise pricing you don't yet need.</p>

<p><strong>5-15 techs, $1M-$5M revenue:</strong> This is ServiceTitan's core market. You're probably already running multiple disconnected tools and feeling the friction: one app for dispatch, another for invoicing, QuickBooks on the side, and someone manually updating a spreadsheet somewhere. The platform math works at this volume. The dispatch board and marketing tools start paying for themselves when your call volume is high enough to matter.</p>

<p><strong>15-plus techs, multiple locations:</strong> ServiceTitan is the clear answer here. Nothing else handles multi-location management, centralized dispatch across branches, and franchise-level performance reporting as well at this scale. The monthly cost is a rounding error relative to payroll at this size.</p>

<h2>The Verdict: Is ServiceTitan Worth It?</h2>

<p><strong>ServiceTitan earns its reputation as the most capable FSM platform in the residential trades in 2026. For operations with 5-plus techs and over $1M in revenue, it's worth the investment. For smaller shops, the pricing and complexity are hard to justify when leaner tools handle the day-to-day at a fraction of the cost.</strong></p>

<p>My rating: 8.5 out of 10. If you're running a 20-truck plumbing operation, this is probably a 9.5 for you. If you're running 2 techs out of your garage, it's closer to a 5 because you're paying enterprise rates for a platform that's four times the size of your actual needs.</p>

<p>The two things that keep it from a higher overall score: the pricing opacity (just publish the prices - every other SaaS product manages to do it) and the support experience at lower tiers, which is a recurring complaint from shops that hit a critical dispatch issue on a busy morning and can't get a fast answer.</p>

<p>If you're going into a demo, bring a clear list of your top operational pain points and ask them to show you exactly how the platform solves each one. Push for a written price quote before the presentation ends - not a follow-up email, an actual number in the room. Read the contract closely, especially the cancellation terms and auto-renewal clauses.</p>

<p>For the right business at the right stage, ServiceTitan pays for itself. The key is being honest about which category you're actually in before you sign a 12-month contract.</p>
