<p><strong>Fieldd is a field service management platform built for mobile and on-demand service businesses, starting at around $99/month for solo operators. It outperforms Jobber and Housecall Pro on customer booking experience and real-time GPS dispatch, but it's a niche fit. Cleaning companies, mobile groomers, and detailing operations will get the most out of it. Traditional trades like HVAC and plumbing should look elsewhere.</strong></p>

<h2>What to Look For Before You Pick a Platform</h2>

<p><strong>For any mobile service business, the non-negotiables are customer self-booking that doesn't require a phone call, real-time GPS so clients stop asking "where's my tech?", invoicing that syncs to QuickBooks without manual exports, and a mobile app your field guys will actually open. Pricing transparency matters too. Per-tech fees add up fast once you grow past a 3-person crew.</strong></p>

<p>If you're currently running dispatch from a shared Google Calendar or a whiteboard, any modern FSM platform will feel like a serious upgrade. The question isn't whether software beats spreadsheets. It's whether the specific platform you pick fits the way your business actually runs, not how you think it runs.</p>

<p>Fieldd gets searched primarily by cleaning companies, mobile pet groomers, auto detailers, and mobile beauty operators. That tracks with what it was built for. If you're an HVAC shop with 8 vans and a service department, this probably isn't your tool. We'll get into that in the picks below.</p>

<p>One thing worth flagging upfront: watch the tier walls on pricing. Platforms that look cheap at the entry level often double or triple per month once you add your fourth or fifth tech. Also check whether QuickBooks integration is included across all plans. Several FSM platforms bury it behind higher tiers and call it a "feature," which is annoying when invoicing is the main reason you're switching software in the first place.</p>

<h2>Fieldd Reviewed: What It Does Well and Where It Falls Short</h2>

<p><strong>Fieldd's biggest differentiator is the white-labeled customer app. Clients download your branded app, book a slot, watch the tech move toward them on GPS, and pay when the job's done. That end-to-end experience is noticeably better than what Jobber or Housecall Pro offer out of the box, especially for service models where the customer relationship is half the product.</strong></p>

<p>Cleaning company owners who've switched to Fieldd consistently mention that real-time GPS tracking alone cut their "where is the cleaner?" phone calls by more than half. If you're dispatching 6 to 8 people a day, that's a real time savings for whoever's managing the office. The customer communication side is built in, so appointment reminders and post-job follow-ups run without anyone manually sending texts.</p>

<p>The automated scheduling is the other feature that gets talked about in Fieldd reviews. It uses location and availability data to optimize booking slots in real time, without someone manually rebuilding the calendar puzzle. For operations running tight geographic clusters, the drive time reduction adds up.</p>

<p>Here's where Fieldd falls short, and it's worth being direct about this. It's a younger platform. The integration library is thinner than Jobber or Housecall Pro. If your accounting workflow is built around QuickBooks and you rely on Zapier to stitch together a handful of other tools, you'll hit limits. The platform is clearly optimized for consumer-facing mobile services. B2B commercial work with complex job costing, multi-location contracts, or equipment maintenance records doesn't fit well. There's also a smaller user community, which matters when you're trying to troubleshoot something at 7 PM before a big day.</p>

<p>Pricing: approximately $99/month for solo operators, around $199/month for small teams, and $299 or more for growing businesses with added techs. A per-tech fee structure kicks in above certain team sizes. A free trial is available, and you should run your actual workflow through it before signing up for anything.</p>

<h2>Top Picks: Fieldd and the Best Alternatives in 2026</h2>

<p><strong>Fieldd is the top pick for mobile and on-demand service businesses. Jobber is the best all-around option for most trades. Housecall Pro fits HVAC and plumbing teams best. Workiz is worth a look for appliance repair and locksmith operations. Kickserv is the right call when budget is the deciding factor above everything else.</strong></p>

<h3>1. Fieldd — Best for Mobile and On-Demand Services</h3>

<p>The white-labeled booking app is the real competitive advantage here. If your business model involves customers tracking a tech to their door or driveway, Fieldd offers the most polished experience in the category. The automated scheduling works well for geographically dense routes. Customer communication is baked in. The staff app is genuinely good, not an afterthought.</p>

<p>The gaps are real, though. Integration depth lags behind the established platforms. Complex commercial work isn't what it was designed for, and you'll feel that friction if you try to push it there. For the right business type, specifically cleaning, mobile grooming, detailing, or mobile beauty, it's the strongest option available.</p>

<p>Pricing starts at approximately $99/month. QuickBooks integration is available on paid plans. Free trial offered.</p>

<h3>2. Jobber — Best All-Around for Most Trades</h3>

<p>For <a href="/comparisons/jobber-vs-housecall-pro">most businesses weighing Jobber against Housecall Pro</a>, Jobber wins on overall breadth without requiring a dedicated admin to configure it. Quotes, scheduling, invoicing, and customer communications work in one place. The mobile app holds up in the field. QuickBooks sync is solid. The reporting gives you enough job profitability visibility without becoming a second job to manage.</p>

<p>If you're running a 3-truck HVAC crew or a plumbing shop doing 15 to 20 calls a week, Jobber is probably your answer. The Core plan at $99/month covers most small operation needs without paying for features you won't use.</p>

<p>Best for: HVAC, plumbing, electrical, landscaping, general contracting. Starts at $49/month.</p>

<h3>3. Housecall Pro — Best for HVAC and Plumbing Operations</h3>

<p>Housecall Pro has deep roots in HVAC and plumbing specifically, and it shows. The dispatch board, automated follow-ups, and membership plan features are genuinely well-developed for those trades. <a href="/reviews/housecall-pro-pricing-2026">Housecall Pro's pricing</a> starts around $79/month and climbs quickly once you add features, but if you're doing maintenance agreements and recurring service contracts, the recurring-job infrastructure justifies it.</p>

<p>The downside: it's not as intuitive as Jobber for teams that don't have someone dedicated to learning a new system. For a solo plumber doing 8 to 10 calls a day, it can feel like too much platform for the actual workload.</p>

<p>Best for: HVAC companies, plumbing, businesses selling maintenance agreements. Starts at approximately $79/month.</p>

<h3>4. Workiz — Best for Appliance Repair and Locksmith</h3>

<p>Workiz punches above its weight on call tracking and the built-in phone system. For appliance repair shops or locksmith operations where inbound call volume is high and you want calls automatically tied to jobs, it handles that better than most FSM platforms in this price range. The <a href="/comparisons/workiz-vs-jobber-2026">Workiz vs. Jobber comparison</a> comes down to whether you care more about phone-integrated dispatch or a broader feature set with better reporting.</p>

<p>Pricing starts around $65/month. The interface has improved. Still not as refined as Jobber or Fieldd, but the phone integration is a real differentiator for the right shop type.</p>

<p>Best for: Appliance repair, locksmith, garage door service. Starts at approximately $65/month.</p>

<h3>5. Kickserv — Best Budget Option</h3>

<p>If budget is the hard constraint, Kickserv starts at roughly $47/month for a small team. It's not polished. The mobile app lags behind the competition. But it covers scheduling, invoicing, and basic customer management without a steep learning curve. For <a href="/comparisons/kickserv-vs-jobber-2026">teams comparing Kickserv to Jobber</a>, the honest take is that Jobber is the better platform if you can afford it. Kickserv is the right pick when you can't, and there's no shame in that.</p>

<p>Best for: Solo operators, very small teams, businesses that need core features at the lowest monthly cost. Starts at approximately $47/month.</p>

<h2>How to Choose Based on Your Specific Business</h2>

<p><strong>The decision is fairly direct. Mobile service business where customers track your tech to their location? Fieldd is worth the premium. Traditional trade operation doing HVAC, plumbing, electrical, or landscaping? Jobber is the safer default. Running on a tight budget with a small crew? Kickserv gets core functions handled at a lower monthly cost. Match the platform to how your customers interact with you, not just how your team uses it internally.</strong></p>

<p>If you're a cleaning company doing residential recurring service with 5 to 10 cleaners, Fieldd is the most natural fit. The automated scheduling and customer tracking app cut admin overhead in ways that are real and measurable. A lot of cleaning operations are still running on Google Calendar and group texts. The gap between that and what <a href="/guides/best-field-service-software-small-cleaning-businesses">modern field service software built for small cleaning businesses</a> offers is significant, and Fieldd sits at the top of that category.</p>

<p>For HVAC, Fieldd isn't the right tool. You need equipment tracking, multi-visit job management, service agreements, and pricing structures that Fieldd doesn't handle well. Housecall Pro or Jobber is the better call there. If you're at a larger scale with commercial contracts and a bigger team, the comparison between enterprise-tier options becomes relevant and the price jump gets steep in a hurry.</p>

<p>Pest control, landscaping, and pool service operators generally do well with Jobber. The recurring job scheduling and basic route management work without much configuration effort. The platform was built broad enough that most trade types can operate in it without hacking around limitations.</p>

<p>One scenario worth calling out: if you're choosing between Fieldd and Jobber specifically for a cleaning business, trial both. Fieldd's customer experience is better. Jobber's overall feature depth is better. Which one matters more depends on whether your biggest pain point is customer communication or internal job management. You won't know for sure until you put your actual workflow through both platforms.</p>

<h2>Our Recommendation</h2>

<p><strong>Fieldd is the top pick for mobile and on-demand service businesses in 2026. For most other trades, Jobber is the safer all-around choice. Don't pay for Housecall Pro or Workiz unless you're specifically in the trade types those platforms were built around. Budget-first buyers should start with Kickserv and upgrade once the business justifies it.</strong></p>

<p>Fieldd built something genuinely different with the white-labeled customer app and real-time GPS tracking. For cleaning companies and similar services, that's not a marketing feature. It solves a real operational problem and the customer experience improvement is noticeable. If your office phone rings constantly with "where's my cleaner?" calls, that alone is worth evaluating Fieldd seriously.</p>

<p>That said, Fieldd is a younger company competing against platforms with years of development behind them. The integration library is thinner. The user community is smaller, which matters when you're searching for a specific workaround or trying to find someone who's solved your exact problem before. Support is responsive but not yet as tested as what Jobber or Housecall Pro offer. You're trading platform maturity for a better customer-facing experience. For some businesses that's the right trade. For others it isn't.</p>

<p>Run the free trial. Map your actual workflow through it, not the ideal version of your workflow. Figure out the two or three things your team would use every single day, and choose based on how well each platform handles those specific functions. That's a better decision framework than picking the software with the best demo.</p>
<h2>Frequently Asked Questions</h2>
<h3>Q: How much does Fieldd cost when you have a 5-person team?</h3>
<p>Fieldd charges per-technician fees, which means costs scale significantly as your crew grows—the platform warns that bills can double or triple once you pass 3 technicians. Starting at around $99/month for solo operators, you'll need a custom quote to predict your actual cost at team size.</p>
<h3>Q: Does Fieldd include QuickBooks integration or is it extra?</h3>
<p>QuickBooks sync is included across all Fieldd pricing tiers, unlike some competitors that reserve it for higher-tier plans. This eliminates manual invoice exports and keeps your accounting data current without the spreadsheet shuffle.</p>
<h3>Q: Which type of service business is Fieldd best for?</h3>
<p>Fieldd is purpose-built for cleaning companies, mobile groomers, auto detailers, and mobile beauty operators—these niches get the most value from its features. If you're an HVAC or plumbing shop, Jobber or ServiceTitan are better fits because Fieldd was designed with smaller, mobile-first service businesses in mind.</p>
<h3>Q: Can customers book and track jobs on Fieldd?</h3>
<p>Yes—Fieldd provides a white-labeled booking portal where customers can self-schedule without calling, and track their technician's GPS location in real-time. This customer experience is one area where Fieldd outperforms Housecall Pro and similar platforms.</p>
