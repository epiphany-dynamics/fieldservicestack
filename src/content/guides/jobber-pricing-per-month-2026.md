<p><strong>Jobber pricing per month starts at $19 for the Lite plan and goes up to $299 for Grow, both on annual billing. Pay month-to-month and add 30-50% to each tier. Most small crews of 2-5 technicians land on Connect at $149/month. The Grow plan at $299/month makes sense for teams of 6-15 who need automated follow-ups and lead management built in.</strong></p>

<h2>What to Look For Beyond the Monthly Price Tag</h2>

<p><strong>When comparing FSM pricing, the plan cost is only part of the story. Card processing fees, user limits, QuickBooks integration tier, and mobile app quality all affect your real monthly spend and day-to-day usability. A platform that looks $50/month cheaper can flip quickly if the accounting integration you need is locked behind the highest tier.</strong></p>

<p>The monthly number is the easy part to compare. What actually matters when you're evaluating field service software pricing:</p>

<ul>
  <li>User limits per tier (some platforms charge per user, others cap by plan)</li>
  <li>Whether QuickBooks sync is included at your tier or reserved for higher plans</li>
  <li>Payment processing fees that stack on top of the subscription</li>
  <li>Mobile app quality for techs in the field who aren't sitting at a desk</li>
  <li>Whether GPS tracking and dispatch visibility are included or cost extra</li>
</ul>

<p>Keep those in mind as we go through each option. The price you see on the pricing page is the floor, not the ceiling.</p>

<h2>Jobber Pricing Per Month: Full Plan Breakdown</h2>

<p><strong>Jobber's four 2026 plans are Lite ($19/month), Core ($49/month), Connect ($149/month), and Grow ($299/month), all on annual billing. Pay month-to-month and those prices jump to $29, $79, $199, and $449. Connect is the most widely used plan for small crews and is where QuickBooks sync and GPS tracking become available.</strong></p>

<h3>Lite: $19/month (annual) / $29/month (monthly)</h3>

<p>For solo operators who need basic quoting and invoicing and nothing more. One user, no online booking, no client notifications. Honest assessment: if you're serious about your business, you'll outgrow this plan in 90 days. It exists as an entry point, not a long-term operating setup. Use it if you're just getting started and need something better than a spreadsheet to track jobs.</p>

<h3>Core: $49/month (annual) / $79/month (monthly)</h3>

<p>Still one user, but online booking, automated appointment reminders, and job forms are included. This is where Jobber starts to feel worth paying for. A solo plumber doing 8-10 calls a day with someone handling dispatch can run the whole operation from Core. The single-user cap is the hard wall. The moment you need two logins, you're either on Connect or splitting screens in ways that don't actually work.</p>

<h3>Connect: $149/month (annual) / $199/month (monthly)</h3>

<p>This is where most service businesses actually live. Up to 5 users, GPS tracking, two-way text messaging with customers, expense tracking, and QuickBooks Online sync. If you're running a 3-truck HVAC crew and your dispatcher needs to see where everyone is while you're pulling wire or crawling through an attic, Connect is the plan that makes Jobber genuinely useful. The QuickBooks integration at this tier is clean. It handles invoicing, payments, and expense categories without the workarounds that some competing platforms require.</p>

<p>Worth factoring in: the payment processing fee of 2.9% + $0.30 per card transaction applies on top of the plan cost. If your monthly card volume is $30,000, that's roughly $900 in processing fees on top of the $149 base. Factor it in before you compare sticker prices with competitors.</p>

<p>For a deeper look at how the features hold up in actual daily use beyond the pricing tiers, the <a href="/reviews/jobber-field-service-management-company-evaluation-review-2026">full Jobber field service management evaluation</a> covers how each plan performs in real field conditions.</p>

<h3>Grow: $299/month (annual) / $449/month (monthly)</h3>

<p>Up to 15 users, automated quote follow-ups, lead source tracking, and a built-in marketing suite. The features are real. The question is whether your operation actually uses them. Automated quote follow-up matters if you're sending 50+ quotes a month and losing track of which ones haven't responded. If you're sending 15 quotes a month and know each customer by name, you're paying $150/month more than Connect for tools that sit unused.</p>

<p>If you're questioning whether Grow is justified for your team size, <a href="/guides/why-is-jobber-so-expensive">this breakdown of what Jobber's higher tiers actually buy you</a> is worth reading before you commit to an annual plan.</p>

<h2>Top Alternatives to Jobber in 2026</h2>

<p><strong>The four strongest alternatives to Jobber at comparable price points are Housecall Pro, Workiz, FieldPulse, and Service Fusion. Housecall Pro is the most direct competitor, with comparable pricing and a stronger entry-tier QuickBooks offering. Workiz runs cheaper at mid-tier. FieldPulse is more flexible on user counts. Service Fusion costs more but gives you unlimited users at every tier.</strong></p>

<h3>1. Housecall Pro: $49 / $129 / $299 per month</h3>

<p>The closest match to Jobber, tier for tier. Basic at $49 includes QuickBooks integration right out of the gate, where Jobber makes you step up to Connect ($149/month) for that same capability. Essentials at $129 covers up to 5 users and hits most of the feature set Jobber puts on Connect. Growth at $299 is unlimited users, which Jobber doesn't offer at any price point.</p>

<p>The mobile app is strong, the customer-facing experience is polished, and the onboarding is comparable to Jobber's. The <a href="/comparisons/jobber-vs-housecall-pro">Jobber vs Housecall Pro comparison</a> goes deeper on where each one actually wins, but if QuickBooks integration is your first requirement, Housecall Pro gets you there cheaper.</p>

<h3>2. Workiz: $45 / $95 / $195 per month</h3>

<p>Workiz runs cheaper at every comparable tier. Two users at $45, five users at $95, ten users at $195. The interface isn't as polished as Jobber's, but the core functionality is solid: scheduling, dispatch, invoicing, and customer communications. For a small electrical or pest control operation focused on job throughput over client-portal presentation, Workiz holds its own. The <a href="/comparisons/workiz-vs-jobber-2026">Workiz vs Jobber breakdown</a> covers where the feature gaps actually show up in day-to-day use.</p>

<h3>3. FieldPulse: $99 / $199 per month</h3>

<p>FieldPulse is worth a look if Jobber's user caps are the sticking point. The pricing runs higher than Workiz but is more flexible on how many users you can add without jumping a full tier. QuickBooks and Xero integrations are included, the mobile app is reliable, and the job costing features are more detailed than what Jobber offers at Connect. Less name recognition than Jobber or Housecall Pro, but the product is solid for growing teams with tighter budgets.</p>

<h3>4. Service Fusion: $195 / $295 / $495 per month</h3>

<p>Costs more at every tier but offers unlimited users on all plans. If you're running a crew where five logins won't be enough and you don't want to jump to Grow pricing just to add a couple more technician logins, Service Fusion's structure can make the math work. The mobile app is less polished than Jobber's, and the interface takes longer to learn. Usually overkill for anything under 8-10 active users, but the unlimited user model matters when you hit a certain scale.</p>

<h2>How to Choose the Right Plan</h2>

<p><strong>Match the plan to your actual workflow, not aspirational features. A solo operator running Core at $49/month is smarter than a solo operator on Grow at $299/month using 15% of what they're paying for. The main decision points are: how many people need logins, whether QuickBooks integration is immediate or can wait, and whether automated follow-up tools will realistically get used by someone in your operation.</strong></p>

<p>If you're running solo or with one helper and the biggest pain point is chasing unpaid invoices and forgetting to follow up on open quotes, Core at $49/month is the right starting point. Run it for 60 days. If you hit the user cap or want dispatch visibility, move to Connect then.</p>

<p>If you're running 2-5 technicians and dispatch is a real role in your business, start on Connect at $149/month. Don't start on Core with a plan to upgrade. Connect is where Jobber's dispatch and communication tools actually work the way the software is designed to function.</p>

<p>If you're running 6+ technicians, losing jobs to poor quote follow-up, and have someone whose job it is to work the sales pipeline, Grow at $299/month is justifiable. If that's not your situation yet, it isn't. The features will sit unused and you'll resent the bill every month.</p>

<h2>Our Recommendation</h2>

<p><strong>Jobber Connect at $149/month on annual billing is the right call for most small service businesses in 2026. It covers dispatch, GPS tracking, QuickBooks sync, and two-way customer communication for up to 5 users. Solo operators should start at Core ($49/month). If Housecall Pro's lower entry cost for QuickBooks integration matters to your setup, it's a genuine alternative worth testing side-by-side before committing.</strong></p>

<p>Jobber's strength is reliability and polish. The mobile app doesn't crash mid-job. The customer-facing experience looks professional. Onboarding a new technician takes an afternoon, not a week of training. For a service business where the owner is the one in the field and not behind a desk managing software all day, that matters more than a feature-count comparison.</p>

<p>The price is real. $149/month is not cheap for a 2-person operation billing $15,000 a month. But think about what that software has to replace: dispatch coordination, invoice chasing, customer reminders, QuickBooks data entry, GPS check-ins, and online booking. Building even two of those workflows manually costs more than $149/month in wasted hours. One missed appointment because of a dispatch error costs more than a month of Jobber.</p>

<p>If the monthly cost is genuinely the deciding factor after reading all this, Workiz at $95/month for the standard tier covers the essentials without embarrassing you in front of customers. The experience isn't as polished, but it works. That's an honest alternative, not a consolation prize.</p>
<h2>Frequently Asked Questions</h2>

<h3>Q: How much extra do you pay annually if you choose month-to-month billing instead of annual billing with Jobber?</h3>
<p>Month-to-month billing costs 30-50% more per tier; for example, the Connect plan jumps from $149/month on annual billing to $199/month on monthly billing, adding $600 per year. Even the budget Lite plan ($19 annual to $29 monthly) costs an extra $120 annually for the flexibility of canceling anytime.</p>

<h3>Q: At what Jobber pricing tier does GPS tracking and QuickBooks integration become available?</h3>
<p>Both GPS tracking and QuickBooks sync launch at the Connect tier ($149/month annual), making it the first plan where dispatch visibility and accounting integration are included. The lower Lite ($19/month) and Core ($49/month) plans lack these critical features, which most field service crews need within their first 90 days of operation.</p>

<h3>Q: What costs beyond the monthly Jobber subscription should you budget for?</h3>
<p>Payment processing fees on customer invoices stack on top of your monthly subscription and vary by plan tier, plus potential overages if you exceed your plan's user limit. QuickBooks integration quality also differs by tier—Lite and Core offer limited accounting sync, while Connect and Grow provide full two-way integration that eliminates manual data entry.</p>

<h3>Q: Which Jobber plan should you upgrade to when your crew grows from 4 to 8 technicians?</h3>
<p>Move from Connect ($149/month annual) to Grow ($299/month annual) when scaling to 8+ technicians, as Grow includes automated follow-ups and advanced lead management built for teams of 6-15. The additional $150/month investment provides workflow automation that saves time on larger crews better than paying extra user fees on a lower tier.</p>
