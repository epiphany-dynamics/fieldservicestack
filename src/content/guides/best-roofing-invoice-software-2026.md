<p><strong>The best roofing invoice software in 2026 depends on crew size. Jobber ($49-$249/month) is the strongest all-around pick for small to mid-sized operations. Housecall Pro ($49-$299/month) wins on mobile ease. ServiceTitan ($398+/month) makes sense once you're past $2M in annual revenue. Solo roofers should look at FieldPulse ($79/month) first.</strong></p>

<h2>What to Look For in Roofing Invoice Software</h2>

<p><strong>Roofing invoicing has specific requirements other trades don't. You need built-in photo documentation, clean material line items, and progress billing covering deposit, draw, and final payment. Your mobile app must work reliably on a job site with spotty signal. QuickBooks sync is non-negotiable for most shops, and e-signature on estimates cuts approval callbacks significantly.</strong></p>

<p>The job cycle in roofing is different from a service call trade. You're not showing up, fixing something in 90 minutes, and collecting $200. You're running a 3-day job with a materials deposit upfront, a draw when the tear-off is done, and a final invoice once the cleanup crew is gone. Any platform that can't handle progress billing is going to create real headaches the moment your projects grow past a simple 1-day repair job.</p>

<p>Photo documentation is also a bigger deal in roofing than most contractors realize until something goes wrong. Before photos protect you in disputes. Damage documentation is the whole job on insurance restoration work. Sending a final invoice that includes photos of the completed project looks professional and cuts down on the "did they actually do the work?" calls from homeowners.</p>

<p>Beyond those roofing-specific needs, the usual checklist applies: scheduling that doesn't require a whiteboard and a prayer, a mobile app your crew will actually open, and clean QuickBooks sync. If you're still sorting out whether to go with a full field service platform versus standalone invoicing, this <a href="/guides/invoice-software-for-construction">breakdown of invoice software for construction businesses</a> covers the tradeoffs worth understanding before you commit.</p>

<h2>Top 5 Roofing Invoice Software Picks for 2026</h2>

<p><strong>Jobber leads the field for 2-10 person operations, with the most complete roofing workflow from estimate to final invoice. Housecall Pro edges it out on mobile simplicity. Workiz is the budget-conscious alternative with built-in call tracking. ServiceTitan is overkill below 10 trucks. FieldPulse is the right starting point for solo operators who want to keep things simple.</strong></p>

<h3>1. Jobber — Best Overall for Small to Mid-Sized Crews</h3>

<p>Jobber is what most roofing contractors land on once they've outgrown paper invoices and Google Sheets. The workflow makes sense: you build a quote, the customer approves it (e-signature included), you schedule the job, crew checks in via mobile, and you convert to an invoice when work is done. That whole loop works cleanly without a lot of manual steps in between.</p>

<p>Pricing is straightforward. Core runs $49/month for 1 user and covers basic invoicing and scheduling. Connect at $149/month handles up to 7 users and is where most 2-4 truck operations land, since it adds the client hub, automated payment reminders, and two-way QuickBooks sync. Grow at $249/month handles up to 30 users and adds reporting that's actually useful when you need to see where your jobs are losing margin.</p>

<p>The mobile app is solid on both iOS and Android. It loads fast, works offline, and photo attachments to jobs are straightforward. The one genuine gap: there's no built-in material cost database. You can add line items manually, but if you want to pull from a saved materials list and track actual costs against estimated costs, you're doing that work yourself. For a full breakdown of what each tier actually includes day-to-day, the <a href="/reviews/jobber-field-service-management-company-evaluation-review-2026">Jobber field service management review</a> goes into the detail that the pricing page doesn't.</p>

<ul>
  <li>Starting price: $49/month (Core, 1 user)</li>
  <li>Best plan for roofing: Connect at $149/month</li>
  <li>QuickBooks integration: Yes, native two-way sync</li>
  <li>Progress billing: Yes, on Connect and above</li>
  <li>Mobile app: iOS and Android, works offline</li>
  <li>Weakness: No built-in material cost database</li>
</ul>

<h3>2. Housecall Pro — Best Mobile Experience</h3>

<p>If your guys are not tech-savvy and you need them actually using the app instead of ignoring it, Housecall Pro deserves a serious look. The mobile interface is genuinely easier to navigate than Jobber's, and that matters when you're asking a 50-year-old roofer to start logging job notes on his phone for the first time. Platform adoption is the whole game if the crew won't open the app.</p>

<p>Basic starts at $49/month for 1 user and covers invoicing, scheduling, and card payments. Essentials at $129/month (up to 5 users) adds estimates, automated customer follow-ups, and the customer portal. MAX at $299/month removes user limits and unlocks advanced reporting. QuickBooks sync is available on all paid tiers.</p>

<p>The customer-facing experience is polished without you doing any design work. The booking widget, appointment reminders, and invoice emails all look professional. The reporting on Basic is thin, though. And if you need job costing broken down by material versus labor, you'll be pulling that math somewhere else. Before signing up, the <a href="/reviews/housecall-pro-pricing-2026">Housecall Pro pricing breakdown</a> lays out exactly what each tier includes and where the walls are.</p>

<ul>
  <li>Starting price: $49/month (Basic, 1 user)</li>
  <li>Best plan for roofing: Essentials at $129/month</li>
  <li>QuickBooks integration: Yes, all paid plans</li>
  <li>Progress billing: Yes</li>
  <li>Mobile app: Best ease-of-use in this category</li>
  <li>Weakness: Reporting is limited on lower tiers</li>
</ul>

<h3>3. Workiz — Best Budget Option for Growing Crews</h3>

<p>Workiz doesn't get talked about as much as the top two, but it's worth looking at if you're price-sensitive and don't need every feature in the catalog. Starter runs $65/month, Team is $149/month, Business is $225/month. You get invoicing, scheduling, estimates, and what I think is an underrated differentiator: built-in phone call tracking.</p>

<p>The call tracking matters more than it sounds. If you're running Google Local Service Ads or Angi leads and want to know which calls actually turned into booked jobs, Workiz captures that automatically. For a roofing operation actively marketing, that data is useful and it's not something Jobber or Housecall Pro handle well at the same price.</p>

<p>The platform is less polished overall and the integration ecosystem is smaller. QuickBooks sync works fine. The mobile app is functional but not something your crew will brag about. If you're weighing this against other budget-tier options, it holds up on a feature-by-feature comparison for straightforward residential work.</p>

<ul>
  <li>Starting price: $65/month (Starter)</li>
  <li>Best plan for roofing: Team at $149/month</li>
  <li>QuickBooks integration: Yes</li>
  <li>Progress billing: Yes</li>
  <li>Mobile app: Functional, less polished than top two</li>
  <li>Unique feature: Built-in call tracking</li>
</ul>

<h3>4. ServiceTitan — Best for Large Operations</h3>

<p>ServiceTitan is the platform that comes up in every conversation about field service software once an operation gets big enough. It's genuinely powerful: job costing, material management, fleet tracking, custom reporting, a full customer communication suite. If you're running 10+ trucks and billing $3M a year, ServiceTitan can handle complexity that will break the smaller platforms.</p>

<p>Pricing starts around $398/month and goes up based on features and user count. Custom quotes are standard above the entry tier. The onboarding takes months and requires a real internal commitment from whoever is managing the implementation. This is not something you set up over a weekend and start using Monday morning.</p>

<p>For smaller operations, the cost is genuinely painful and the complexity is real overkill. The question of whether you've actually reached the point where it pays for itself is worth thinking through carefully. The <a href="/comparisons/servicetitan-vs-jobber-2026">ServiceTitan vs Jobber comparison for 2026</a> breaks down where that crossover actually happens, which is more useful than guessing at a revenue threshold.</p>

<ul>
  <li>Starting price: ~$398/month (custom above that)</li>
  <li>QuickBooks integration: Yes, plus built-in accounting tools</li>
  <li>Progress billing: Yes, full support</li>
  <li>Mobile app: Excellent</li>
  <li>Best for: 10+ trucks, $2M+ annual revenue</li>
  <li>Weakness: Cost and onboarding timeline</li>
</ul>

<h3>5. FieldPulse — Best for Solo Operators</h3>

<p>FieldPulse is the right answer for a solo roofer or a 2-person operation that needs professional invoicing without paying for features they'll never use. Solo starts at $79/month and team pricing scales from there. Setup takes a few hours, not a few weeks, and the interface is clean enough that you won't spend the first month trying to find basic functions.</p>

<p>You get quotes, invoices, job scheduling, customer management, and QuickBooks integration. Photo attachments work fine. The mobile app is lightweight in a good way. There's no call tracking, no GPS fleet management, no advanced job costing. For a one-truck operation, that's fine, because you don't need any of that yet.</p>

<p>The ecosystem is smaller than Jobber's, and above 5 employees you'll start feeling the limits. Getting started on FieldPulse and switching to Jobber when you outgrow it is a legitimate path. The <a href="/comparisons/jobber-vs-fieldpulse-2026">Jobber vs FieldPulse comparison</a> maps out exactly where FieldPulse starts falling short so you're not surprised by it mid-growth.</p>

<ul>
  <li>Starting price: $79/month (Solo)</li>
  <li>QuickBooks integration: Yes</li>
  <li>Progress billing: Yes</li>
  <li>Mobile app: Lightweight, easy to navigate</li>
  <li>Best for: 1-2 person operations</li>
  <li>Weakness: Limited scalability above 5 users</li>
</ul>

<h2>How to Choose the Right Roofing Invoice Software</h2>

<p><strong>Match your crew size to the platform. Solo operator, start with FieldPulse or Housecall Pro Basic. Running 2-5 trucks, Jobber Connect is the right fit. Above 8 trucks and growing fast, take a real look at ServiceTitan even though the cost is painful. Switching platforms later with all your historical data intact is far more disruptive than paying a bit more now.</strong></p>

<p>The most common mistake roofing contractors make when picking software is buying for where they are right now instead of where they'll be in 18 months. If you're at 3 trucks with a real plan to hit 8 trucks by next summer, starting on Housecall Pro Basic to save $80/month is going to cost you a painful data migration in about a year. Think about that before optimizing on the entry price.</p>

<p>If you're doing a lot of insurance restoration work, that changes the calculation. Insurance jobs have specific documentation requirements: damage photos, scope of work broken down by line item, supplement tracking if the insurance scope gets revised. Jobber and ServiceTitan handle this better than Workiz or FieldPulse, which are designed more for straightforward residential jobs where the scope is agreed upfront and doesn't change.</p>

<p>Think about who is actually going to use this day-to-day. If you're the owner and the only person logging invoices, ease of use matters mostly for you. But if you have 4 guys in the field who need to attach photos and mark jobs complete from their phones, the mobile experience is the whole game. Housecall Pro consistently wins that evaluation because the field-facing interface was clearly built with non-technical users in mind, not just the office side.</p>

<p>One more thing worth considering: whether the software handles the estimate approval step cleanly. A lot of roofing contractors are still emailing PDFs and chasing down verbal approvals. Any platform on this list supports e-signature on estimates, and once you see how many fewer callbacks you're making to confirm a customer actually said yes, you won't go back to the old way.</p>

<h2>Our Recommendation</h2>

<p><strong>For most roofing businesses in 2026, Jobber Connect at $149/month is the right call. It covers the full job cycle, syncs with QuickBooks, handles progress billing, and the mobile app holds up in the field. Solo operators should start with Housecall Pro Basic at $49/month and upgrade only when they genuinely need more.</strong></p>

<p>Jobber is not perfect. The material cost tracking is underdeveloped, and on jobs with significant supply costs you'll feel that gap. But the overall workflow for a residential roofing operation is as close to purpose-built as any general field service platform gets without going to a niche roofing-specific tool. The quote-to-invoice pipeline works, the automated payment reminders work, and the QuickBooks sync doesn't break regularly.</p>

<p>If team adoption is your primary concern and you're worried about guys in the field actually opening the app, flip the ranking. Housecall Pro is easier to get non-technical crew members using consistently, and a platform that gets used is worth more than a platform with better features that nobody opens.</p>

<p>ServiceTitan is the long-term answer for operations that have scaled past the point where Jobber handles the complexity. But get there first, then switch. Paying $400/month to run a 2-truck operation because you're planning for someday doesn't make financial sense. Start where you are, pick the right tool for that size, and upgrade when the job volume actually demands it.</p>
