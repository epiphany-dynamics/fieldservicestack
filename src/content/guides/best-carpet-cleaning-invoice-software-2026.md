<p><strong>The best carpet cleaning invoice software in 2026 is Jobber at $49-$349/month for teams that need recurring client management and QuickBooks sync, or Housecall Pro at $69-$299/month for operations that prioritize in-field payment collection. Solo operators doing fewer than 50 jobs a month should look at ServiceM8's usage-based pricing starting at $9/month first.</strong></p>

<h2>What to Look For in Carpet Cleaning Invoice Software</h2>

<p><strong>Carpet cleaning has specific billing requirements that generic invoice tools miss entirely: before/after photo documentation, per-room or per-square-foot job costing, recurring client reminders on 6-12 month cycles, and on-site payment collection before you pack up the truck. Software that skips those four things creates more admin work than it saves.</strong></p>

<p>The generic "send an invoice and hope they pay" approach does not work well in this trade. Carpet cleaning customers go cold fast. If you're pulling away from the job without collecting payment or a card on file, you're going to spend time chasing invoices you didn't need to chase.</p>

<p>Here's what actually matters when you're evaluating carpet cleaning invoice software:</p>

<ul>
<li><strong>Photo documentation</strong> built into the job record, not bolted on as an afterthought. When a customer says you made a stain worse, you need timestamped photos tied to the invoice.</li>
<li>Per-room or area-based pricing templates so techs aren't manually calculating every job from scratch on the customer's porch.</li>
<li>Recurring job scheduling with automated reminders. A customer who gets a "time for your annual carpet cleaning" text in October will book again. One who doesn't hear from you probably won't.</li>
<li>Mobile invoicing that works offline. Not every house in your service area has reliable cell signal inside.</li>
<li>Credit card processing with transparent fees. The difference between 2.9% and 3.5% sounds small until you're processing $15,000 a month.</li>
<li>QuickBooks or Xero sync that actually works. If your accountant uses one of these, the data should flow automatically without manual exports.</li>
<li>Online booking, if you're doing any digital marketing. Customers who find you at 10pm want to book at 10pm.</li>
</ul>

<p>For carpet cleaners also running upholstery, tile, or area rug services, you'll need clean line-item customization on invoices. Some of the cheaper tools handle multi-service jobs poorly. If you're running commercial accounts alongside residential, the billing differences are significant enough that it's worth checking the <a href="/guides/best-commercial-cleaning-invoice-software-2026">best commercial cleaning invoice software guide</a> before settling on a tool built around residential workflows.</p>

<h2>The Top 5 Carpet Cleaning Invoice Software Options in 2026</h2>

<p><strong>Jobber is the most complete option for carpet cleaning businesses with two or more technicians, Housecall Pro is better for solo and small crews who want cleaner payment collection at the door, and ServiceM8 is the right call for solo operators watching costs closely. Workiz and FieldPulse fill the gap for mid-size operations that have outgrown the basics.</strong></p>

<h3>1. Jobber: Best Overall for Carpet Cleaning Teams</h3>

<p>Jobber is the tool I'd put in front of most carpet cleaning business owners first. The Core plan at $49/month covers a solo operator well: professional invoices, client records, basic scheduling, and QuickBooks Online sync. Move up to Connect at $149/month (up to 5 users) and you get online booking, automated follow-up messages, and two-way texting with clients.</p>

<p>The recurring job feature works well for carpet cleaners specifically. You set a client to recur every 6 months or annually, and Jobber surfaces that reminder automatically. Pair that with the automated "how'd we do" review request that fires after an invoice is paid, and you have a lightweight retention system built in without any extra setup.</p>

<p>The mobile app is solid on both iOS and Android. Technicians can create invoices, attach before/after photos, collect payment via card reader, and get a signature on a tablet all from the job site. The photo capture isn't as polished as a dedicated tool, but it covers you when a customer disputes a pre-existing stain.</p>

<p>The Grow plan at $349/month adds job costing, custom reporting, and support for up to 15 users. For most carpet cleaning operations under 10 trucks, Connect is the right plan. See the full tier breakdown in this <a href="/guides/jobber-pricing-per-month-2026">Jobber pricing breakdown</a> if you want to map features against your team size before committing.</p>

<p>QuickBooks integration is one of the better ones in this category. Jobs sync automatically, income categories map cleanly, and sales tax handles correctly in most states. Where Jobber falls short: GPS fleet tracking is limited, and if you're running more than 8-10 trucks you'll start feeling the ceiling on dispatch visibility.</p>

<h3>2. Housecall Pro: Best for In-Field Payment Collection</h3>

<p>Housecall Pro built its product around getting paid at the door, and it shows. The in-app card reader integration, instant payout options, and autopay on recurring jobs are smoother here than in Jobber. If your average ticket runs $200-$400 and you want money collected before you roll up the hose, Housecall Pro's payment workflow is genuinely better.</p>

<p>The Basic plan starts at $69/month for one user. Essentials runs $169/month for a small team and adds price book templates, a customer portal, and automatic review requests. The Pro tier at $299/month layers in real-time GPS tracking and more advanced reporting.</p>

<p>Photo documentation is stronger out of the box than Jobber. You take before/after photos directly in the app, annotate them, and they attach to both the job record and the invoice automatically. For carpet cleaning, that's not a nice-to-have feature, it's protection against disputes.</p>

<p>The main issue with Housecall Pro is the QuickBooks sync. It works, but it has created duplicate entries for some users, and the account mapping setup isn't as intuitive as Jobber's. If your bookkeeper lives in QuickBooks, that's worth factoring in. Read the full <a href="/reviews/housecall-pro-review-2026">Housecall Pro review</a> for a longer look at where it holds up and where it doesn't.</p>

<h3>3. Workiz: Best for Multi-Truck Operations</h3>

<p>Workiz is where I'd point a carpet cleaning company running 4 or more trucks with a dispatcher working the phones. The dispatching board, real-time GPS tracking, and technician performance reporting are ahead of both Jobber and Housecall Pro at comparable price points.</p>

<p>Pricing starts around $65/month for a single user on the Standard plan, but the value becomes clear on team plans where you're coordinating multiple crews across a service area. The lead tracking and source attribution features help if you're running Google Ads or Yelp campaigns and need to know which channel is actually producing booked jobs.</p>

<p>The invoice side is fully functional: custom templates, line items, photo attachments, signatures, and online payment links. It's not flashy, but it works. The mobile app is well-reviewed and handles the connectivity issues you run into in residential neighborhoods with poor indoor signal.</p>

<p>For context on how Workiz fits alongside some of the smaller-scale tools, the <a href="/guides/best-field-service-software-small-cleaning-businesses">best field service software for small cleaning businesses</a> guide covers several of these options with different use cases mapped out.</p>

<h3>4. FieldPulse: Best Value for Growing Crews</h3>

<p>FieldPulse is one of the more underrated options in this category. At $99/month for a single user, it covers invoicing, estimates, scheduling, GPS, photo attachments, QuickBooks sync, and a client portal. That's a lot of coverage per dollar compared to what Jobber and Housecall Pro charge for equivalent features.</p>

<p>The reason it sits at number four and not higher is the mobile app. It works, but the offline mode has limitations and the UI isn't as polished as the top two. Techs who are comfortable with smartphones adapt fine. If you've got someone on your crew who avoids anything new on their phone, expect some friction in the first few weeks.</p>

<p>The invoicing templates are customizable and the estimate-to-invoice workflow is fast. The price book feature lets you pre-load room sizes and service types so techs aren't manually entering prices at the customer's door. That alone saves time on every job.</p>

<h3>5. ServiceM8: Best for Solo Operators on a Budget</h3>

<p>ServiceM8 takes a completely different approach to pricing than everyone else on this list. Instead of a flat monthly fee, you pay based on job volume. The Starter plan runs $9/month and covers 15 jobs. The Growing plan is $29/month for 50 jobs. Unlimited jobs runs $99/month.</p>

<p>For a solo carpet cleaner doing 3-4 jobs a day, 5 days a week, you're running roughly 60-80 jobs a month. That puts you at the $29-$99/month range depending on volume, which is still cheaper than most competitors at that job count and output level.</p>

<p>ServiceM8's invoice quality is high. Templates are professional, sales tax handles correctly, and the client-facing experience looks clean on any device. The catch: the app runs on iOS only, no Android. That's a hard stop for a large chunk of operators, so check your phone before you get too far into a trial.</p>

<p>If you're a solo operator who isn't ready to spend $100 or more per month on software, ServiceM8 gives you professional invoicing at a price that actually makes sense at lower job volume.</p>

<h2>How to Choose Based on Your Setup</h2>

<p><strong>The right carpet cleaning invoice software depends almost entirely on team size and how you currently collect payment. A solo operator doing residential work has different needs than a 5-truck crew with a dispatcher. Most carpet cleaning businesses fall into one of three situations, and the right pick shifts depending on which one applies to you.</strong></p>

<h3>Solo Operator, Under 60 Jobs a Month</h3>

<p>Start with ServiceM8 if you're on an iPhone and your job volume sits under 50 per month. The $29/month Growing plan covers your output and the invoicing quality is legitimately good. If you or your techs are on Android, skip ServiceM8 and go straight to Jobber Core at $49/month. The recurring client management alone will pay for itself within the first quarter if you're doing any repeat residential work.</p>

<h3>Small Team, 2-5 Technicians</h3>

<p>This is where Jobber Connect at $149/month and Housecall Pro Essentials at $169/month go head to head. If getting paid at the door is your primary frustration right now, Housecall Pro's payment workflow is the better pick. If chasing recurring clients and syncing cleanly with QuickBooks is the bigger pain point, go Jobber. Both are solid products at this team size, and the $20/month difference isn't really the deciding factor either way.</p>

<p>If you're on the fence about whether Jobber is worth the monthly cost at your current revenue level, there's a straightforward answer in this post on <a href="/reviews/is-jobber-worth-it-home-service-business-2026">whether Jobber is worth it for home service businesses</a>.</p>

<h3>Growing Operation, 5 or More Trucks</h3>

<p>At this size you need real dispatch visibility, GPS tracking, and the ability to see how individual techs are performing. Workiz and the upper tiers of both Housecall Pro and Jobber handle this. The differentiator at this scale is usually how well the software matches your dispatch style. If you're running tight geographic routes with a lot of same-day scheduling, Workiz's dispatching board has a clear edge over the alternatives.</p>

<h2>Our Recommendation</h2>

<p><strong>For most carpet cleaning businesses in 2026, start with Jobber. The Core plan at $49/month handles solo work cleanly and the Connect plan scales to small teams without getting complicated. If in-field payment collection is your top priority and QuickBooks sync is secondary, Housecall Pro edges ahead. ServiceM8 is the right pick for iOS-only solos keeping a close eye on monthly costs.</strong></p>

<p>What pushes Jobber to the top of this list isn't one feature. It's the combination of a reliable mobile app, QuickBooks integration that actually works month to month, and recurring job management that keeps your customer base warm without manual effort. Carpet cleaning is a relationship business. You want software that stays in front of customers automatically, not software you have to babysit.</p>

<p>Whatever you pick, actually run a few test jobs through it before committing to an annual plan. Most of these tools offer 14-day free trials. Use them on real jobs with real customers and see how the software holds up when your tech is standing in someone's living room trying to send an invoice with one bar of signal on a Thursday afternoon.</p>
