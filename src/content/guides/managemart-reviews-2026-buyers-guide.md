<p><strong>ManageMart is a cloud-based field service management platform with plans starting at $15.99/month. It handles scheduling, invoicing, client management, and basic GPS tracking well enough for solo operators and crews under 5 techs. Where it falls short is QuickBooks integration depth and reporting, which are noticeably thinner than what you get from Jobber at $49/month or Housecall Pro at $79/month.</strong></p>

<h2>What Is ManageMart?</h2>

<p><strong>ManageMart is a cloud-based FSM platform aimed at small service businesses. It covers job scheduling, client records, estimates, invoicing, online payments, and GPS tracking on higher-tier plans. Pricing starts at $15.99/month, which puts it among the more affordable paid options in the field service software market in 2026.</strong></p>

<p>If you're running a solo landscaping or cleaning operation and the dispatch nightmare you're dealing with is mostly in your own head (forgetting which client is where on Tuesday), ManageMart can fix that problem for a pretty small monthly fee. It's not trying to be ServiceTitan. It's not even trying to be Jobber. It's trying to get your jobs organized and your invoices out the door, and for a lot of small operators, that's enough.</p>

<p>The platform has been around since the early 2010s and serves contractors, cleaners, landscapers, pest control companies, and similar small service businesses. The UI is clean and straightforward, which is either a positive or a warning sign depending on how complex your operation is.</p>

<h2>What to Look For Before You Buy</h2>

<p><strong>Before picking any FSM software, check four things: scheduling flexibility, invoicing speed, accounting integration quality, and mobile app reliability in the field. Scheduling should let you drag and drop without three confirmation screens. Invoicing should take under 60 seconds to send from your phone. QuickBooks sync should move actual job data, not just payment totals.</strong></p>

<p>Mobile app quality matters more than most software reviews admit. If the app crashes on a 4G connection while you're trying to close out a job at 5:30 PM, that's not a minor inconvenience. That's you sitting in a customer's driveway doing it manually and then double-entering it later. Test the mobile app before you commit to any platform.</p>

<p>For a broader look at how different platforms handle these basics across trades, the <a href="/guides/field-service-management-buyers-guide">Field Service Management Software Buyer's Guide</a> breaks down what separates decent software from software that techs will actually use day to day.</p>

<p>Accounting integration is another one people overlook until it's too late. "QuickBooks integration" on a marketing page can mean anything from full two-way sync of customers, jobs, invoices, and payments to "we export a CSV you can import manually." Find out exactly which one you're getting before you sign the contract.</p>

<h2>ManageMart Reviewed: Features and Pricing</h2>

<p><strong>ManageMart's free plan works for 1 user with basic features. Paid plans run $15.99/month (Starter), $49.99/month (Professional), and $99.99/month (Enterprise). Core scheduling and invoicing work well. Reporting is thin on all but the top tier. QuickBooks integration exists but is more basic than competitors at similar or higher price points.</strong></p>

<h3>Scheduling and Dispatch</h3>

<p>The scheduling interface is clean. You get a calendar view, you can assign jobs to techs, and recurring jobs work without much setup. If you're running a 3-truck lawn care crew with fixed weekly routes, this handles it without friction. Where it gets awkward is real-time dispatch changes. Moving jobs around mid-day is clunky compared to platforms like Jobber or Workiz that have drag-and-drop dispatch boards built for exactly that situation.</p>

<h3>Invoicing and Payments</h3>

<p>Invoicing is one of ManageMart's stronger areas. You can generate an invoice from a completed job, send it via email with a payment link, and collect via credit card or ACH. For a solo plumber doing 8-10 calls a day, this is functional. The invoice templates are basic but professional-looking. Online payment processing runs through Stripe on paid plans.</p>

<h3>Mobile App</h3>

<p>The mobile app (iOS and Android) covers the basics: view schedule, update job status, add notes, generate invoices. It's not the slickest app in this space, but it works. Offline mode handles reading data reasonably well, though syncing when you're back online can lag occasionally. For a solo tech or small crew, it's usable. For a 10-truck operation where dispatch accuracy is everything, you'll find it limiting.</p>

<h3>QuickBooks Integration</h3>

<p>This is where ManageMart shows its budget roots. The QuickBooks integration syncs invoices and payments, which covers the basic accounting need. But it doesn't sync customers bidirectionally as cleanly as Jobber does, and it lacks the job cost tracking that platforms like FieldPulse or Housecall Pro have built out. If your bookkeeper relies on job-level data in QuickBooks, expect to do some manual cleanup each month.</p>

<h3>Pricing Breakdown</h3>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Free:</strong> 1 user, basic scheduling and invoicing, limited client records</li>
  <li><strong>Starter ($15.99/month):</strong> Up to 3 users, full invoicing, estimates, client management</li>
  <li><strong>Professional ($49.99/month):</strong> Up to 10 users, GPS tracking, recurring jobs, reporting</li>
  <li><strong>Enterprise ($99.99/month):</strong> Unlimited users, priority support, advanced reporting</li>
</ul>

<p>The free plan is worth testing to see if the interface works for your operation, but you'll hit its limits quickly. The Starter plan at $15.99/month is where most solo operators land and where ManageMart is most competitive against the field.</p>

<h2>Top ManageMart Alternatives to Consider</h2>

<p><strong>The main ManageMart alternatives worth considering in 2026 are Jobber ($49/month), Housecall Pro ($79/month), FieldPulse ($99/month flat for unlimited users), and Workiz ($65/month). Each costs more than ManageMart's Starter plan, but each delivers meaningfully more on specific dimensions like dispatch infrastructure, reporting, or customer communication tools.</strong></p>

<h3>Jobber: Best Overall for Growing Crews</h3>

<p>Jobber is the default recommendation for a reason. The dispatch board is one of the best in the market, the QuickBooks sync actually works both ways, and the client hub feature (where customers can view their job history and pay invoices online) cuts down the "did you get my invoice?" calls. The <a href="/reviews/jobber-field-service-management-company-evaluation-review-2026">Jobber Field Service Management Review</a> goes deeper on where it earns its price. Core plan starts at $49/month for 1 user, which is steep compared to ManageMart, but you get substantially more dispatch infrastructure for it.</p>

<h3>Housecall Pro: Best for Customer-Facing Features</h3>

<p>If you're in a market where customers expect online booking, automated appointment reminders, and a professional post-job follow-up sequence, Housecall Pro earns the higher price. Their Basic plan runs $79/month. The <a href="/reviews/housecall-pro-review-2026">Housecall Pro Review</a> breaks down the full feature set. The trade-off is that several advanced features are gated behind the Essentials plan at $189/month, so verify which tier you actually need before you start the trial.</p>

<h3>FieldPulse: Best Flat-Rate Option for Teams</h3>

<p>FieldPulse charges around $99/month and gives you unlimited users, which becomes a deal once you hit 4 or more techs. The reporting is better than ManageMart at every tier, and the job costing features are genuinely useful for contractors who want to know if a particular job type is actually profitable. The <a href="/guides/fieldpulse-reviews-2026-buyers-guide">FieldPulse Reviews guide</a> covers the full pricing breakdown and what you get at each tier.</p>

<h3>Workiz: Best for Dispatch-Heavy Operations</h3>

<p>Workiz is built for operations where dispatch is the daily headache. If you're running 5 or more techs and the problem you're solving is "who's nearest, who's available, who do I send to this emergency call," Workiz handles that better than ManageMart at any tier. Plans start around $65/month. The VoIP integration (calls logged directly to customer records) is a feature you won't find in ManageMart at all.</p>

FieldPulse wins on per-user value for teams. Workiz wins on dispatch. ManageMart's advantage is cost, and that's a real advantage for solo operators who don't need the rest.</strong></p>

<h2>How to Choose the Right Software for Your Operation</h2>

<p><strong>Match the software to your current operation, not the size you hope to be in two years. ManageMart fits solo operators and 2-3 tech crews with simple recurring jobs and a tight budget. Step up to Jobber or FieldPulse when you hit 4 techs or need real reporting. Choose Housecall Pro if customer communication tools are a core part of your service model.</strong></p>

<p>If you're a solo HVAC tech doing 6-8 calls a day, ManageMart's Starter plan at $15.99/month handles your scheduling, invoicing, and client history. You don't need a full dispatch board. You don't need two-way QuickBooks sync yet. You need to stop losing track of who paid and who didn't, and ManageMart solves that problem.</p>

<p>If you're a 5-truck pest control company, ManageMart's ceiling will hit you within a few months. The reporting won't tell you what you need to know about technician productivity or job margins, and the dispatch tools won't keep up with how fast your schedule changes throughout the day. At that point, the pricing difference between ManageMart and Jobber stops mattering much.</p>

<p>Cleaning businesses are a case where invoicing frequency and recurring billing features matter a lot. ManageMart handles recurring jobs on the Starter plan, which is a genuine plus. If you also need automated late-payment follow-ups and client communication sequences, that's where you'll need to step up to Housecall Pro or Jobber.</p>

<h2>Our Recommendation</h2>

<p><strong>ManageMart is worth trying if you're a solo operator or running a 2-3 tech crew with straightforward jobs and a budget under $20/month. Start on the free plan to test the interface, then move to Starter if it fits. If you hit the reporting or dispatch ceiling within 90 days, move to Jobber. ManageMart is a reasonable first step, not a long-term home for most growing businesses.</strong></p>

<p>The honest take: ManageMart does what it says on the label for the operators it's built for. A solo landscaper or cleaning company owner who just needs jobs organized and invoices out the door will find it more than adequate at $15.99/month. The free plan is genuinely useful for testing, not just a lead magnet with nothing behind it.</p>

<p>Where you'll outgrow it is reporting. Once you want to know which job types are most profitable, which techs are closing the most work, or where you're losing margin, ManageMart won't give you those answers cleanly. That's the ceiling. Know it going in and you won't be disappointed.</p>

<p>If you're already leaning toward Jobber or Housecall Pro, both offer free trials and the step up in price buys a meaningful step up in capability. The <a href="/guides/what-is-better-jobber-or-housecall-pro-2026">Jobber vs. Housecall Pro breakdown</a> is worth reading before you decide between the two, especially if your operation sits right in the range where either could work.</p>
<h2>Frequently Asked Questions</h2>
<h3>Q: How much does ManageMart actually cost compared to Jobber?</h3>
<p>ManageMart's base plan is $15.99/month versus Jobber's $49/month—a $33 monthly difference that appeals to solo operators and small crews. But Jobber includes stronger reporting and QuickBooks integration, so the lower cost only wins if you're not heavily dependent on those features.</p>
<h3>Q: Does ManageMart integrate with QuickBooks like other field service software?</h3>
<p>ManageMart syncs payments to QuickBooks but doesn't move detailed job data, while Jobber and Housecall Pro do. If you use QuickBooks for project profitability tracking, this gap is a real limitation in ManageMart.</p>
<h3>Q: At what team size does ManageMart become too limited?</h3>
<p>ManageMart is optimized for solo operators and crews under 5 technicians; beyond that, its thinner reporting and accounting features start creating friction. If you're hiring a second crew soon, starting with Jobber or Housecall Pro saves a migration later.</p>
<h3>Q: Is ManageMart's mobile app reliable for field teams on poor connections?</h3>
<p>ManageMart includes GPS tracking on higher plans and has a straightforward mobile interface, but testing it on a 4G connection before committing is essential since field app crashes matter more than lab performance.</p>
