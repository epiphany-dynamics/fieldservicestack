<p><strong>FieldPulse is a mid-tier field service management platform starting at $99/month for up to 3 users. It covers scheduling, dispatch, estimates, invoicing, and full customer history. Best fit for HVAC, plumbing, and electrical operations running 2-8 techs who need a real all-in-one system without paying ServiceTitan prices to get it.</strong></p>

<h2>What to Look For in Field Service Management Software</h2>

<p><strong>The non-negotiables when picking FSM software are mobile app quality, QuickBooks integration, dispatch speed, and honest pricing. Too many platforms bury per-user fees or charge extra for GPS tracking that should be standard. Know exactly what you're paying before the trial ends, not after you've onboarded your whole team.</strong></p>

<p>If you're researching FieldPulse, you're probably somewhere between "we're drowning in paper" and "we can't justify $400 a month yet." That's a real gap in the market, and it's exactly where FieldPulse positions itself.</p>

<p>The things that actually matter in day-to-day field work:</p>

<ul>
  <li>Your techs are never at a desk, so mobile app performance matters more than the web interface. A slow or crashy app in the field kills productivity faster than bad scheduling ever could.</li>
  <li>For a 3-truck HVAC crew, scheduling conflicts and phone tag cost real money every week. Drag-and-drop dispatch and live job status updates are table stakes, not premium features.</li>
  <li>Getting paid same-day, on-site is the goal. If your tech emails an invoice from the parking lot and waits a week to collect, that's a software problem dressed up as a cash flow problem.</li>
  <li>Most field service businesses live in QuickBooks. A platform that doesn't sync cleanly with QBO adds hours of manual reconciliation every month that nobody has time for.</li>
  <li>Know your total cost ceiling before signing. Per-user fees and "feature packs" can push a $99 base plan past $300 fast once your team grows.</li>
</ul>

<p>If you're earlier in your research, the <a href="/guides/field-service-management-buyers-guide">field service management software buyer's guide</a> covers the full decision framework across all trade types before you narrow down to specific platforms.</p>

<h2>FieldPulse: Full Breakdown for 2026</h2>

<p><strong>FieldPulse starts at $99/month for 3 users and includes scheduling, dispatch, estimates, invoicing, customer history, flat-rate pricing catalogs, GPS tracking, and QuickBooks Online sync. The mobile app works well in real field conditions. It's not the flashiest platform in this category, but it doesn't have major gaps either, which is more than you can say for everything at this price point.</strong></p>

<h3>Pricing</h3>

<p>The base plan at $99/month covers 3 users. Additional users run roughly $30-40 per month each depending on the tier you're on. For a 5-person operation, expect to land somewhere around $150-180/month total. That's competitive when you compare it directly against Housecall Pro or Jobber at the same team size. FieldPulse does offer a free trial, and it's worth actually using it with a real tech before committing.</p>

<h3>Core Features</h3>

<ul>
  <li>Drag-and-drop scheduling and dispatch board</li>
  <li>Customer and job history with full CRM-style records</li>
  <li>Estimates and invoices with digital signature collection</li>
  <li>Flat-rate and custom pricing catalogs</li>
  <li>Two-way QuickBooks Online sync</li>
  <li>iOS and Android mobile app</li>
  <li>GPS tracking included at base price (not an add-on)</li>
  <li>Customer portal with online booking capability</li>
  <li>Recurring service agreements</li>
  <li>In-app credit card and ACH payment processing</li>
</ul>

<h3>Mobile App</h3>

<p>The mobile app is where FieldPulse earns most of its reputation. For a tech doing 6-8 service calls a day, pulling up job details, checking customer history, updating job status, and collecting payment on-site matters more than any web dashboard feature. FieldPulse's app handles all of that without needing a strong connection, which is useful when you're in a basement, a crawlspace, or on a rural property with spotty signal.</p>

<p>There is a real learning curve. The interface is not as clean or intuitive as Jobber's app, and new techs typically need a week or two before they stop asking questions about basic navigation. Once it clicks, it moves fast. The point is: plan for onboarding time and don't assume everyone figures it out on day one.</p>

<h3>QuickBooks Integration</h3>

<p>Two-way sync with QuickBooks Online is included at the base price. Invoices created in FieldPulse push to QBO, payments sync back, and customer records stay consistent across both platforms. The sync isn't always instant, but it's reliable enough that you're not spending Friday afternoon doing manual reconciliation.</p>

<h3>Where FieldPulse Falls Short</h3>

<p>Reporting is the weakest area. If you want to track job profitability by tech, identify your most profitable service types, or run deeper business performance reports, the reporting suite will leave you wanting more. It's functional for the basics and not much beyond that.</p>

<p>The customer portal and online booking experience are usable but not polished. If building your online review count and booking repeat customers automatically is a big priority, Housecall Pro does that work better.</p>

<p>Customer support has gotten mixed reviews over the years. Some users report fast responses and helpful reps. Others have hit long waits during busy periods. It's not a dealbreaker, but it's worth knowing going in so you're not caught off guard when you actually need help.</p>

<h2>Top FieldPulse Alternatives</h2>

<p><strong>The four strongest alternatives are Jobber ($69-$349/month, best UX in the category), Housecall Pro ($79-$189+/month, best marketing and customer communication tools), Workiz ($198/month and up, built for high-volume dispatch), and Service Fusion ($195/month with unlimited users). Each one trades something for something else. Pick based on which pain point is actually costing your business money right now.</strong></p>

<h3>Jobber</h3>

<p>Jobber is the default recommendation for most small service businesses, and the reason is simple: the interface is the cleanest in the field, the mobile app performance is excellent, and the feature set covers everything a 1-10 tech operation needs. Pricing runs from $69/month (Core, 1 user) to $349/month (Grow, full team features with marketing automations included).</p>

<p>Where Jobber beats FieldPulse: overall UX, customer communication tools, and adoption speed for new techs. Where it falls short: at the higher tiers, the price adds up faster than FieldPulse, and some contractors find FieldPulse's flat-rate pricing catalog more flexible for their quoting workflow. The <a href="/comparisons/jobber-vs-fieldpulse-2026">Jobber vs FieldPulse 2026 comparison</a> goes deep on the feature-by-feature differences if you're deciding between these two specifically.</p>

<h3>Housecall Pro</h3>

<p>Housecall Pro's edge is its marketing and customer communication toolkit. Automated review requests, email campaigns, and a consumer-facing booking experience that actually looks professional. If you're building a residential customer base and competing on service reputation, HCP invests more in those features than FieldPulse does, and the gap is noticeable.</p>

<p>Pricing is $79/month for Basic (1 user) and $189/month for Essentials. Per-user fees kick in above certain limits on some plans, so a larger crew can see the total cost climb faster than the base price suggests. The <a href="/reviews/housecall-pro-review-2026">Housecall Pro 2026 review</a> breaks down what you actually get at each tier and where it makes sense versus where it doesn't.</p>

<h3>Workiz</h3>

<p>Workiz targets high-volume operations: appliance repair, locksmith, garage door, anything where job turnaround is fast and dispatch needs to be completely frictionless. Multi-location support is better here than at most competitors in this price range. Starting price is around $198/month for a small team. Setup is more involved than FieldPulse or Jobber, which is worth factoring in if your team isn't particularly tech-comfortable. But if you're running 5+ techs doing quick in-and-out jobs all day, the dispatch tools justify the extra effort.</p>

<h3>Service Fusion</h3>

<p>Service Fusion comes in at $195/month on the Starter plan with unlimited users, which is a genuinely useful differentiator once you're running a bigger crew and watching per-user costs multiply. The trade-off is a UI that isn't as polished as FieldPulse or Jobber, and a mobile app that technicians tend to complain about in online reviews. For owners who are comfortable trading setup complexity for more customization options and no per-user ceiling, it deserves a look. For anyone who needs techs operational on day one, it's a harder sell.</p>

<h2>How to Choose the Right Platform for Your Operation</h2>

<p><strong>Match the software to your actual bottleneck. If dispatch is chaos, prioritize scheduling tools. If you're chasing invoices all week, focus on on-site payment collection. If your techs lose time on admin between jobs, look at automation. The right FSM platform is the one your crew will actually use every day, not the one with the longest feature list on a comparison page.</strong></p>

<p>For a 3-truck HVAC crew, FieldPulse and Jobber are both strong options. FieldPulse's flat-rate pricing catalog is a real advantage for HVAC shops that want consistent estimate pricing across all their techs, especially when different guys are quoting the same job types. If you want a deeper look at HVAC-specific features and which platforms are built with that trade in mind, the <a href="/guides/best-hvac-software-2026">best HVAC software picks for 2026</a> goes further than this comparison can.</p>

<p>For a solo plumber doing 8-10 calls a day, $99/month for FieldPulse is probably more than you need right now. Jobber's Core plan at $69/month covers scheduling, invoicing, and QBO sync without paying for dispatch and fleet features you won't use until you hire your first tech. Save the difference and revisit when the team grows.</p>

<p>For a cleaning or landscaping business with recurring clients and a large customer list, Housecall Pro's automated communication tools earn their price premium. The automated review requests, rebooking reminders, and customer-facing booking experience are things FieldPulse simply doesn't invest in at the same level. If that side of the business is where you're losing ground to competitors, Housecall Pro is worth the extra cost.</p>

<p>One thing that trips people up: they pick a platform based on the sales demo, then discover the mobile experience is rough once techs are out in the field. Always run a real trial with an actual tech on an actual device before you commit. If the tech is still confused after a week, you have your answer. A polished web demo means nothing if the app falls apart at the job site.</p>

<h2>Frequently Asked Questions</h2>

<h3>Q: How does FieldPulse's pricing compare to ServiceTitan for a small HVAC crew?</h3>
<p>FieldPulse starts at $99/month for up to 3 users, roughly 75% cheaper than ServiceTitan's $400+ monthly entry, though per-user overages and feature add-ons increase costs as your team grows. The critical difference is pricing transparency—FieldPulse publishes per-user fees upfront rather than burying them in trial fine print. For a 5-person crew, total cost typically lands between $150–250/month depending on feature selection.</p>

<h3>Q: What mobile features does FieldPulse offer for field dispatch and invoicing?</h3>
<p>FieldPulse's mobile app includes drag-and-drop job assignment, real-time job status updates, and on-site invoicing with same-day payment options—the three features with the highest impact on field productivity and cash flow. The app prioritizes offline capability, ensuring techs can keep working during connectivity gaps, and is designed for crews that rarely return to the office. Mobile performance is built as the primary interface, not a secondary feature tacked onto the web platform.</p>

<h3>Q: Does FieldPulse sync automatically with QuickBooks?</h3>
<p>Yes, FieldPulse integrates directly with QuickBooks Online and syncs job data, invoices, and customer information automatically without manual entry. This eliminates hours of monthly reconciliation work that field service owners typically waste on double-data-entry between systems. Real-time sync keeps your accounting current and cash flow visibility accurate.</p>

<h3>Q: What size team is FieldPulse built for—can a 6-person crew use it effectively?</h3>
<p>FieldPulse is purpose-built for crews of 2–8 technicians, so a 6-person team is squarely in its designed range. Scaling beyond 8–10 techs can strain the dispatch interface and per-user costs may exceed the platform's value proposition for larger operations. If you're planning to grow to 15+ techs within 2 years, a more enterprise-grade platform may be a better long-term investment.</p>
