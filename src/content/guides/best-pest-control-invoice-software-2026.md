<p><strong>The best pest control invoice software in 2026 is GorillaDesk for solo operators and small route businesses, starting at $49/month with built-in chemical tracking and recurring billing. <h2>What to Look For in Pest Control Invoice Software</h2>

<p><strong>Pest control invoicing has specific needs that generic billing software misses: recurring service agreements, chemical application logs, per-customer treatment history, and the ability to capture a signature on a tablet while standing in someone's backyard. If the software treats every invoice as a one-time transaction, it will slow you down within two weeks.</strong></p>

<p>Most HVAC or plumbing shops can get by with pretty basic invoicing tools. Pest control is different. You're managing subscriptions. Quarterly treatments that auto-invoice without you touching anything. Customers who call six months later asking what you sprayed near their kid's bedroom. That's not standard field service territory, and the software you pick should know the difference.</p>

<p>Here's what actually matters when evaluating options:</p>

<ul>
<li>Recurring billing and service agreements (monthly, quarterly, annual treatments)</li>
<li>Chemical and pesticide application logging for compliance and liability</li>
<li>Per-customer service history your techs can pull up on-site</li>
<li>Mobile invoicing with offline capability (some routes have terrible signal)</li>
<li>Customer-facing payment portal and digital signature capture</li>
<li>QuickBooks or Xero sync that doesn't require manual cleanup every Friday</li>
<li>Route optimization for multi-stop days</li>
<li>Automated renewal reminders so you're not chasing lapsed quarterly accounts</li>
</ul>

<p>Pricing ranges widely depending on whether you go with a pest-control-specific platform or a general <a href="/guides/field-service-management-buyers-guide">field service management tool</a> that happens to cover pest control well enough. Both approaches work. The tradeoffs are real and worth understanding before you commit.</p>

<h2>The 5 Best Pest Control Invoice Software Options in 2026</h2>

<p><strong>GorillaDesk tops the list for independent operators because it's purpose-built for pest control, with recurring invoices, chemical logs, and a mobile app that holds up in the field. PestPac handles enterprise complexity better. Jobber is the right call if you run multiple trades under one business. Housecall Pro and FieldRoutes fill specific gaps depending on operation size and how deep your tech patience goes.</strong></p>

<h3>1. GorillaDesk: Best for Independent Pest Control Operators</h3>

<p>GorillaDesk is built specifically for pest control and lawn care, and that specificity shows the moment you set up a recurring service agreement. The software knows what quarterly means. It auto-generates invoices, tracks which products you used at each property, and stores that data per customer so when someone calls two years later asking about that ant treatment in their pantry, you can pull it up in thirty seconds.</p>

<p>The mobile app is genuinely good. Techs can complete jobs, capture signatures, and send invoices without cell signal. It syncs when they're back in range. That matters when you're running route work in suburban or rural areas where signal drops constantly and you can't wait around for a bar to appear.</p>

<p><strong>GorillaDesk Pricing (2026):</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Basic: $49/month (1 user, core invoicing and scheduling)</li>
<li>Pro: $99/month (unlimited users, chemical tracking, customer portal)</li>
<li>Business: $149/month (route optimization, advanced reporting, bulk SMS)</li>
</ul>

<p>QuickBooks sync is available on Pro and Business plans. The Basic plan is fine if you're solo, but if you have even one tech in the field, pay the extra $50 for Pro. The chemical tracking alone is worth it for compliance records. If an applicator gets accused of using something they shouldn't have, you need that log and you need it to be clean.</p>

<p>What GorillaDesk doesn't handle well is multi-trade. If you do pest control and HVAC, or pest control and landscaping, under the same company umbrella, the platform starts feeling narrow. For pure pest control businesses running up to five or six trucks, it's the best per-dollar option in this category. It's not close.</p>

<h3>2. PestPac by WorkWave: Best for Large Route Operations</h3>

<p>PestPac is the enterprise choice. It's been in the pest control industry for decades, and the feature depth reflects that: multi-branch management, state-level compliance reporting, fleet GPS tracking, and a routing engine that actually saves fuel on a 20-stop day. If you're managing 10 or more routes out of multiple locations, PestPac is hard to match on functionality.</p>

<p>The flip side is cost and setup time. PestPac doesn't publish pricing publicly. Expect somewhere in the $200-400/month range depending on user count and the modules you need. Implementation takes time, and you'll want to budget for a few hours with their support team to get everything configured correctly. This is not a plug-and-play tool.</p>

<p>The invoicing module is thorough. Service agreements, one-time treatments, and commercial accounts all live on the same platform. Their customer portal lets clients view their service history, pay invoices online, and schedule re-services without calling your office. For after-hours inquiries that would otherwise go to voicemail, that self-service piece recovers real revenue.</p>

<p>QuickBooks integration exists, but some users report it requires more manual reconciliation than they'd like. If accounting accuracy is a weak point in your operation, ask specifically about that in the sales call before you sign anything.</p>

<h3>3. Jobber: Best for Multi-Trade Businesses That Include Pest Control</h3>

<p>Jobber isn't pest-control-specific, but it handles pest control invoicing well enough that a lot of companies running pest plus one or two other trades end up here. Recurring billing works. Quotes, invoices, and payments flow cleanly. The customer hub is one of the better self-service portals in the field service category, and the QuickBooks sync is reliable in a way that saves real time at month-end close.</p>

<p>If you're running pest control alongside exterior cleaning or general home service out of the same operation, Jobber's flexibility is a genuine advantage. You're not managing two separate platforms. For the complete breakdown of what works and what doesn't, the <a href="/reviews/jobber-review-2026">full Jobber 2026 review</a> covers it in detail.</p>

<p><strong>Jobber Pricing (2026):</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Core: $49/month (1 user)</li>
<li>Connect: $149/month (up to 5 users, client hub, two-way texting)</li>
<li>Grow: $249/month (unlimited users, quote follow-ups, referral tracking)</li>
</ul>

<p>The chemical tracking that pest control operators specifically need isn't there natively. You're working around it with custom fields or keeping separate compliance logs. Not a dealbreaker if you have another system for that piece, but it's a real gap compared to GorillaDesk or PestPac. Go into Jobber knowing that limitation exists.</p>

<h3>4. Housecall Pro: Best for Growing Pest Control Companies Wanting Automation</h3>

<p>Housecall Pro's strongest selling point for pest control is its automation layer. Recurring invoice reminders, automated review requests after completed jobs, follow-up texts when a customer hasn't rebooked their quarterly treatment. For a pest control company trying to protect retention on subscription accounts, those automations have measurable value. Not theoretical value. Actual callbacks that would have otherwise lapsed.</p>

<p>The mobile app holds up in the field. Invoicing is fast. The customer-facing experience from booking confirmation to payment link looks professional without requiring any design work on your end. For a full cost breakdown and whether the pricing tier is justified for your stage, the <a href="/reviews/housecall-pro-review-2026">Housecall Pro 2026 review</a> goes deep on that.</p>

<p><strong>Housecall Pro Pricing (2026):</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Basic: $59/month (1 user)</li>
<li>Essentials: $149/month (up to 5 users)</li>
<li>MAX: $189/month (unlimited users, advanced reporting)</li>
</ul>

<p>Like Jobber, Housecall Pro isn't built specifically for pest control and chemical tracking isn't native. But if you're a pest control company that's grown to three or four techs and you want better customer automation without paying for PestPac's complexity and implementation timeline, Housecall Pro is worth a demo.</p>

<h3>5. FieldRoutes: Best for Scaling Pest Control and Lawn Businesses</h3>

<p>FieldRoutes is part of the ServiceTitan family, built specifically for pest control and lawn service companies. It sits between GorillaDesk and PestPac in scope and pricing, which makes it an interesting middle-ground option for operations in the 5-15 truck range that have outgrown GorillaDesk but aren't ready for PestPac's enterprise overhead.</p>

<p>The invoicing and routing are built for subscription-based route businesses. Automated billing on service cycles, route density optimization, and customer self-service for payments and scheduling. Reporting is stronger than GorillaDesk's, but setup is more involved and the sales process takes longer than you'd probably like.</p>

<p>Pricing is custom (sales call required), but it typically runs higher than GorillaDesk. If you're evaluating FieldRoutes, it's worth considering <a href="/reviews/is-servicetitan-worth-it-2026">whether ServiceTitan's broader ecosystem makes sense</a> for where your business is heading, since they share platform lineage and the upgrade path between them exists.</p>

<h2>How to Choose the Right Pest Control Invoice Software</h2>

<p><strong>The decision comes down to a handful of clear factors: whether you need chemical tracking built in, how many trucks you're running, what your budget ceiling is, and whether pest control is your only trade or one of several services under the same business. Get those answers and the shortlist writes itself.</strong></p>

<p>If you're a solo operator or running two or three trucks doing pest control only, GorillaDesk is the obvious starting point. The pricing makes sense, the feature set matches the job, and you won't spend six weeks in implementation. Set up a trial, load a few real customers, build out two or three service agreements, and see if the recurring billing does what you need.</p>

<p>If you're doing pest control alongside HVAC, cleaning, or general home service, don't buy pest-control-specific software. You'll end up managing two platforms and the friction isn't worth the niche features. Jobber or Housecall Pro will cover 90% of what you need and give you one system for the whole operation. For a direct head-to-head on those two, the <a href="/comparisons/jobber-vs-housecall-pro">Jobber vs. Housecall Pro 2026 comparison</a> breaks it down side by side.</p>

<p>If you're north of 10 trucks or managing multiple service locations, the math on GorillaDesk starts getting uncomfortable and you'll probably want PestPac or FieldRoutes regardless. Both have the routing, compliance reporting, and multi-location support you'll actually need at that scale. Accept the longer sales cycle and implementation timeline. It's the cost of getting the right tool.</p>

<p>One underrated factor: ask each vendor exactly what their QuickBooks sync does. "Syncs with QuickBooks" can mean automatic two-way sync, or it can mean you can export a CSV. Those are not the same thing, and if your bookkeeper runs everything through QuickBooks, you need a clear answer before you sign anything.</p>

<h2>Frequently Asked Questions</h2>
<h3>Q: How much does pest control invoice software cost per month?</h3>
<p>Most pest control invoice software ranges from $49 to $150+ per month depending on business size and feature set. GorillaDesk starts at $49/month for solo operators, while platforms like PestPac by WorkWave typically cost $100+ per month for businesses managing 10+ trucks with route optimization and multi-user access.</p>
<h3>Q: Do pest control invoicing tools include chemical tracking and compliance logging?</h3>
<p>Yes, purpose-built pest control software like GorillaDesk and PestPac includes chemical application logs and per-customer treatment history for compliance and liability documentation. This feature is critical because it automates what generic billing software forces you to track manually—recurring quarterly treatments, specific pesticides used, and historical records customers may need months or years later.</p>
<h3>Q: Can pest control invoice software integrate with QuickBooks and Xero?</h3>
<p>Most modern pest control platforms including GorillaDesk, Jobber, and Housecall Pro offer direct sync with QuickBooks or Xero, though the quality of integration varies. The key is verifying that data flows automatically without requiring manual cleanup in your accounting software each week—poor integrations create more work than they solve.</p>
<h3>Q: Does pest control invoice software work offline with mobile signatures?</h3>
<p>Yes, field-focused platforms like GorillaDesk, Jobber, and Housecall Pro offer offline mobile invoicing with digital signature capture, which is essential for technicians working in areas with poor cell signal. This capability lets you complete invoices and capture customer signatures on a tablet in the field without returning to the office.</p>
