<p><strong>Connecteam is a workforce management app that handles scheduling, GPS time tracking, team communication, and digital checklists. To use it for field service, you build your crew in the team directory, configure the Operations hub with shift schedules and job assignments, enable GPS clock-in with geofencing, and run team comms through the built-in chat. Free for up to 10 users; paid plans start at $29/month for up to 30 users.</strong></p>

<h2>What to Look For in Field Service Workforce Software</h2>

<p><strong>Field service workforce software needs to do four things well: confirm crews are physically on-site (GPS clock-in), assign and communicate jobs clearly, handle shift scheduling without phone tag, and produce clean records for payroll. Software that misses any of those four forces you to cover the gap manually, which defeats the point.</strong></p>

<p>Most field service owners I've talked to have the same setup. Dispatch is in one tool, payroll is in another, team communication happens in a group text nobody wants to manage, and job documentation exists in theory but gets skipped in practice. The question isn't whether you need software. It's whether you need a single platform that handles everything or a few focused tools that work together without getting in each other's way.</p>

<p>Connecteam solves the workforce side: who's working, when they clocked in, what's assigned to them, and how the team communicates. It doesn't solve the customer side. No invoicing, no estimates, no online booking. That distinction matters before you spend money on anything. If you're looking for a platform that handles the full job cycle from customer booking through payment collection, start with the <a href="/guides/field-service-management-buyers-guide">Field Service Management Software Buyer's Guide 2026</a> and come back to Connecteam after you've got that layer sorted out.</p>

<p>For the workforce coordination problems specifically, here's what actually matters when you're evaluating options:</p>

<ul>
  <li>GPS time clock with real geofencing enforcement, not just location logging after the fact</li>
  <li>Shift scheduling your field techs can check on their phone in under 10 seconds</li>
  <li>Job assignments with notes, checklists, and documents already attached</li>
  <li>Team communication that doesn't run through your personal cell number</li>
  <li>Payroll export that connects to whatever accounting software you already use</li>
</ul>

<h2>How to Get Started with Connecteam</h2>

<p><strong>Getting Connecteam running for a field crew takes one full day. Create your account, add users by phone number or CSV upload, organize them into groups by trade or crew, configure the Operations hub with your job schedule, and turn on GPS time clock with geofencing. The mobile app is where field workers spend all their time, so test it on your crew's actual phones before you call it done.</strong></p>

<h3>Account Setup and Adding Your Crew</h3>

<p>Sign up at connecteam.com and a setup wizard asks about your industry and team size. Don't rush this part. The answers affect which templates surface first, and starting with the right template saves you 20 to 30 minutes of manual configuration.</p>

<p>Adding team members lives under the Users section. You can add them one at a time with name, phone number, and email, or upload a CSV file if you've got a bigger operation. Each person gets an invite by text or email with a direct download link. The app is clean and genuinely simple to navigate. I've seen HVAC techs in their late 50s figure it out without any hand-holding, which is not something you can say about every platform that markets itself as easy to use.</p>

<p>Set up user groups right away: "Plumbers," "HVAC Techs," "Crew A," "Office Staff," whatever matches your structure. This becomes important the moment you want to push a schedule update or announcement to one specific crew without blasting everybody else.</p>

<h3>Configuring the Scheduling Module</h3>

<p>The scheduling module lives in the Operations hub. You build a weekly schedule on a visual calendar, assign shifts by person or by job, and set patterns to repeat. For a pest control company running the same routes every week, this step alone replaces a significant amount of manual coordination that was probably happening over text.</p>

<p>The part most people miss: you can attach job notes, required forms, and checklists directly to a shift. When a tech opens their daily schedule, they don't just see a time and address. They see the pre-arrival inspection checklist, service notes from the last visit, and any documentation you've uploaded for that job type. That kind of front-loading saves callbacks and rework without adding any extra steps for the field tech.</p>

<h3>Setting Up GPS Time Clock</h3>

<p>This is Connecteam's best feature for field crews. Under Time Clock, you enable GPS stamps on clock-in and clock-out, and you can configure geofencing by job site. Enter an address, define a radius (50 feet up to 500 feet depending on the site), and your techs can only clock in when they're physically present at that location. No more clocking in from the truck two miles down the road.</p>

<p>On shared devices, you can require a selfie at clock-in. Most small crews with individual smartphones skip this, but if you run a cleaning company where crews share a tablet at each location, it's a useful option. Time tracking reports show hours by employee, by job, and by pay period. Payroll exports to QuickBooks, Gusto, Paychex, and several others, though the depth of integration depends on which plan you're on.</p>

<h3>Team Communication Setup</h3>

<p>Connecteam's communication hub is what separates it from basic scheduling apps. Group chats, direct messaging, announcement channels, a company knowledge base, and a news feed all live in one place. Think of it as a team communication app designed for people who are never at a desk.</p>

<p>For a cleaning company with 15 crews spread across different neighborhoods, this replaces the group text thread that was previously unmanageable. Post supply order reminders, share safety policy updates with read receipts so you know who actually saw them, pin documents techs need on-site, and keep operational updates out of your personal inbox. The read receipt feature specifically is worth the price of entry if you've ever had to prove that a policy change was communicated to your whole team.</p>

<h2>Connecteam's Core Features for Field Service</h2>

<p><strong>The four Connecteam features field service companies rely on most are GPS time clock with geofencing, shift scheduling with job attachments, team communication and document sharing, and digital checklists and forms. These cover workforce operations effectively. They don't replace customer management or invoicing, so plan your software stack accordingly before committing.</strong></p>

<h3>Digital Checklists and Forms</h3>

<p>You build custom forms in the Operations hub. For HVAC, that's seasonal maintenance checklists. For roofing, pre-job safety inspections. For pest control, treatment logs with chemical type, application method, and quantity. You can set forms as required before a tech can clock out of a job, which is the practical way to make sure documentation gets done rather than skipped at the end of a long day.</p>

<p>Completed forms sync to the admin dashboard with timestamps, the submitting tech's name, and GPS location at submission. That combination of details matters more than people realize when you're dealing with insurance claims or a customer disputing what work was done.</p>

<h3>Training and Onboarding</h3>

<p>Connecteam's Courses feature lets you build training modules with video, images, and quizzes. For an electrical contractor bringing on apprentices, you can build a company-specific onboarding course, a safety certification review, and an equipment guide all in one place. New hires complete it async on their phone before their first day on-site.</p>

<p>High-turnover trades get the most out of this. If you're onboarding five new cleaning techs a month, having a structured digital course cuts your in-person training time significantly. The trade-off is that self-paced digital training doesn't replace hands-on mentoring for anything that involves real safety risk. Use it for the orientation and policy layer, not the technical skills layer.</p>

<h3>Job Dispatch (Know Its Limits)</h3>

<p>Connecteam includes basic job dispatch: assign a job with location, time, notes, and attachments. It's not a full FSM dispatch board. There's no customer portal, no automated appointment reminders going out to customers, and no route optimization. For that level of dispatch functionality, <a href="/reviews/jobber-review-2026">Jobber's scheduling and dispatch tools</a> are purpose-built for customer-facing field service in a way Connecteam doesn't try to be.</p>

<h2>Where Connecteam Falls Short</h2>

<p><strong>Connecteam's biggest gaps for field service: no customer-facing features at all (no estimates, invoices, or online booking), no route optimization, and deeper payroll integrations require paid tiers. If your main problems are chasing down payments, converting leads to booked jobs, or sending professional proposals, Connecteam doesn't touch any of that.</strong></p>

<p>The pricing model is worth understanding before you commit. The $29/month Basic plan covers up to 30 users for scheduling, time tracking, and communication. That's a reasonable price. But if you need advanced reporting, longer time clock history exports, or workflow automations, you move to $49 or $99/month. The free plan is genuinely functional for small teams, but the 10-user cap is easy to outgrow within a year of any real growth.</p>

<p>The other thing that catches people off guard: you still need a separate tool for estimates, invoices, and customer communication. If you're running a 3-truck HVAC crew and you add Connecteam at $29/month, you're still looking at something like <a href="/comparisons/connecteam-vs-housecall-pro-2026">Housecall Pro or a comparable FSM platform</a> for the customer side. Budget for both or choose one platform that covers both sides less elegantly. Neither answer is wrong; it depends on where your current bottleneck actually is.</p>

<h2>Connecteam vs. Field Service Alternatives</h2>

<p><strong>Connecteam competes with workforce tools like Homebase and When I Work, not with FSM platforms like Jobber or ServiceTitan. Comparing Connecteam to Jobber is apples to oranges: Jobber is a customer-facing service platform, Connecteam is an internal operations tool. A lot of field service companies run both because the problem spaces don't overlap much.</strong></p>

<h2>How to Choose: Who Should Actually Use Connecteam</h2>

<p><strong>Connecteam makes sense for field service companies where the bottleneck is internal coordination, not customer management. It's built for 5 to 150-person operations running distributed crews. If your main pain points are scheduling chaos, time theft, or crew communication, Connecteam directly addresses those. If your problem is getting estimates out faster or collecting payments on time, solve that first.</strong></p>

<p>Here's how to think about it by situation:</p>

<ul>
  <li>A 15-person landscaping crew: Connecteam is close to a perfect fit. Assign crews by zone, GPS enforce clock-in at job sites, run seasonal maintenance checklists, and move team communication off your personal phone once and for all.</li>
  <li>A solo HVAC tech or 2-truck operation: probably overkill. At this size, a single FSM platform handles scheduling and customer work in one place without the overhead of managing a separate workforce tool.</li>
  <li>A cleaning company with 10 or more distributed crews: very strong fit. The time clock, checklist, and communication features are designed for exactly this kind of business where teams are spread out and turnover runs high.</li>
  <li>An electrical contractor bringing on apprentices: good fit for training modules and safety documentation. Still needs an FSM for estimates and billing, so factor in that second subscription before committing.</li>
  <li>A 30-plus person plumbing operation: consider pairing Connecteam for the people side with <a href="/guides/best-plumbing-software-2026-guide">purpose-built plumbing software</a> for dispatch and customer billing. The two cover different enough ground that running both is cleaner than forcing one to do both jobs.</li>
</ul>

<h2>Frequently Asked Questions</h2>
<h3>Q: How much is Connecteam for a 20-person field crew per month?</h3>
<p>Connecteam costs $29/month for up to 30 users, so a 20-person crew pays a flat $29/month with no per-person overage. The free tier covers only up to 10 users, making the $29 plan the practical minimum for field service teams larger than a handful of people.</p>
<h3>Q: Does Connecteam replace my dispatch software, or is it crew-only?</h3>
<p>Connecteam handles crew coordination—scheduling, job assignment, GPS clock-in, and team communication—but doesn't manage customer dispatch, booking, or route optimization. It pairs with your dispatch system, not replaces it, ensuring field workers execute the jobs assigned to them through your existing tools.</p>
<h3>Q: Does Connecteam connect to payroll, or do you export timesheets manually?</h3>
<p>Connecteam produces clean attendance records with GPS-verified clock-in times and shift data ready for payroll systems. It generates the records payroll software needs but doesn't process payroll itself; most users export the data or integrate it with their existing payroll platform.</p>
<h3>Q: What's the actual geofencing workflow—do workers have to be on-site to clock in?</h3>
<p>Yes; Connecteam enforces geofencing on clock-in, requiring workers to be physically within the job site boundary to log time. This prevents payroll fraud and confirms crews are actually on-site, rather than just recording location after clocking in remotely.</p>
