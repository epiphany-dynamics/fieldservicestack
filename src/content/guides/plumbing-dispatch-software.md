**Plumbing dispatch software assigns the right technician to the right job in the right order, tracks parts and truck stock, and pushes address, history, and flat-rate pricing to the tech's phone. For plumbers, the make-or-break features are same-day emergency routing, callback tracking, and estimate-to-invoice speed, not generic calendar tools.**

Plumbing throws problems that a generic scheduling app never planned for. A burst supply line at 4 p.m. jumps the queue past three drain-cleaning stops. A water heater install needs a two-person crew and a permit. A warranty callback has to route back to the tech who did the original work. The right software makes those calls fast. The wrong one turns your dispatcher into a human switchboard. This guide scores what matters for the plumbing trade specifically.

## What plumbing dispatch software actually needs to do

**Good plumbing dispatch software handles four jobs at once: it slots emergency calls into a full board without blowing up the day, it pushes job history and flat-rate pricing to the technician, it captures parts used off the truck, and it moves an approved estimate to a paid invoice before the tech leaves the driveway.**

Most field service platforms schedule appointments. Plumbing needs more than appointments. Consider a Tuesday where a dispatcher is holding six booked jobs and a call comes in for a flooding basement. The software should show which tech is closest, who has a wet vac and a pump on the truck, and which of the six booked jobs can slide to tomorrow without a customer revolt.

The features that separate plumbing-ready tools from generic ones:

- **Skill and truck-stock matching** so a repipe job does not get sent to a tech carrying only drain gear.
- **Callback flagging** that ties a return visit to the original invoice and technician, so you can see whether a job is a genuine warranty issue or a new problem.
- **Flat-rate price book** on the mobile app, so techs present options at the door instead of guessing.
- **Permit and multi-tech job support** for water heater swaps, sewer line replacements, and re-pipes.
- **Deposit and financing capture** in the field for larger tickets.

## How to score plumbing dispatch software before you buy

**Score every vendor against the jobs your dispatch board actually runs, not a feature list. Weight emergency routing, mobile estimating, and callback tracking heavily, because those three drive plumbing revenue and repeat-visit cost. Demo each tool with one real emergency scenario and one multi-tech install from your own week.**

Use a weighted scorecard so a slick demo does not overrule daily fit. Score each area 1 to 5, multiply by the weight, and add it up. The weights below are a starting point; adjust them to your shop.

| Capability | Why it matters for plumbing | Weight |
|---|---|---|
| Emergency insertion and routing | Same-day burst-pipe calls fund the month | 5 |
| Mobile flat-rate estimating | Techs sell options at the door | 4 |
| Callback and warranty tracking | Repeat visits eat margin silently | 4 |
| Parts and truck-stock tracking | Prevents second trips for a $6 fitting | 3 |
| Estimate-to-invoice speed | Cash in the account, not in a shoebox | 4 |
| Multi-tech and permit jobs | Water heaters and re-pipes need it | 3 |
| Reporting by tech and job type | Shows which work actually pays | 2 |

A tool that scores high on booking but low on emergency insertion is fine for a maintenance-heavy commercial shop and wrong for a residential service-and-repair plumber living on same-day calls. Score against your mix.

## Real plumbing dispatch scenarios and how software should handle them

**The honest test of any platform is how it behaves on your worst Tuesday. Run three scenarios in every demo: an after-hours emergency, a warranty callback on a two-week-old job, and a scheduled water heater install that needs two techs and a permit. If the tool stumbles on these, it will stumble every day.**

### The 4 p.m. flooding emergency

A call comes in with water spreading across a finished basement. Good software surfaces the nearest available tech with the right equipment, shows the customer's address and any prior job history, and lets the dispatcher bump a lower-priority stop with a one-tap customer notification. A pairing worth having: a [missed-call text-back service](/guides/missed-call-text-back-service/) so the calls you cannot answer during the scramble still get a reply and a booking link instead of dialing your competitor.

### The warranty callback

Two weeks after a faucet install, the customer calls back with a drip. The system should tie that callback to the original invoice and technician, flag it as warranty, and route it back to the same tech when possible. Without this, callbacks hide inside your normal job volume and you never learn which techs or which parts generate return trips.

### The water heater install

This is a scheduled, higher-ticket job that may need two techs, a permit, and a deposit. The software should block the right crew and time window, attach the permit checklist, and capture a deposit or financing approval in the field. If the platform treats this like a 30-minute service call, your dispatcher is doing that math by hand.

## What plumbing dispatch software costs

**Expect roughly $30 to $200 per user per month for most residential plumbing platforms, with enterprise tiers running higher and often requiring a custom quote and onboarding fee. Price the total for your real seat count plus payment-processing fees, not the advertised starting rate, which usually covers a single user.**

Pricing tiers in this market generally break into three bands. Entry tools cover scheduling and invoicing for a one-truck operation. Mid-market tools add flat-rate price books, memberships, and stronger dispatch boards. Enterprise platforms add call-center features, capacity planning, and deep reporting, and they quote based on your size. For a concrete example of the enterprise end, see the breakdown of [what ServiceTitan costs in 2026](/guides/what-does-servicetitan-cost-2026/), and compare mid-market fit through the [Housecall Pro vs Service Fusion comparison](/compare/housecall-pro-vs-service-fusion-2026/).

One number to model before you sign: payment processing. If a platform takes a cut of every card and financing transaction, a shop running big water-heater and re-pipe tickets can pay more in fees than in subscription. Ask for the effective rate on a $4,000 job, not just the monthly seat price.

## When dispatch software is the wrong fix

**If your problem is that calls go unanswered or estimates never get sent, new dispatch software will not fix it, and may hide the leak. Software routes work; it does not create the discipline to answer phones, follow up on estimates, or close callbacks. Fix the process first, then buy the tool that supports it.**

A single-truck plumber doing 15 jobs a week on a whiteboard and a paper invoice book may genuinely be fine, and a $150-per-month platform can be more overhead than help until volume grows. Before buying, run a quick audit of where jobs actually stall using the [dispatch-to-invoice audit approach](/guides/how-to-automate-business-processes/). If the bottleneck is answering calls or sending estimates, solve that first. Buy software to scale a working process, not to rescue a broken one.

## Frequently asked questions

### What is the difference between dispatch software and scheduling software for plumbers?

Scheduling software books appointments onto a calendar. Dispatch software adds the real-time decisions: who goes to the emergency, who has the right parts, how to reshuffle a full board when a burst pipe jumps the queue. Most plumbing platforms include both, but weak dispatch logic is a common gap. Test it directly in the demo.

### Do I need plumbing-specific software or will general field service software work?

General field service platforms work if they support flat-rate price books, callback tracking, multi-tech jobs, and truck-stock. Many popular tools do. The label matters less than whether it handles your emergency insertion and estimate-to-invoice flow. Score against the scenarios above rather than chasing a "plumbing" badge.

### How much should a small plumbing shop expect to pay?

A one- to three-truck shop typically lands in the roughly $30 to $200 per user per month range depending on features, plus payment-processing fees on card and financing transactions. Model your real seat count and a typical big-ticket job's processing cost before committing to an annual plan.

### Can plumbing dispatch software reduce callbacks?

Not by itself, but it makes callbacks visible. When a return visit is tied to the original invoice and technician, you can spot which techs, parts, or job types generate repeat trips and address the root cause. Software that buries callbacks inside normal volume leaves that margin leak invisible.

### What should I test in a free trial or demo?

Run three of your own real jobs through it: an after-hours emergency, a two-week-old warranty callback, and a two-tech water heater install with a permit and deposit. Time how long estimate-to-invoice takes on a phone. If the tool handles your worst Tuesday cleanly, it will handle the routine days.
