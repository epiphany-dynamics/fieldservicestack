<p><strong>ServiceTitan is not a traditional CRM. It's a field service management platform with CRM-like features built in: customer history, contact records, follow-up reminders, and marketing automation. It goes well beyond what a CRM does, covering dispatch, invoicing, technician GPS tracking, and pricebook management. Base pricing starts around $398/month.</strong></p>

<p>The question comes up because "CRM" gets used loosely. When people ask whether ServiceTitan is a CRM, they're usually asking: does this thing track my customers, store my job history, and help me follow up with people who haven't called in a while? Yes, it does all of that. But calling it a CRM is like calling a Swiss Army knife a can opener. Technically accurate in one narrow case, and it misses what the tool actually is.</p>

<p>Here's what actually matters: does ServiceTitan replace your need for a standalone CRM, and if you don't want to pay ServiceTitan's price, what else handles the customer relationship side well? That's the question this guide answers.</p>

<h2>What to Look for in a Field Service CRM</h2>

<p><strong>Field service businesses need customer management tools built for the job-cycle model: inbound calls, dispatched techs, repeat visits, and annual maintenance agreements. The must-haves are full customer job history, follow-up reminders for dormant accounts, post-job communication tools, and some way to track open estimates. Marketing automation is a bonus for larger operations.</strong></p>

<p>A CRM built for a B2B sales team tracks leads through a funnel over weeks or months. That's mostly not what a plumber or HVAC contractor needs. What you actually need from customer management software in field service:</p>

<ul>
<li>Every job you've ever done for a customer on one screen, with notes and photos attached</li>
<li>A way to log what the tech found on-site and what the CSR promised on the phone, in the same record</li>
<li>Follow-up reminders for maintenance agreements, annual cleanings, or customers who haven't called in 18 months</li>
<li>Automated texts or emails after a job to collect reviews and feedback</li>
<li>Some way to track estimates that haven't turned into jobs yet</li>
</ul>

<p>Every platform in this guide hits most of those five. The differences are in depth, price, and how much setup it takes to make those features actually work. ServiceTitan's version is more complete than anyone else's in this space. It's also more expensive and harder to learn. That tradeoff is the core of what you're deciding here.</p>

<h2>Top 5 FSM Platforms with CRM Features, Ranked for 2026</h2>

<p><strong>ServiceTitan leads on depth and marketing automation, starting at approximately $398/month. Jobber at $169/month and Housecall Pro at $169/month cover the basics well for smaller operations. Service Fusion offers flat-rate unlimited user pricing from $225/month. FieldPulse at around $99/month is the best pick for small crews that need estimate pipeline tracking.</strong></p>

<h3>1. ServiceTitan</h3>

<p>ServiceTitan is built for residential and commercial trades at scale: HVAC, plumbing, electrical, roofing, pest control. For a 15-truck operation running 50+ calls a day, it's the most complete tool in the industry. The customer management reflects that. Every record has a full job history, call recordings, tech notes, photos, and a timeline of the customer relationship going back to the first call. The marketing suite lets you run automated follow-ups after service calls, target lapsed customers who haven't booked in 12 months, and fire off direct mail campaigns without leaving the platform. Most standalone CRMs don't do any of that out of the box.</p>

<p>Where it falls short on the CRM side: it doesn't handle long B2B sales cycles. If you're quoting a $200k commercial job with a three-month decision timeline and multiple stakeholders, ServiceTitan doesn't give you the pipeline views and activity logging you'd want. It's built for residential service, not enterprise sales. For commercial HVAC shops with a real sales team working new construction contracts, the honest answer is a real sales CRM running alongside ServiceTitan.</p>

<p>Pricing runs approximately $398/month on the low end, scaling toward $1,500+/month for larger teams once user seats and add-ons are included. The mobile app is strong and covers the full job workflow on-site. QuickBooks two-way sync is included. The learning curve is real: plan 2-4 weeks of actual training time before your team is comfortable. If you want the full cost picture before signing, the <a href="/guides/what-does-servicetitan-cost-2026">ServiceTitan cost breakdown</a> covers what you're actually going to spend once implementation fees are factored in.</p>

<h3>2. Jobber</h3>

<p>Jobber is the right call for small to mid-size operations (1-15 techs) across most residential trades. The customer management is solid for the price. Every client record has full job history, notes, quotes, and invoices in one place. Automated follow-up reminders work without much configuration. The automated review request after job completion is a genuine feature: it drives Google reviews without anyone on your team having to remember to ask. For a 5-truck plumbing crew doing mostly inbound service calls, that covers the main CRM bases without any drama.</p>

<p>Jobber doesn't have deep marketing automation or lead tracking for prospects who aren't clients yet. There's no pipeline view for open estimates. The reporting is good but not the kind of operational analytics ServiceTitan produces. For most operations at this scale, none of that is a real problem.</p>

<p>Pricing: Core at $69/month (1 user), Connect at $169/month (up to 5 users), Grow at $349/month. Most operations land on Connect. The mobile app is one of the cleaner tech apps in this price range. QuickBooks two-way sync works reliably. Most owners are up and running within a day or two. The <a href="/reviews/jobber-review-2026">full Jobber review</a> covers where it earns its price and where it runs out of runway for bigger operations.</p>

<h3>3. Housecall Pro</h3>

<p>Housecall Pro sits between Jobber and ServiceTitan on both price and feature depth. Customer management includes job history, tech notes, automated text and email notifications, and a customer portal where clients can track arrival windows. The marketing tools include automated follow-ups after service and a basic email campaign builder. More than Jobber on the marketing side, less than ServiceTitan overall.</p>

<p>The pricing has crept up over the last couple years. The $65/month Basic plan is stripped down. For the customer management features that actually matter, you typically need the $169/month Essentials plan or the $299/month MAX plan. That puts it in the same price range as Jobber's Connect tier, so the comparison comes down to which feature set fits your workflow better. The <a href="/reviews/housecall-pro-review-2026">Housecall Pro review</a> breaks down the tier differences with specifics on what you actually get at each price point.</p>

<p>Mobile app is functional. QuickBooks sync is included. Moderate learning curve: more involved than Jobber, much less than ServiceTitan.</p>

<h3>4. Service Fusion</h3>

<p>Service Fusion's angle is flat-rate unlimited user pricing. Starter at $225/month, Plus at $350/month, Pro at $495/month. If you're growing a team and you hate paying an extra $30-50 per seat every time you hire a tech, that pricing model is worth paying attention to. Customer management is competent: records, job history, notes, basic follow-up tools. No marketing automation to speak of.</p>

<p>For a growing crew that mainly needs job history, invoicing, and dispatch without per-user fees, it's solid value. For someone who actually wants CRM-style marketing follow-up or lead tracking, the feature set is limited. The mobile app works but the UI is dated. QuickBooks integration is included.</p>

<h3>5. FieldPulse</h3>

<p>FieldPulse has been building out its customer management tools over the last couple years and it shows. Customer records, job history, follow-up reminders, and a basic pipeline view for open estimates. It's one of the few options in this price tier that gives you anything resembling estimate pipeline tracking, which helps when you're quoting bigger jobs and need to see where things stand without digging through notes.</p>

<p>Pricing starts around $99/month and scales with users. The mobile app is clean and easy to use. QuickBooks sync is included. It's one of the more intuitive platforms to set up. For a small crew (under 10 techs) that wants something between basic job management and full enterprise FSM, FieldPulse is worth a real look.</p>

<h2>How to Choose Based on Your Operation Size</h2>

<p><strong>Small operations (1-10 techs) should start with Jobber or FieldPulse. Mid-size operations (10-25 techs) where marketing follow-up matters should evaluate Housecall Pro or ServiceTitan. Large operations above 20 techs with high call volume will likely find ServiceTitan pays for itself through the reporting, dispatch optimization, and marketing automation alone.</strong></p>

<p>There are three situations worth thinking through separately.</p>

<p><strong>Small operation, 1-10 techs:</strong> You don't need ServiceTitan's CRM depth. You need clean customer records, fast invoicing, and a tech app that doesn't crash on the job site. Jobber at $169/month handles all three. The automated review requests alone are worth the subscription price for most operations in this tier. You don't need to spend $400/month to get good customer management.</p>

<p><strong>Mid-size operation, 10-20 techs:</strong> This is where the decision gets more interesting. If you're doing high call volume and running real marketing campaigns to drive repeat business, Housecall Pro's email campaign tools start to make sense. If you're growing fast and want the full operational picture (reporting, dispatch optimization, CSR coaching), ServiceTitan starts to pencil out even at the higher price.</p>

<p><strong>Commercial work with real sales cycles:</strong> If you're quoting large contracts with multiple decision-makers and timelines that run weeks or months, none of these platforms handles the sales pipeline side particularly well. The practical answer is pairing your FSM (ServiceTitan or Jobber for job management) with a sales CRM like HubSpot's free or Starter tier for the pipeline side. More pieces to manage, but it's the honest answer for that use case.</p>

ServiceTitan is worth the investment for operations with 10+ techs where the marketing automation, call recording, and reporting will actively drive revenue. Don't buy enterprise software to solve a problem that a $169/month tool already handles.</strong></p>

<p>ServiceTitan is not a CRM. It's a field service management platform that includes excellent customer management tools. For a large residential HVAC or plumbing operation, those tools are genuinely good and they're part of a platform that also handles dispatch, invoicing, technician tracking, and marketing in one place. You'd be hard-pressed to find a standalone CRM that does better at the things that actually matter for residential service.</p>

<p>But "excellent customer management tools inside an expensive platform" is not the right answer for every operation. If you're running 5 trucks and your main job is making sure techs get to the right address and customers get invoiced, Jobber handles that and stores your customer history just fine for a lot less money.</p>

<h2>Frequently Asked Questions</h2>
<h3>Q: Does ServiceTitan have all the customer management features of HubSpot or Salesforce?</h3>
<p>ServiceTitan covers job-cycle customer management—service history, maintenance reminders, post-job follow-up—but lacks advanced lead scoring, multi-stage sales pipelines, and AI-driven prospect ranking that dedicated CRMs offer. For field service businesses, ServiceTitan's integration with dispatch and invoicing is more valuable than general CRM features, but B2B sales teams would find it limiting.</p>
<h3>Q: What important CRM features does ServiceTitan lack?</h3>
<p>ServiceTitan doesn't include sophisticated lead scoring, complex sales pipeline stages, or AI-powered prospect analytics—tools designed for teams managing long sales cycles with multiple decision-makers. If your business model is sales-driven rather than service-driven, these missing features could be a significant gap.</p>
<h3>Q: Do I need both ServiceTitan and a separate CRM?</h3>
<p>For plumbing, HVAC, and most field service businesses, ServiceTitan is sufficient on its own—it handles customer history, job logging, follow-up automation, and post-job communication without additional CRM software. A separate CRM makes sense only if you run parallel B2B sales operations with complex deal pipelines.</p>
<h3>Q: How does ServiceTitan's price compare to standalone CRM tools?</h3>
<p>ServiceTitan starts at approximately $398/month, roughly 9–36 times higher than entry-level CRMs like HubSpot Free ($0) or Pipedrive ($11/month), but includes dispatching, invoicing, and technician GPS tracking that would otherwise require multiple subscription tools.</p>
