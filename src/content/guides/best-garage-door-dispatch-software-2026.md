<p><strong>Workiz is the best garage door dispatch software for most shops in 2026, with plans starting at $65/month. It handles live dispatch, GPS tracking, and phone call-to-job creation better than general field service tools. For solo operators, Jobber at $49/month is the right size. <h2>What to Look For in Garage Door Dispatch Software</h2>

<p><strong>Garage door dispatch is different from other trades because jobs are short, call volume is high, and the same address shows up again. Look for a fast dispatch board with GPS tracking, auto-job creation from inbound calls, address-level customer history, and a mobile app strong enough to handle payment collection and job photos without a call back to the office.</strong></p>

<p>When a spring snaps on a Friday morning, you're taking 6 calls before 9 AM. The right software lets your office person look at a board, drag a tech to the closest job, and fire off a confirmation text in under a minute. If you're doing that across two different apps or on a whiteboard, you're losing jobs to whoever answers faster.</p>

<p>Here's what actually matters for garage door specifically:</p>

<ul>
<li>Live dispatch board with drag-and-drop and real-time tech locations on a map</li>
<li>Auto-created jobs from inbound calls, not manual entry every time a customer calls</li>
<li>Customer history tied to address, not just name, so you know 123 Oak St had a spring swap 14 months ago</li>
<li>Parts inventory tracking for springs, cables, openers, and remotes</li>
<li>On-site payment collection through the mobile app</li>
<li>Job photos attached to the work order before the tech leaves the driveway</li>
<li>QuickBooks or accounting integration to stop double-entering invoices</li>
</ul>

<p>One test that tells you everything about a software's mobile quality: can a tech in a customer's driveway create a job, add parts, take photos, and collect payment without calling the office? Walk through that exact workflow during the trial. If it takes more than 5 minutes to complete, the software will slow your techs down every single day.</p>

<p>If you want to see how dispatch tools overlap with invoicing features in this trade, the <a href="/guides/best-garage-door-invoice-software-2026">best garage door invoice software guide for 2026</a> covers the same platforms. Most of the same names show up in both lists, which tells you how few tools are actually built with this trade in mind.</p>

<h2>Top 6 Garage Door Dispatch Software Picks for 2026</h2>

<p><strong>Workiz leads for most garage door operations, with Jobber right behind it for small shops and Housecall Pro for growing teams. ServiceTitan is built for scale, not for the average garage door company. Service Fusion earns its spot with flat-rate unlimited-user pricing. Kickserv is the budget option that handles the basics without embarrassing you in front of customers.</strong></p>

<h3>1. Workiz - Best Overall for Garage Door Dispatch</h3>

<p>Workiz was designed for high-volume service businesses, and it shows in the dispatch workflow. The board is fast, the GPS map is genuinely useful, and the automatic job-from-call feature is the single best thing about it for this trade. When a customer calls, the software creates a job lead, pulls up their address history, and queues it for dispatch. Your office person isn't manually typing anything.</p>

<p>GPS tracking shows tech locations live on the dispatch map. You can see who's 10 minutes from wrapping up, adjust the queue in real time, and give customers an accurate ETA without picking up the phone. That visibility alone cuts the "where is my tech" calls down significantly.</p>

<p>The mobile app holds up well in practice. Techs can view job details, add parts, photograph before and after, and collect payment before leaving the driveway. It works in low-signal areas, which matters if you cover rural or semi-rural routes. Techs who've used both Workiz and Jobber consistently say Workiz feels faster for back-to-back jobs.</p>

<p>Pricing in 2026: Starter is $65/month for 2 users, Team is $169/month for up to 5 users, Business is $299/month for up to 10. QuickBooks Online integration is available on all paid plans. The 7-day free trial actually lets you test the dispatch board with real data, not just a canned walkthrough.</p>

<p>The weakness: reporting isn't as deep as ServiceTitan, and multi-location or franchise management hits the platform's ceiling faster than you'd like. For a single-location shop with 2-8 techs, it's the best fit on this list.</p>

<h3>2. Jobber - Best for Small Garage Door Operations</h3>

<p>If you're running solo or with one helper, Jobber makes sense at $49/month for the Core plan (1 user) or $129/month for Connect (up to 5 users). The <a href="/reviews/jobber-review-2026">full Jobber review</a> covers the details, but for dispatch specifically: the scheduling tools are solid and the calendar is intuitive. It's just not as fast as Workiz on busy days when calls are stacking up.</p>

<p>QuickBooks integration works reliably. Online booking is built in. Customer texts and emails are automatic. Where Jobber falls short for garage door is the phone call workflow. You're creating jobs manually from each call rather than having them created automatically. At 6-8 calls a day, that's manageable. At 15-plus, it creates real friction.</p>

<p>For a solo operator doing 6-10 calls a day with no dedicated office staff, Jobber handles the load without feeling bloated. Once you're at 3 trucks and the volume picks up, Workiz is worth the extra cost.</p>

<h3>3. Housecall Pro - Best for Growing Teams</h3>

<p>Housecall Pro sits between Jobber's simplicity and ServiceTitan's complexity. The dispatch board is strong, the customer-facing features are mature (automated review requests, online booking, payment links), and the reporting is useful once you get past initial setup.</p>

<p>Pricing: Basic is $65/month for 1 user, Essentials is $169/month for up to 5 users, MAX is custom-priced for larger teams. The <a href="/reviews/housecall-pro-review-2026">Housecall Pro review</a> goes deeper on where the QuickBooks integration gets complicated, and it does get complicated with certain workflows. Test it specifically before you go live, not after.</p>

<p>The mobile app is one of the best on this list and consistently rates well from techs who are not especially tech-oriented. That matters when your hiring pool is skilled tradespeople, not software users. If you're between 5 and 15 techs and customer communication quality is a priority, Housecall Pro at the Essentials tier earns its price. Below 5 users, the per-seat cost against Workiz is harder to justify.</p>

<h3>4. ServiceTitan - Best for Large Multi-Truck Operations</h3>

<p>ServiceTitan is the most capable and most expensive tool on this list. There's no public pricing because it's negotiated by contract. The general range runs $125 to $350 per user per month, with annual minimums and typical 2-year contracts. For a 2-truck garage door shop, it will not pay for itself in any reasonable timeframe.</p>

<p>At 10-plus trucks, or with multiple locations, the depth of reporting, automated marketing, and technician performance tracking starts to justify the spend. The dispatch board is excellent. The mobile app is powerful but complex enough that you'll need real onboarding time before techs are comfortable with it. Read the <a href="/reviews/servicetitan-review-2026">ServiceTitan review</a> before you book a demo. The sales process is aggressive and the implementation is a months-long project, not an afternoon of setup.</p>

<h3>5. Service Fusion - Best Flat-Rate Value</h3>

<p>Service Fusion's pitch is simple: flat pricing, unlimited users. The Starter plan is $195/month for your entire team. A shop with 6 techs paying $169/month for just 5 users on Housecall Pro is already spending more per person. The math at Service Fusion gets better every time you add a truck.</p>

<p>The tradeoff: the dispatch board and mobile app are functional but not as polished as Workiz or Housecall Pro. The mobile app gets mixed reviews from techs who've come from better-designed platforms. QuickBooks integration is reliable and has been in the market long enough to be well-tested. Pricing: Starter $195/month, Plus $295/month, Pro $495/month. All tiers include unlimited users. If cost predictability as you scale matters more than slick UX, Service Fusion is worth a demo.</p>

<h3>6. Kickserv - Budget Pick for Solo Operators</h3>

<p>Kickserv doesn't try to compete with Workiz on dispatch speed, and it's priced accordingly. The Lite plan is $59/month for up to 5 users. Standard is $99/month for up to 10. There's even a free plan for 2 users if you're just getting off paper records.</p>

<p>You won't get GPS tracking or auto-job-from-call at any Kickserv tier. What you do get is clean scheduling, invoicing, QuickBooks integration, and a mobile app that works reliably. For a solo operator who needs basic scheduling and invoicing without extra complexity, Kickserv is honest about what it is. Don't expect it to handle 15 calls on a storm day without adding friction to your morning.</p>

<h2>How to Choose the Right Dispatch Software for Your Shop</h2>

<p><strong>Match the software to your current call volume and truck count, not to your aspirations. A 2-truck shop buying ServiceTitan for its feature list is the same logic as buying a 3/4-ton truck to haul a lawnmower. Identify the specific dispatch problem costing you the most time each day and find the tool that solves it at the lowest complexity.</strong></p>

<p>Some scenarios worth walking through before you commit to a trial:</p>

<p>If you're solo and doing 6-10 calls a day, Kickserv or Jobber handles your volume without waste. The dispatch complexity in Workiz doesn't pay off until you have a second tech to coordinate in real time.</p>

<p>If you're at 3-5 trucks and your office person spends 30 minutes every morning calling techs to figure out where everyone is, Workiz solves that problem directly. The GPS dispatch map and auto-job creation pay for the difference in price within a week of use.</p>

<p>If you have 6-plus techs and the per-user pricing on Workiz or Housecall Pro is climbing, Service Fusion's unlimited-user flat rate is worth a serious look. The interface is less polished, but the economics work at that headcount in a way the per-seat tools don't.</p>

<p>If you're managing multiple locations, running a franchise model, or need financial reporting tied to individual technician performance metrics, ServiceTitan is the right tool despite the cost and the implementation timeline. Nothing else on this list matches it at that scale.</p>

<h2>Frequently Asked Questions</h2>
<h3>Q: How much does garage door dispatch software typically cost per month?</h3>
<p>Most garage door-focused dispatch tools range from $49–$65/month for small operations, with Jobber at $49/month for solo operators and Workiz at $65/month for shops handling moderate call volume. Platforms like ServiceTitan cost more but typically only justify the investment once you're managing 8 or more service trucks with complex billing and reporting needs.</p>
<h3>Q: Can garage door dispatch software create jobs directly from inbound phone calls?</h3>
<p>Yes—top platforms auto-create jobs from incoming calls, eliminating manual data entry every time a customer calls. This is essential for high-call-volume garage door work, where your office team can drag a technician to a map, see their GPS location, and send a confirmation text within a minute of hanging up.</p>
<h3>Q: What happens when a customer calls for the second garage door repair at the same address?</h3>
<p>Quality garage door software stores customer history at the address level, not just by name, so your team instantly sees that "123 Oak St" had a spring replacement 14 months ago. This prevents wasted diagnostics, speeds up repairs, and surfaces opportunities to recommend preventive maintenance like cable inspections.</p>
<h3>Q: Can a technician finish a garage door job without calling the office?</h3>
<p>The best garage door apps let technicians complete the entire job workflow on-site—adding parts used, uploading photos, and collecting payment—without returning to the office or making a follow-up call. If the mobile experience takes more than 5 minutes to complete a job, it defeats the purpose for high-volume garage door dispatch.</p>
