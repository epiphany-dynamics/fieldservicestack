<p><strong>The best garage door invoice software in 2026 is Jobber ($49-$249/mo) for most small-to-mid-size operations, with Housecall Pro ($79-$189/mo) as a strong second for teams that do everything from their phones. Solo operators on a tight budget can start with Invoice Ninja's free tier and collect card payments with zero monthly fees.</strong></p>

<h2>What to Look For in Garage Door Invoice Software</h2>

<p><strong>Garage door invoicing has specific demands other trades don't: parts-heavy line items (springs, cables, openers, panels), frequent same-day job completion, on-site payment collection, and photo documentation for warranty work. Any platform you pick needs to handle all of that without making your tech tap through five screens to send a $300 invoice.</strong></p>

<p>Garage door work moves fast. A torsion spring replacement is a 45-minute job and the customer expects to pay before you pack up. If your invoicing software takes longer to navigate than the job took, you've already got a problem.</p>

<p>Here's what actually matters when you're evaluating these platforms:</p>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Mobile invoicing speed</strong> - Can your tech send an invoice from the driveway in under two minutes?</li>
  <li><strong>Parts catalog / price book</strong> - Pre-loaded line items for common parts cut invoice time and kill inconsistent pricing</li>
  <li><strong>On-site payment collection</strong> - Credit card, ACH, and digital wallet support with processing fees at 2.9% or lower</li>
  <li><strong>Photo attachment</strong> - Before/after photos attached to the job record for warranty and insurance documentation</li>
  <li><strong>QuickBooks or accounting sync</strong> - Non-negotiable if you're not doing your books by hand</li>
  <li><strong>Scheduling and dispatch</strong> - Most garage door businesses don't need a separate scheduling tool if the FSM platform handles it</li>
</ul>

<p>If you're running a two-truck crew taking 10+ calls a day, scheduling becomes just as important as invoicing. The <a href="/guides/scheduling-software-for-service-technicians-2026">best scheduling software for service technicians in 2026</a> goes deeper on that side of the equation if dispatch is your bigger headache right now.</p>

<h2>Top Garage Door Invoice Software for 2026</h2>

<p><strong>Jobber leads for most garage door businesses because it balances invoicing, scheduling, and client communication at a price small teams can actually afford. Housecall Pro wins on mobile experience. Workiz is worth a look once you're scaling past five techs. ServiceTitan is overkill unless you're running a serious multi-location operation.</strong></p>

<h3>1. Jobber - Best Overall for 1-10 Techs</h3>

<p>Jobber is the platform that comes up most in garage door and home service conversations, and it's earned the reputation. The price book feature lets you load your common parts and labor codes once. After that, your tech builds an invoice by selecting items instead of typing prices from memory. That alone eliminates a lot of the inconsistent pricing that shows up when you have two techs quoting the same job differently.</p>

<p>The Core plan at $49/month covers one user and handles quoting, invoicing, and payment collection. Connect ($129/month) adds online booking, automated follow-up messages, and a client hub where customers can view and pay invoices. Grow ($249/month) adds referral tracking and lead management. Most small operations can live in Core or Connect and never need the third tier.</p>

<p>QuickBooks Online sync is available at the Connect tier and above. At Core, you're doing manual exports. That's a real limitation if you're already using QuickBooks and want the connection to be automatic. Worth knowing before you sign up for the entry plan.</p>

<p>The mobile app is solid without being flashy. It does the job. For a full breakdown of the platform, the <a href="/reviews/jobber-review-2026">Jobber review for 2026</a> covers features and limitations in detail.</p>

<p><strong>Starting price:</strong> $49/mo (Core), $129/mo (Connect), $249/mo (Grow)</p>
<p><strong>QuickBooks sync:</strong> Connect and above</p>
<p><strong>Card processing fee:</strong> 2.9% + $0.30 per transaction</p>

<h3>2. Housecall Pro - Best Mobile Experience</h3>

<p>If your techs are sending invoices from a phone rather than a tablet, Housecall Pro edges out Jobber on pure mobile usability. The interface is cleaner on a small screen and payment collection takes fewer taps. That matters when you're standing in someone's unlit garage at 7pm trying to close out the job.</p>

<p>Pricing starts at $79/month for one user (Basic) and jumps to $189/month for up to five users (Essentials). The MAX tier is custom-priced for larger teams. The Basic plan includes invoicing, payment processing, scheduling, and a customer portal. QuickBooks Online sync comes included at all paid tiers, which is a better deal than Jobber's setup.</p>

<p>The downside is price. At $79/month for one user, you're paying $30/month more than Jobber's entry tier. If you're a solo tech with straightforward invoicing needs, that adds up to $360 a year for features you might not use. Check the <a href="/reviews/housecall-pro-pricing-2026">Housecall Pro pricing breakdown</a> before committing, because the advertised rates don't always reflect what you actually pay after add-ons and payment processing fees.</p>

<p><strong>Starting price:</strong> $79/mo (Basic), $189/mo (Essentials), custom (MAX)</p>
<p><strong>QuickBooks sync:</strong> All paid tiers</p>
<p><strong>Card processing fee:</strong> 2.69% per transaction</p>

<h3>3. Workiz - Best for Growing Multi-Tech Operations</h3>

<p>Workiz gets less attention than Jobber or Housecall Pro but it handles garage door operations well, especially once you're past two or three techs. The built-in VoIP phone system is the real differentiator. Workiz logs calls, auto-creates jobs from inbound calls, and tracks missed calls alongside your other job data. For a busy garage door company fielding 20+ calls a day, that's genuinely useful and not something the other platforms do natively.</p>

<p>Invoicing is clean and the price book works similarly to Jobber's. Where Workiz lags is the learning curve. The platform has more options and the initial setup takes longer. It's not the right tool for someone who wants to get invoicing running in an afternoon.</p>

<p>The Starter plan at $65/month (one user) is competitive. The Team plan at $169/month covers up to three users and unlocks QuickBooks sync. A detailed side-by-side is in the <a href="/comparisons/workiz-vs-jobber-2026">Workiz vs Jobber comparison</a> if you're already deciding between the two.</p>

<p><strong>Starting price:</strong> $65/mo (Starter), $169/mo (Team), $299/mo (Business)</p>
<p><strong>QuickBooks sync:</strong> Team and above</p>
<p><strong>Card processing fee:</strong> 2.9% per transaction</p>

<h3>4. FieldEdge - Best for Parts-Heavy Invoicing</h3>

<p>FieldEdge is built for trades that do a lot of parts tracking, which makes it a reasonable fit for garage door work. The inventory management is tighter than what you get in Jobber or Housecall Pro, and the flat-rate pricing integration is useful if you've built out a rate card for common repairs.</p>

<p>The downside is transparency. FieldEdge doesn't publish rates publicly. You have to request a demo to get a quote, and what contractors typically report puts it in the $100-$150/month per user range. That's expensive compared to the alternatives, and the interface feels older than it should for what you're paying. It makes the most sense for larger operations where the advanced inventory features actually get used.</p>

<p><strong>Starting price:</strong> Custom (typically $100-$150/user/mo)</p>
<p><strong>QuickBooks sync:</strong> Yes</p>
<p><strong>Card processing fee:</strong> Varies by plan</p>

<h3>5. ServiceTitan - Best for Enterprise Operations</h3>

<p>ServiceTitan is a serious platform and it's priced accordingly. You're looking at $398/month and up, often with implementation fees layered on top. For a solo tech or two-truck crew, that number makes no sense. For a garage door company running eight or more techs across multiple markets, the reporting, dispatch board, and revenue tracking tools start to justify the cost.</p>

<p>The invoicing is excellent. The dispatch board is the best in the industry. The learning curve is steep and onboarding takes weeks, not days. The platform also has a reputation for locking customers into long contracts, so read the terms carefully before you sign anything.</p>

<p><strong>Starting price:</strong> $398/mo+, custom contract</p>
<p><strong>QuickBooks sync:</strong> Yes</p>
<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Multi-location operations with 8+ techs</p>

<h3>6. Invoice Ninja - Best Budget Option for Solo Operators</h3>

<p>Invoice Ninja is free for up to 20 clients and handles basic invoicing, estimates, and payment collection through Stripe or PayPal. It's not a field service management platform. There's no scheduling, no dispatch, no job photo workflow. But if you're a solo operator who just needs to send professional invoices and collect payments without paying $50-$80/month for a full FSM platform, it works and it works well enough.</p>

<p>The Pro plan at $12/month removes client limits and adds custom branding. The Enterprise plan at $20/month adds multi-user access. For a one-person operation that handles scheduling in a Google Calendar and just needs clean, branded invoices, this is worth knowing about before you commit to a more expensive platform you don't fully need yet.</p>

<p><strong>Starting price:</strong> Free (up to 20 clients), $12/mo (Pro), $20/mo (Enterprise)</p>
<p><strong>QuickBooks sync:</strong> Limited (manual export only)</p>
<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Solo operators, tight budgets, invoice-only use cases</p>

<h2>How to Choose the Right Software</h2>

<p><strong>The size of your operation determines most of this decision. Solo operators and very small crews should start with Jobber Core or Invoice Ninja. Teams of 3-7 techs get the most from Jobber Connect or Housecall Pro Essentials. Once you're running 8 or more technicians across multiple service areas, it's worth having a ServiceTitan conversation, contracts and all.</strong></p>

<p>If you're a solo operator doing 6-8 jobs a day, the priority is speed. You need to get paid the same day, ideally before you pull out of the driveway. Jobber Core or Housecall Pro Basic both cover this. The $30/month difference between them is real over a year, but Housecall Pro's better mobile experience might be worth it if you're doing everything from your phone.</p>

<p>If you're running a 3-truck crew, the math changes. Your techs need to build invoices without calling you to ask what to charge for a cable drum replacement. A price book with pre-loaded parts is not optional at this point. Both Jobber Connect and Housecall Pro Essentials cover it. Workiz earns a spot in your evaluation at this stage because the call tracking gets more useful as your inbound volume grows.</p>

<p>If accounting integration is the thing keeping you up at night, Housecall Pro wins on QuickBooks availability at the entry tier. With Jobber, you're paying for the $129 Connect plan before you get automatic sync. That's an $80/month difference worth factoring in before you commit.</p>

<p>The <a href="/guides/field-service-management-buyers-guide">field service management software buyer's guide</a> covers the full buying process if you want to think through criteria beyond invoicing, like fleet tracking, customer communications, and reporting.</p>

<h2>Our Recommendation</h2>

<p><strong>Start with Jobber if you have 1-5 techs and want a platform that grows with you. Start with Housecall Pro if your team works primarily from phones and QuickBooks sync from day one matters to you. Skip ServiceTitan until you're running at least 8 techs and have someone who can manage the onboarding without it eating your entire month.</strong></p>

<p>For most garage door businesses, Jobber is the honest answer. The pricing is reasonable, the feature set covers everything a typical operation needs, and the learning curve is short enough that a tech who's never used FSM software can figure it out in a day. The Connect plan at $129/month is where the platform starts to really deliver, with automatic QuickBooks sync, automated invoice follow-up reminders, and a client hub for self-service payments.</p>

<p>If $49-$129/month isn't the right move yet and you just need clean invoicing and card payment collection, Invoice Ninja's free tier handles 80% of the workflow for nothing. It's not a long-term solution, but it beats chasing payments over text message.</p>

<p>The one scenario where I'd push back on Jobber: if your business does a significant portion of commercial work with net-30 payment terms and complex accounts receivable, Jobber's AR management is adequate but not deep. In that case, Housecall Pro or FieldEdge handles the complexity better. For most residential garage door operations, though, you won't hit that ceiling.</p>

<p>Whatever you pick, use the free trial seriously. Most of these platforms offer 14 days. Load your price book, run a test job, and time how long it takes your tech to collect payment from start to finish. That test tells you more than any feature comparison chart.</p>
<h2>Frequently Asked Questions</h2>
<h3>Q: What payment processing fees should I expect when collecting payments on-site with garage door invoice software?</h3>
<p>Most platforms including Jobber and Housecall Pro charge 2.9% + $0.30 per card transaction, with ACH transfers typically 1-2% lower. Staying below 3% in total processing costs keeps your payment flow economical, especially when customers are paying $300-$500 invoices directly from the driveway.</p>

<h3>Q: How much time can garage door invoicing software actually save per job compared to manual invoices?</h3>
<p>Mobile invoice apps can generate and send a complete invoice from the job site in under 2 minutes, versus 30-45 minutes of manual data entry at the end of the day. For a 10-job day, that's 4-7 hours of office work eliminated—enough to pay for the software within your first month.</p>

<h3>Q: Is Jobber or Housecall Pro the better value for a solo garage door tech on a budget?</h3>
<p>Jobber's entry tier ($49/month) undercuts Housecall Pro ($79/month) by $360 annually, though Housecall Pro's mobile-first interface appeals to single operators who never return to the office. The 30-dollar monthly difference often justifies itself if your tech spends 100% of their day in the field.</p>

<h3>Q: Do garage door invoice platforms include pre-loaded parts catalogs, or do you build your own?</h3>
<p>Quality platforms like Jobber let you create customizable price books with springs, cables, openers, and panels pre-loaded with your costs and markups. Setup takes 1-2 hours initially, but eliminates manual line-item entry on every invoice and ensures pricing consistency across all jobs.</p>
