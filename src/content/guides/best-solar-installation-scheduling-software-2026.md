<p><strong>Solar installation scheduling software needs to track multi-stage jobs, not just same-day dispatch. The best options in 2026: Jobber ($49-$249/month), ServiceTitan (custom, roughly $398+/month base), Scoop Solar (custom pricing), Housecall Pro ($65-$169/month), FieldPulse ($99/month base), and JobNimbus ($25+/user/month). For most solar crews under 10 techs, Jobber Connect at $149/month is the right starting point.</strong></p>

<h2>What Solar Scheduling Actually Requires</h2>

<p><strong>A standard solar install runs 5-8 stages from site survey to live system, spread over 4-12 weeks. Your scheduling software needs milestone tracking, permit document storage, automated customer status updates, crew and equipment coordination, and clean QuickBooks integration that handles material costs and permit fees without manual data entry.</strong></p>

<p>Solar is not a same-day trade. You sign a residential contract in February and the install crew shows up in April, after the permit cleared, the utility approved the interconnection agreement, and the panels arrived from the distributor. Most FSM tools are optimized for a plumber booking a leak repair and closing it out by 3pm. Using them for solar creates gaps.</p>

<p>The gaps that actually hurt: no permit status tracking, no document storage for utility paperwork, no automated communication during the 6-10 week waiting period, and invoicing that can't separate panel costs from inverter from mounting hardware from permit fees. Customers will call you every other week if you go silent. The right platform solves that without you building a system from scratch in Google Sheets.</p>

<p>If you want a solid baseline for what to evaluate in any field service scheduling platform before going solar-specific, the <a href="/guides/scheduling-software-for-service-technicians-2026">guide to scheduling software for service technicians</a> covers the core features worth checking before you commit to anything.</p>

<p>For solar specifically, here is what separates a platform that works from one that just technically runs:</p>

<ul>
<li>Multi-stage job tracking with actual milestone statuses, not just "scheduled" and "complete"</li>
<li>Mobile app with offline sync and fast work order close-out for crews who are on rooftops, not at desks</li>
<li>Automated customer communication that sends status updates when permits are submitted, approved, and when the install date is confirmed</li>
<li>Document storage for permits, HOA approval letters, interconnection agreements, and inspection sign-offs</li>
<li>QuickBooks integration that handles complex line items without requiring someone to re-enter everything by hand</li>
</ul>

<h2>The 6 Best Solar Installation Scheduling Software Tools for 2026</h2>

<p><strong>Jobber leads for small-to-mid crews at $49-$249/month, with the best mobile app in its price range and native QuickBooks sync. Scoop Solar is the only purpose-built option. ServiceTitan has the deepest feature set but prices accordingly. Housecall Pro works best for new companies. FieldPulse is the budget pick for solo operators. JobNimbus fits solar/roofing crossover businesses best.</strong></p>

<h3>1. Jobber: Best Overall for Small to Mid-Size Solar Crews</h3>

<p>Pricing: Core $49/month (1 user), Connect $149/month (up to 5 users), Grow $249/month (up to 15 users). All billed annually. Monthly billing runs higher on every tier.</p>

<p>For a 3-6 person solar crew doing 20-50 installs a month, Jobber is probably the right answer. Not because it is built for solar (it is not) but because the core platform is genuinely solid and the workarounds are manageable.</p>

<p>You build solar workflows using custom job fields and work order statuses. Tag jobs by permit stage, inspection result, or interconnection status. It takes an afternoon to configure and then it runs. The dispatch board is clean, and more importantly, the field team actually uses the app. I have talked to solar companies running platforms with better feature sets that fell apart because the install crew stopped opening the app after two weeks. Jobber avoids that problem. The mobile app sits at 4.7 stars on iOS and 4.6 on Android as of mid-2026. Offline sync works reliably. Photo capture ties directly to work orders without an extra step.</p>

<p>QuickBooks integration is native and bidirectional. Invoices, payments, and expense items move automatically. It is one of the cleaner integrations on this list and one less headache at month-end.</p>

<p>Weak spots: no native permit tracking, no HOA workflow, and no utility interconnection scheduling. At 60+ installs a month the custom field workarounds start feeling like real limitations. For a direct look at how Jobber stacks up against the enterprise alternative at scale, the <a href="/comparisons/servicetitan-vs-jobber-2026">ServiceTitan vs Jobber breakdown</a> lays out where each one pulls ahead.</p>

<h3>2. ServiceTitan: Best for Larger Solar Operations</h3>

<p>Pricing: Custom. Expect a base in the $298-$498+/month range with a contract. No public pricing page; you book a demo and get a quote. Add-ons push the real number higher.</p>

<p>ServiceTitan built a dedicated solar vertical and it shows in the workflow structure. Multi-stage job tracking is native here, not hacked together from generic fields. You can map every milestone from site survey to utility interconnection, set stage dependencies, assign tasks per stage, and pull dashboards showing exactly where jobs are bottlenecking across your entire pipeline.</p>

<p>The dispatch engine is enterprise-grade. GPS tracking, technician routing, real-time schedule updates, and automated customer messaging all live in one place. The reporting is the best on this list, which matters when you are running multiple crews and need to understand cost-per-install trends over time.</p>

<p>For a solar company with 5+ crews and 80+ installs a month, ServiceTitan starts making sense. Below that volume, the price and the 4-6 week implementation timeline are genuinely hard to justify. This is not a platform you set up over a weekend. The <a href="/reviews/servicetitan-review-2026">full ServiceTitan review</a> covers the pricing structure and contract terms in more detail. Read it before you book the demo, not after.</p>

<p>QuickBooks integration is available, but ServiceTitan also has its own accounting module that many larger operations use instead. Verify the integration behavior during the demo if QuickBooks is non-negotiable for your bookkeeper.</p>

<h3>3. Scoop Solar: Best Purpose-Built Solar Platform</h3>

<p>Pricing: Custom. Contact for a quote. Mid-market range; pricing is typically comparable to ServiceTitan for similar feature sets.</p>

<p>Scoop Solar is the only platform on this list designed specifically for solar installation. Permit tracking is built in from the ground up, not bolted on from a generic field. You can track permit application status by jurisdiction, attach documents, set follow-up reminders, and pull reports on approval timelines across multiple counties. Same for utility interconnection. If you work across several utilities with different interconnection timelines and paperwork requirements, that is genuinely useful in a way that Jobber's custom fields cannot match.</p>

<p>Crew scheduling and equipment staging share a single view. You can see the panel delivery arriving Tuesday but the install crew not scheduled until Wednesday and fix it on the spot, not after a frustrated homeowner calls about panels sitting in their driveway.</p>

<p>The solar sales cycle is also baked into the platform. Lead tracking, proposal generation, and project handoff are connected, so the data the sales rep captured during the site assessment follows the job into production without a manual transfer step that creates errors.</p>

<p>Weak spots: if your company does any work outside solar (electrical, HVAC, roofing), Scoop Solar covers the solar side well and not much else. Companies with mixed service lines may end up running two platforms, which creates its own scheduling and billing complications.</p>

<h3>4. Housecall Pro: Best for New Solar Companies</h3>

<p>Pricing: Basic $65/month (1 user), Essentials $169/month (up to 5 users), MAX is custom pricing. All billed annually.</p>

<p>Housecall Pro is not built for solar. It is built for same-day service businesses. But it is the fastest platform to get running, and for a new solar company doing 10-20 installs a month, that ease of onboarding matters more than having permit tracking dashboards you will not use yet anyway.</p>

<p>The customer communication tools are strong out of the box. Automated appointment reminders and post-install review requests work without configuration. That automated review ask after a successful install pays for the subscription in referrals, especially in the first two years when your business lives and dies on word-of-mouth.</p>

<p>QuickBooks integration is native and clean. The mobile app is solid. For a full picture of where this platform genuinely helps and where it runs out of steam, the <a href="/guides/housecall-pro-pros-and-cons-2026">Housecall Pro pros and cons guide</a> covers the gaps honestly.</p>

<p>Weak spots: no milestone tracking, no permit workflow, and the scheduling features top out faster than you would expect as job volume grows. Most solar companies that start on Housecall Pro migrate to Jobber or ServiceTitan within 18-24 months. That is not a failure, but factor the migration time into the decision.</p>

<h3>5. FieldPulse: Best Budget Option for Small Teams</h3>

<p>Pricing: $99/month base plan, $20/user/month for additional users. No per-job fees.</p>

<p>FieldPulse does not have Jobber's name recognition, but the flat per-user pricing is predictable and the feature set covers the basics for a 2-3 person solar operation without surprises at billing time. Custom job statuses let you build a workable solar workflow. Scheduling, invoicing, customer communication, and basic reporting are all covered without a lot of overhead.</p>

<p>For a solo solar installer who does not want to pay Jobber or Housecall Pro prices, this is a legitimate option. QuickBooks integration is bidirectional and works reliably. Mobile app is functional, not impressive.</p>

<p>Weak spots: no CRM for managing longer solar sales cycles, limited document storage, no permit tracking. The platform tops out faster than Jobber when job complexity or crew size increases. It is a good starting point, not a long-term home for a growing solar company.</p>

<h3>6. JobNimbus: Best for Solar/Roofing Crossover Companies</h3>

<p>Pricing: Starter plans around $25/user/month. Pro and Business tiers run higher. Annual billing discounted.</p>

<p>A significant number of solar companies also do roofing work, or at minimum coordinate closely with roofing crews for penetration prep and underlayment before an install. JobNimbus started in the roofing software space and added solar features, which makes it a natural fit for any operation that regularly touches both trades under one roof.</p>

<p>The project pipeline structure is strong. You build detailed job stages with task checklists and required documents at each step, which gives you more structure than Jobber's custom fields without ServiceTitan's price tag. The proposal tools are also better than most FSM platforms on this list, which matters during the solar sales process when you are competing against three other quotes and need a clean, professional output without switching to a separate tool.</p>

<p>QuickBooks integration is available and works well. Mobile app is reliable without being exceptional.</p>

<p>Weak spots: not as strong on real-time crew scheduling and dispatch as Jobber or Housecall Pro. If day-of coordination and live dispatch tracking are your primary pain points, JobNimbus is not the first choice.</p>

<h2>How to Choose the Right Solar Scheduling Software</h2>

<p><strong>Match the software to your install volume and business type. Under 20 installs/month: Housecall Pro or FieldPulse. At 20-60 installs/month: Jobber Connect at $149/month. Solar/roofing crossover: JobNimbus. Dedicated solar-only at real volume: Scoop Solar. Over 60 installs/month with multiple crews: ServiceTitan. Do not buy features you will not need for six months.</strong></p>

<p>The biggest mistake solar companies make when picking scheduling software is evaluating by feature count. More features is not better if your four-person crew spends extra time navigating menus instead of running installs. Start with the simplest tool that handles your actual workflow today, and upgrade when you hit a real wall, not a hypothetical one.</p>

<p>A few specific scenarios worth naming directly:</p>

<ul>
<li>Solo solar installer doing 8-15 jobs a month: FieldPulse base plan or Jobber Core. You need scheduling and invoicing, not enterprise dispatch dashboards.</li>
<li>4-truck crew doing 30-50 installs a month, biggest problems are permit tracking and "where's my install" calls from customers: Jobber Connect with custom fields configured for job stage tracking. Not a perfect solar fit, but a functional one.</li>
<li>Running solar and roofing under one company: JobNimbus. The workflow crossover support is genuinely useful and you will not find it elsewhere at a comparable price point.</li>
<li>Scaling past 60 installs a month and starting to lose jobs in the gaps: demo both ServiceTitan and Scoop Solar. ServiceTitan fits better if you have a mixed service business. Scoop Solar fits better if solar is your only trade.</li>
</ul>

<p>One thing that gets skipped in almost every software evaluation: test the mobile app with the person who will actually use it in the field before you sign anything. Not the office manager. Not you. The tech who will be 20 feet up on a roof trying to close out a work order with one hand. If the app does not pass that test, nothing else on the spec sheet matters.</p>

<h2>Frequently Asked Questions</h2>
<h3>Q: How much should you spend on solar scheduling software for a small crew?</h3>
<p>Jobber and Housecall Pro work for 2-10 person teams at $49-$169/month, with Jobber Connect adding full multi-stage tracking at $149/month. For crews under 15 techs with straightforward permit workflows, these general tools save $300+ monthly compared to solar-specific platforms like Scoop Solar ($398+), making them the practical choice for most residential solar installers.</p>

<h3>Q: How do solar scheduling platforms handle the 6-10 week waiting period between contract and install?</h3>
<p>The best tools automate customer status updates tied to real milestones—when permits clear, utility approves interconnection, or panels arrive from the distributor, the system sends notifications without manual intervention. This eliminates the "customer calls every two weeks" problem by keeping stakeholders informed during the inactive scheduling gap.</p>

<h3>Q: What permit and document features do solar crews actually need in scheduling software?</h3>
<p>Beyond photos, solar platforms should store utility interconnection agreements, city permits, and approval letters timestamped to specific job stages in one searchable place rather than scattered across email and Google Drive. This prevents the lost-document crisis that delays installs and ensures permit compliance records exist when utility or lender inspections happen.</p>

<h3>Q: Can you schedule a solar install 8 weeks out when panels haven't arrived yet?</h3>
<p>Yes—the best platforms let you schedule crew assignments by stage (survey → permit pending → equipment arrival → install) with conditional logic that reschedules the install date when equipment actually arrives, preventing the common mistake of booking crews before permits clear or panels ship.</p>
