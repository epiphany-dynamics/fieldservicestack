<p><strong>ServiceTitan customer reviews in 2026 average 4.3 stars on G2 and Capterra, but that number papers over very different experiences. Shops running 10 or more techs rate it near the top of any FSM list. Smaller contractors call it overpriced and overly complex. Pricing starts around $398/month with implementation fees that commonly run $5,000-$15,000 and 2-year contracts standard.</strong></p>

<h2>What ServiceTitan Customers Actually Say in 2026</h2>

<p><strong>Positive reviews cluster around the dispatch board, marketing ROI reporting, and service agreement management. Negative reviews almost always mention three things: cost, the 2-year contract, and onboarding that drags on for months. The software itself earns genuine praise. The sales experience and post-sale support get picked apart regularly.</strong></p>

<p>Pull up G2 or Capterra and filter ServiceTitan reviews to one and two stars. The complaints are nearly identical across reviewers: "Cost more than quoted." "Implementation took four months and we felt abandoned after signing." "Customer support is hard to reach when something breaks." None of that means the platform is bad software. It means it's enterprise software sold aggressively to shops that weren't ready for it.</p>

<p>The positive reviews tell a different story. Multi-location HVAC and plumbing operations talk about finally having visibility into which technicians are converting calls into big jobs, which marketing channels are generating actual revenue (not just call volume), and what their service agreement renewal rates look like month over month. That's real operational intelligence. If you're running a $3M HVAC shop and that data is currently trapped in someone's head or scattered across spreadsheets, ServiceTitan can genuinely change how you run the business.</p>

<p>The sticking point is getting there. Customers regularly report paying $10,000-$15,000 in onboarding fees before scheduling their first job in the system. Add the monthly software cost and you're well past $20,000 in year one before you see a dollar of ROI. For a 15-truck operation doing serious revenue, the math eventually works out. For a 3-truck crew trying to stop losing track of invoices, there are better places to start.</p>

<p>Our full <a href="/reviews/servicetitan-review-2026">ServiceTitan Review 2026</a> goes deeper into the platform itself and who it actually makes sense for beyond what customer reviews alone can tell you.</p>

<h2>What to Look for Before You Sign Anything</h2>

<p><strong>Before comparing platforms, get clear on your non-negotiables: mobile app quality for your techs, reliable QuickBooks sync, and contract terms. Any platform requiring a 2-year commitment deserves hard questions before you sign. Most shops that regret their FSM purchase didn't ask the right questions upfront and got sold something that fit the salesperson's quota, not their operation.</strong></p>

<p>Here is what actually separates a good FSM decision from an expensive one.</p>

<ul>
<li><strong>Mobile app quality:</strong> Your techs live in this thing all day. If it's slow or confusing, they'll go back to texting you from the job site. Test it on an actual Android device in a demo, not just a polished iPhone walkthrough in a sales call.</li>
<li><strong>Dispatch and scheduling:</strong> A demo board always looks clean. Run them through real scenarios: tech calls out sick mid-route, customer wants to move their appointment three times, two jobs land in the same neighborhood. Watch how the board actually handles it.</li>
<li><strong>QuickBooks integration:</strong> "We integrate with QuickBooks" is almost meaningless on its own. Ask specifically how invoices sync, whether customer records stay in sync bidirectionally, and how payments flow. Get a specific answer, not a sales pitch.</li>
<li><strong>Pricing transparency:</strong> If you have to talk to sales before getting a number, expect the number to be higher than you want. Get everything in writing before you get attached: monthly fee, per-tech fees, implementation, training, and early cancellation terms.</li>
<li><strong>Contract length:</strong> Month-to-month costs more per month but protects you while you figure out if the platform actually fits. Two-year contracts require real confidence. Smaller shops should think hard before locking in.</li>
</ul>

<h2>Top FSM Platforms in 2026: Honest Assessments</h2>

<p><strong>ServiceTitan sits at the top for large shops but costs 3-5x more than competitors like Jobber and Housecall Pro. Shops under 10 techs get 75-80% of the functionality from those alternatives at a fraction of the price. Service Fusion and FieldEdge fill the middle for operations that need more depth without enterprise-level overhead.</strong></p>

<h3>1. ServiceTitan</h3>

<p>Starting price around $398/month for the Starter plan (billed annually). Enterprise tiers climb past $600/month. Implementation typically runs $5,000-$15,000. Two-year contracts are standard, not optional.</p>

<p>The platform earns its price for the right shop. The dispatch board is the best available in any FSM software. Marketing attribution reporting shows you exactly which campaigns generate revenue, not just calls. CSR scripting and call recording are built for shops with dedicated office staff. Service agreements, equipment history, and preventive maintenance scheduling all work cleanly together.</p>

<p>If you're debating whether the jump from a simpler platform is worth it, the <a href="/comparisons/servicetitan-vs-jobber-2026">ServiceTitan vs Jobber 2026 comparison</a> lays out the exact tradeoffs by company size and makes the decision a lot easier.</p>

<p><em>Best for: HVAC, plumbing, and electrical operations with 10 or more techs and at least $1M in annual revenue who are ready to invest in proper onboarding and have office staff to actually run the platform.</em></p>

<h3>2. Jobber</h3>

<p>Pricing: Core at $49/month (1 user), Connect at $149/month (up to 7 users), Grow at $299/month (unlimited users), all billed annually. No implementation fee. Most shops are up and running in a day or two, not months.</p>

<p>Jobber is where a lot of former ServiceTitan customers end up, and most of them don't look back. The mobile app is clean. QuickBooks sync works the way it's supposed to. The client hub where customers can approve quotes and pay invoices online has genuinely moved the needle for smaller contractors chasing down payments. Customer reviews consistently call out how fast new hires get up to speed on it.</p>

<p>The ceiling is real. Service agreement depth, advanced reporting, and call center tools aren't in Jobber's wheelhouse. But that ceiling is higher than most 5-truck shops will hit in the first few years. The <a href="/reviews/jobber-review-2026">Jobber Review 2026</a> covers exactly where the platform runs out of runway and what triggers a shop to outgrow it.</p>

<p><em>Best for: Solo operators up to about 10 techs who want something that works without a 6-month setup project.</em></p>

<h3>3. Housecall Pro</h3>

<p>Pricing: Basic at $59/month (1 user), Essentials at $149/month (up to 5 users), Max at $254/month (up to 8 users), billed annually. Add-ons for phone features and financing stack on top of base pricing.</p>

<p>Housecall Pro has put real work into the residential service market. The automated customer follow-up sequences, review request automation, and flat-rate pricing tool are all genuinely useful in a day-to-day shop. Dispatch is clean. The customer-facing experience looks polished compared to older platforms in this price range.</p>

<p>The consistent complaints in customer reviews point to support quality that varies depending on who picks up, and pricing that climbs faster than expected once you start layering in add-ons. Full pricing details are in the <a href="/reviews/housecall-pro-review-2026">Housecall Pro Review 2026</a>.</p>

<p><em>Best for: Residential service companies with 2-8 techs, including HVAC, plumbing, cleaning, and pest control, who want polished customer communication and automated review requests without ServiceTitan's overhead.</em></p>

<h3>4. Service Fusion</h3>

<p>Pricing: Starter at $195/month, Plus at $295/month, Pro at $495/month. No per-user fees on any plan. That last detail matters more than it sounds once your headcount grows.</p>

<p>Service Fusion is underrated in the mid-market conversation. The no-per-user pricing becomes a real financial advantage past 5-6 people. QuickBooks integration is solid. The flat-rate pricing tool works. Dispatch gets the job done without a lot of flash.</p>

<p>The mobile app lags behind Jobber and Housecall Pro in polish, and customer support response times get called out in reviews more than they should. But the platform is more capable than its reputation gives it credit for, particularly for shops in the 8-20 tech range that feel too big for Jobber and can't stomach ServiceTitan pricing.</p>

<p><em>Best for: Mid-size multi-trade shops looking for depth without per-user pricing as headcount grows.</em></p>

<h3>5. FieldEdge</h3>

<p>FieldEdge doesn't publish pricing publicly. Expect $100-$200 per user per month depending on your plan and how the negotiation goes. Built specifically for HVAC, plumbing, and electrical, and the trade focus shows in ways that matter.</p>

<p>The service agreement module and equipment tracking are better than most competitors at this price point. QuickBooks integration is native and clean. Long-term customers tend to stay put.</p>

<p>The interface looks dated compared to anything in the Jobber price range, and implementation takes time to get right. But for a trade-focused shop where equipment history and maintenance agreements drive a significant piece of revenue, it holds up under pressure.</p>

<p><em>Best for: Established HVAC and plumbing shops with 5-15 techs where service agreements and equipment tracking are central to the operation.</em></p>

<h2>How to Choose Based on Your Operation</h2>

<p><strong>Crew size and budget narrow the field fast. Under 5 trucks: Jobber or Housecall Pro. Five to fifteen trucks with trade-specific needs: Service Fusion or FieldEdge. Ten or more trucks with serious reporting and marketing needs: ServiceTitan is worth evaluating seriously, but only if you can absorb the implementation cost and timeline without it wrecking your cash flow.</strong></p>

<p>If you're running a 3-truck HVAC crew doing mostly residential service calls, Jobber at $149/month covers everything you need: scheduling, dispatch, invoicing, QuickBooks sync, customer communication. You do not need ServiceTitan right now.</p>

<p>If you're at 8 techs across plumbing and HVAC with service agreements making up 30% of your revenue, that's where Service Fusion or FieldEdge deserve a serious look. The reporting depth starts to matter. The flat-rate pricing tools save real time at scale. You're still not in ServiceTitan territory, and you shouldn't force it.</p>

<p>At 15 or more techs doing real volume, the conversation changes. ServiceTitan's marketing attribution and dispatch optimization can pay for themselves, but only if you have the office staff and discipline to actually use the data. If the platform sits there half-configured because nobody has time to learn it properly, you've just signed a very expensive contract for software that runs in the background while your team uses workarounds.</p>
