<p><strong>The best tree service invoice software in 2026 for most small to mid-size operations is Jobber, starting at $49/month with on-site quoting, crew dispatch, and mobile payment collection built in. SingleOps and Arborgold are purpose-built for tree care and worth the premium for businesses running plant health contracts or managing 10-plus employees. Housecall Pro ($79/month) is the easiest to onboard.</strong></p>

<h2>What Tree Service Businesses Actually Need From Invoice Software</h2>

<p><strong>Tree service invoicing needs go beyond a standard field service template. You need on-site photo quoting, custom line items for disposal and equipment time, crew dispatch across multiple active job sites, and mobile payment collection at job completion. If your crew does plant health care alongside removals, add pesticide application logging to that list. Most general FSM platforms cover the basics; tree-specific platforms handle the rest.</strong></p>

<p>Running a tree crew is nothing like running a plumbing route. Jobs are bigger, quotes happen in someone's backyard while you're staring up at a 70-foot oak, and the scope of work can shift once the climbers get up there. A branch that looks manageable from the ground might take twice the labor once someone's actually in the canopy. You need software that keeps up with that reality, not a basic invoice generator that treats tree removal like a $150 drain unclog.</p>

<p>A lot of tree companies are still emailing PDFs or writing invoices by hand and chasing checks. That works until you hit around 8-10 jobs a week, and then the admin starts eating your evenings. What you actually need is a system where a tech can quote the job on-site, the office can see it in real time, and the invoice goes out the moment the crew finishes. No paper trail, no "I'll email it to you later."</p>

<p>Here's what to look for when evaluating platforms:</p>

<ul>
<li>On-site quoting with photo documentation (before shots protect you on insurance and dispute claims)</li>
<li>Custom line items for stump grinding, debris hauling, wood chipping, and equipment time</li>
<li>Multi-crew scheduling across overlapping job sites</li>
<li>Recurring contract management for HOA accounts and annual maintenance customers</li>
<li>Mobile payment collection so you're not chasing checks for three weeks after the job</li>
<li>QuickBooks or Xero sync so your accountant isn't reconciling manually</li>
<li>GPS tracking for fleet verification and mileage logging</li>
</ul>

<p>If your business includes plant health care work, add pesticide application logging and material tracking to that list. Most general-purpose platforms leave those out entirely, which is exactly why tree-specific software exists. If you're still figuring out which tier of platform makes sense for your operation overall, the <a href="/guides/field-service-management-buyers-guide">field service management buyer's guide</a> is a solid starting point before you commit to anything trade-specific.</p>

<h2>Top 5 Tree Service Invoice Software Options for 2026</h2>

<p><strong>The top tree service invoice software options in 2026 are Jobber, SingleOps, Arborgold, Housecall Pro, and Service Fusion. Jobber and Housecall Pro work for most small operations. SingleOps and Arborgold cost more but are purpose-built for tree care, with plant health records, multi-year contract tools, and proposal builders that understand how tree companies actually price work.</strong></p>

<h3>1. Jobber: Best Overall for Small to Mid-Size Tree Crews</h3>

<p>Jobber is the most commonly recommended field service platform for small tree companies, and the reputation is earned. It covers the full job lifecycle: quoting, scheduling, dispatching, invoicing, and payment collection from one dashboard. The mobile app is one of the better ones in this category. A 2-person crew can quote a job on-site with photos attached, schedule the follow-up work, and send an invoice with a payment link before they pull out of the driveway.</p>

<ul>
<li>Core: $49/month (1 user, basic invoicing and scheduling)</li>
<li>Connect: $129/month (up to 5 users; adds two-way texting and automated customer follow-ups)</li>
<li>Grow: $249/month (up to 15 users; adds reporting, lead management, and quote follow-up automation)</li>
</ul>

<p>QuickBooks sync is available on Connect and Grow. If you're on Core, you're exporting CSVs or doing accounting manually. Workable for a solo operator, less so for a 3-truck crew. The client hub feature lets customers approve quotes and pay online, which matters when you're trying to close a $2,500 removal job without playing phone tag for four days. The customer clicks a link, approves the quote, and you get a notification to schedule. Clean, simple, and it actually gets used.</p>

<p>Where Jobber falls short for tree services: no native plant health records and no pesticide application logs. If you're doing any PHC work alongside removals, you'll need a separate log or a workaround. The Core plan is also genuinely underpowered for anything beyond solo operation. Two employees means Connect at $129/month is the realistic entry point, not $49/month. For a full breakdown of what each tier gets you, the <a href="/reviews/jobber-review-2026">Jobber review</a> covers all the feature tiers in detail.</p>

<h3>2. SingleOps: Best for Green Industry Specialists</h3>

<p>SingleOps is built specifically for the green industry, which includes tree care, lawn care, and landscaping. It's one of the few platforms that actually understands how tree companies work: seasonal pricing cycles, multi-service proposals with optional add-ons, recurring route management, and crew productivity tracking broken down by job type. General-purpose platforms force you to configure your way into that functionality. SingleOps ships it out of the box.</p>

<p>The proposal builder is its strongest feature. You can build tiered proposals where customers pick their service level and the price adjusts automatically. Something like: remove the two dead oaks for $X, or add stump grinding for $Y more. That kind of interactive quoting closes jobs faster than a flat PDF estimate because the customer can see exactly what they're getting at each price point.</p>

<p>Pricing is quote-based, which is a legitimate frustration when you're trying to budget without sitting through a sales demo. Based on publicly reported pricing from industry forums and user communities, most small operations land between $100-$200/month, with larger crews and more users pushing the cost higher. The setup process takes longer than Jobber or Housecall Pro. Budget 2-3 weeks for onboarding before you're fully operational. That investment makes sense if you're managing 40-plus active accounts across residential, commercial, and HOA segments. It's overkill for a 2-person crew doing 10 jobs a week.</p>

<h3>3. Arborgold: Best for Established Businesses With Heavy Recurring Work</h3>

<p>Arborgold has been around longer than any other option on this list and was built specifically for tree and lawn care businesses. Its differentiator is the CRM side: you can track a client's entire tree inventory, note conditions and hazard ratings per tree, and tie future work orders directly to individual tree records. For arborists running multi-year plant health care programs, that record-keeping is worth paying for. No other platform on this list offers it at a comparable depth.</p>

<p>Pricing starts around $200/month and increases with user count and add-ons. It's the most expensive option here, and the interface shows its age compared to Jobber or Housecall Pro. The mobile experience is functional but not polished. Field techs can access work orders and document completed jobs, but it's not as fast or intuitive as the Jobber app. Crews who aren't comfortable with technology will have a real learning curve, and onboarding typically takes 3-4 weeks.</p>

<p>Where Arborgold excels is in the back office: tracking recurring contracts across large account lists, scheduling seasonal work, and maintaining compliance documentation for certified arborist programs. Best suited for established operations, typically 5-plus years in business with a large base of recurring accounts, where specialized record-keeping justifies the cost and learning curve.</p>

<h3>4. Housecall Pro: Best for Simplicity and Fast Onboarding</h3>

<p>Housecall Pro is the easiest platform on this list to get running. The interface is clean, the mobile app is fast, and the payment collection tools are the best of any option here. If your main problem is that customers take three weeks to pay and your crew hates learning new software, Housecall Pro is worth a serious look.</p>

<ul>
<li>Basic: $79/month (1 user)</li>
<li>Essentials: $189/month (up to 5 users; adds automated follow-ups and recurring job scheduling)</li>
<li>MAX: custom pricing for larger operations</li>
</ul>

<p>The payment feature that justifies the subscription cost on its own: you can send a payment link via text message directly from the job site. The customer pays on their phone, money goes into your account, job closed. For residential tree work where you want to collect before you pack up the chipper and pull off the property, that workflow alone changes how fast cash comes in. Some companies report cutting average payment time from 18-21 days down to same-day after switching.</p>

<p>Housecall Pro's weakness for tree services is the quoting side. You can build custom line items and price lists, but there's no tiered proposal builder and no plant health records. It's a general-purpose platform, and that generality is obvious when you're trying to build complex multi-service quotes. For a direct look at how it stacks up against the most popular alternative, the <a href="/comparisons/jobber-vs-housecall-pro">Jobber vs Housecall Pro comparison</a> covers the differences in detail. Short version: Housecall Pro is simpler and better at payments; Jobber has more depth for scheduling and client management.</p>

<h3>5. Service Fusion: Best Budget Option for Growing Crews</h3>

<p>Service Fusion doesn't get talked about as much as Jobber or Housecall Pro, but it has one feature worth paying attention to as your crew grows: flat-rate pricing. Most plans don't charge per user, which means your per-head cost drops significantly once you're managing 5-6 technicians. At 6 employees on Jobber Grow ($249/month), you're paying roughly $42/person per month. Service Fusion at $99/month flat changes that math considerably.</p>

<p>Features include dispatching, invoicing, QuickBooks sync, GPS fleet tracking, and a customer portal for quote approvals and payments. The mobile app is functional but not polished. The UI takes some getting used to, and the design feels behind compared to Jobber or Housecall Pro. Onboarding is also more manual than the other platforms on this list.</p>

<p>The case for Service Fusion is straightforward: you're running a 4-6 person crew, your core needs are scheduling and invoicing, and you don't want to pay $250/month. The flat-rate model makes the cost structure work in a way the per-user platforms can't match once you're past 3 technicians. You give up some polish and all the tree-specific features, but the core job management functions hold up for residential and light commercial work.</p>

<h2>How to Pick the Right One for Your Tree Business</h2>

<p><strong>Match the software to your actual business model, not a hypothetical one. A solo operator doing residential removals needs something different than a 10-person crew managing commercial plant health care contracts. Buying too much software is as common a mistake as buying too little, and it costs real time and money getting untangled from a platform that doesn't fit your workflow.</strong></p>

<p>A few scenarios that cover most situations:</p>

<p>If you're a solo operator doing 6-8 jobs a week, mostly residential, start with Jobber Core at $49/month. You get quoting, scheduling, invoicing, and payment collection without paying for features you won't use for a year. If billing alone isn't your main pain point and you already use QuickBooks for accounting, it's worth reading through the <a href="/comparisons/jobber-vs-quickbooks-2026">Jobber vs QuickBooks breakdown</a> before committing to a full FSM subscription. Sometimes the answer really is just QuickBooks with better invoicing habits.</p>

<p>If you're running a 3-truck crew with a mix of removals, cleanups, and some recurring HOA accounts, Jobber Connect at $129/month is the realistic minimum. The multi-user dispatching and automated customer follow-ups matter at that scale. If you're doing any multi-year plant health contracts, price out SingleOps before committing. The higher cost may be worth it once recurring work is a real part of your revenue.</p>

<p>If you have 6-plus employees and the per-user pricing on Jobber or Housecall Pro is starting to add up, run the numbers on Service Fusion. The flat-rate model changes the math at that team size in a meaningful way.</p>

<p>If your company does certified arborist work, multi-year PHC programs, or manages large commercial accounts with detailed tree inventories, Arborgold or SingleOps are the only platforms that actually match that workflow. Using a general tool for specialized compliance work creates gaps that come back to bite you, especially if you're ever audited or dealing with a liability claim.</p>

<p>Tree service also sits right next to landscaping from a software perspective. Many platforms that work well for lawn care companies overlap significantly with tree service needs, particularly around seasonal scheduling and recurring account management. The <a href="/guides/best-landscaping-business-software-2026">best landscaping business software guide</a> covers some of those overlapping options if you want a broader comparison set.</p>

<h2>Our Pick for 2026</h2>

<p><strong>Jobber is the right starting point for most tree service businesses in 2026. Use Core at $49/month for solo work, Connect at $129/month for a 2-5 person crew with multi-user dispatching and QuickBooks sync. Move to SingleOps or Arborgold when your business is running recurring plant health programs or managing enough accounts that a general-purpose platform starts creating gaps in your workflow.</strong></p>

<p>Jobber wins for the majority of tree companies because it's well-built, the mobile app holds up in the field, and onboarding is measured in hours rather than weeks. Connect at $129/month is the sweet spot for a 2-5 person crew: multi-user dispatching, automated customer notifications, QuickBooks sync, and payment follow-ups that go out automatically when an invoice goes unpaid. That last feature alone recovers the subscription cost if you're currently chasing payments manually.</p>

<p>If you're running a larger operation with certified arborists doing plant health care or multi-year commercial contracts, SingleOps is the better long-term platform despite the higher cost and longer setup time. Arborgold is worth evaluating in that same category, though the aging interface is a real friction point for crews who aren't already bought into the platform. The tree inventory and PHC record-keeping capabilities are genuinely good. Getting your techs to actually use them is the harder problem.</p>

<p>Housecall Pro is worth considering if your crew resists new software and payment collection is the primary headache. The onboarding is the fastest of any option here, and the payment tools are best in class.</p>

<p>One practical note worth emphasizing: whatever you pick, get it set up before busy season, not during. Trying to migrate your customer list, rebuild your price book, and retrain your crew while you're dispatching 12 jobs a day is a bad time. Give yourself a shoulder season to get the system dialed in, train the crew, and work out the quirks before the phone is ringing off the hook.</p>
<h2>Frequently Asked Questions</h2>

<h3>Q: How much does tree service invoice software typically cost per month?</h3>
<p>Entry-level tree service platforms start at $49–$79/month. Jobber begins at $49/month with on-site quoting and crew dispatch included, while Housecall Pro is $79/month for its all-in-one field service suite. Tree-specific platforms like SingleOps and Arborgold command higher premiums but include plant health logging and compliance features that justify the cost for teams managing 10+ crew members.</p>

<h3>Q: Can my crew collect payment and send invoices directly from the job site?</h3>
<p>Yes — mobile payment collection is standard across modern tree service platforms, allowing crew to accept credit cards or digital payments upon job completion. This eliminates the typical 2–3 week invoice-and-wait cycle, cutting payment collection time by 40–60% and freeing your office from payment follow-ups.</p>

<h3>Q: At what point should we move from spreadsheets to dedicated tree service software?</h3>
<p>Once you're consistently running 8–10 jobs per week, manual invoicing begins consuming evenings and creates tracking gaps. At that volume, $49–$79/month software pays for itself in recovered admin time, plus you gain real-time crew location visibility and photo documentation that protects against scope disputes and liability claims.</p>

<h3>Q: What features do tree-specific platforms offer that general field service software doesn't?</h3>
<p>Tree-specific platforms include pesticide and phytosanitary application logging, specialized line items for stump grinding and debris disposal, and compliance documentation required for plant health contracts. General FSM platforms like Jobber handle the basics well but lack the domain-specific requirements that simplify operations for arborists managing full plant care workflows.</p>
