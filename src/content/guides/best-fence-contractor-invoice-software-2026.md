<p><strong>The best fence contractor invoice software in 2026 is Jobber ($49-$249/month), which handles field estimates, job scheduling, invoicing, and QuickBooks sync in one mobile-friendly platform built for crews on the move. Housecall Pro ($79-$289/month) and FieldPulse ($99/month flat) are the next best options depending on your crew size and budget priorities.</strong></p>

<h2>What to Look For in Fence Contractor Invoice Software</h2>

<p><strong>Fence contractor invoice software needs to handle quick on-site estimates with variable material pricing (wood vs. vinyl vs. chain link costs swing wildly), collect deposits before materials get ordered, document jobs with photos, and sync with QuickBooks without manual data entry. A mobile app that works in a field with spotty cell coverage is non-negotiable.</strong></p>

<p>Fencing is one of those trades where the estimate and the invoice can look completely different by the time the job is done. You priced 200 linear feet of 6-foot wood privacy fence, then the customer decides mid-install they want a double gate on the back instead of a single. Or the ground is rockier than expected and post-setting takes twice as long. Software that can't handle change orders and line-item adjustments in the field is going to create a billing mess.</p>

<p>Here's what actually matters when you're picking fence contractor invoice software:</p>

<ul>
  <li>Field estimate creation with material line items (panels, posts, gates, hardware, concrete)</li>
  <li>Photo documentation for before/during/after — protects you on warranty disputes</li>
  <li>Deposit collection tied to the estimate approval, before you order materials</li>
  <li>Change order handling without having to rebuild the whole invoice from scratch</li>
  <li>QuickBooks or Xero sync so your bookkeeper isn't re-entering everything manually</li>
  <li>Scheduling that handles multi-day jobs and crew assignment across job sites</li>
  <li>Automated payment follow-up — the "your invoice is 14 days overdue" text nobody wants to send by hand</li>
  <li>A mobile app that doesn't crash when you're standing in a field with two bars of signal and gloves on</li>
</ul>

<p>One thing a lot of fence contractors overlook is job costing. If you're doing residential wood fence, commercial chain link, and ornamental iron, your margins on each are completely different. Software that tracks material cost per job tells you whether that $18/linear foot price is actually making you money or just keeping you busy. The <a href="/guides/invoice-software-for-construction">best invoice software for construction trades</a> covers job costing in more depth, and it's worth reading even if fencing is your only vertical.</p>

<h2>Top 5 Fence Contractor Invoice Software Picks for 2026</h2>

<p><strong>Jobber is the top pick for most fence contractors in 2026, with Housecall Pro and FieldPulse close behind. The right choice depends on crew size: solo operators can get away with Invoice Ninja for free, a 2-4 truck operation is best served by Jobber or FieldPulse, and companies running dedicated dispatch need Housecall Pro or Workiz.</strong></p>

<h3>1. Jobber - Best Overall for Fence Crews</h3>

<p>Pricing: Core $49/month, Connect $149/month, Grow $249/month (billed annually).</p>

<p>Jobber is the industry standard for small and mid-size field service operations, and fence contractors are no exception. The estimate-to-invoice workflow is the best in this price range. You build an estimate on your phone while walking the property, send it to the homeowner with an approval link, collect a deposit when they click approve, schedule the crew, and the final invoice goes out automatically when the job is marked complete. That whole chain is one connected workflow, not separate steps across different apps.</p>

<p>The Connect plan at $149/month is where Jobber becomes worth the money. You get QuickBooks Online sync, two-way text messaging with customers, GPS tracking for your crews, and automatic payment reminders. For a fence company running two or three trucks, this is the right tier. The Core plan at $49/month is fine if you're solo and just need clean invoicing and scheduling, but the lack of QuickBooks sync on Core is a real limitation if you're already using QBO.</p>

<p>The mobile app is genuinely good. Not perfect (the job notes search is clunky and the loading screens add up over a long day), but it's fast, stable, and the technician-facing view is simple enough that guys who hate using their phones can figure it out without a training session. That matters more than any feature list when you're trying to get a four-person crew to actually adopt new software.</p>

<p>The knock on Jobber is the pricing jump between tiers. Going from Core to Connect doubles the monthly cost, and that stings if you're a two-person operation that only needs a couple of Connect features. If you're comparing Jobber to Housecall Pro at a similar budget, the <a href="/comparisons/jobber-vs-housecall-pro">Jobber vs. Housecall Pro 2026 breakdown</a> is worth reviewing before you commit.</p>

<h3>2. Housecall Pro - Best for Mid-Size Fence Companies</h3>

<p>Pricing: Basic $79/month (1 user), Essentials $189/month (up to 5 users), Max $289/month (unlimited users).</p>

<p>Housecall Pro is a stronger product than most people give it credit for. The customer communication tools are the best in this category by a clear margin. Built-in two-way SMS, automated appointment reminders, review request automation, and a customer-facing portal where homeowners can approve estimates, pay invoices, and see job history. For fence contractors doing heavy residential work where you're competing on responsiveness as much as price, that communication layer matters.</p>

<p>The consumer financing integration is a real differentiator for fencing specifically. A full wood privacy fence around a half-acre lot can run $8,000-$15,000 depending on your market and materials. Offering financing at the point of estimate approval closes jobs that would otherwise stall. Housecall Pro's financing integration through Wisetack makes that easy to offer without setting up a separate merchant account.</p>

<p>The downside is entry-level pricing. At $79/month for one user on Basic, you're paying more than Jobber Core for fewer features. The Essentials plan at $189/month is where Housecall Pro starts delivering value that justifies the cost, and that's a significant monthly commitment for a smaller fence operation that's still figuring out its volume.</p>

<h3>3. FieldPulse - Best Value for Smaller Operations</h3>

<p>Pricing: $99/month for up to 2 users, $30/month per additional user.</p>

<p>FieldPulse doesn't get talked about as much as Jobber or Housecall Pro, but the flat-rate pricing model is genuinely appealing for fence contractors who hate unpredictable software bills. You know exactly what you're paying. Two users at $99/month undercuts Jobber Core while including QuickBooks sync on all plans — something Jobber doesn't offer until the $149/month Connect tier. That's a meaningful difference if accounting integration is your main reason for switching software.</p>

<p>The job costing features are better than most people expect at this price point. You can track estimated versus actual material costs per job, which is useful when you're pulling materials from multiple suppliers across a single project. The photo documentation is solid, with before/after attachments tied directly to work orders rather than floating in a photo gallery.</p>

<p>The mobile app is the weak point. It's functional, but slower and less polished than Jobber's. For crews who live in the app all day across multiple jobs, that gap adds friction over time. The <a href="/guides/fieldpulse-reviews-2026-buyers-guide">FieldPulse 2026 review</a> digs into that in more detail if you want to trial it with open eyes.</p>

<h3>4. Workiz - Best for Scheduling-Heavy Operations</h3>

<p>Pricing: Lite $65/month (2 users), Standard $169/month (5 users), Pro $299/month (unlimited users).</p>

<p>Workiz started as a scheduling and dispatch platform, and that's still where it's strongest. The dispatch board is excellent. If you're managing multiple fence crews across different job sites, same-day schedule changes, and jobs that get pushed a day or two because of weather (which fence work deals with constantly), Workiz handles that complexity better than most platforms at this price range.</p>

<p>The built-in phone and SMS system is something you don't see elsewhere at this price point. Every customer call can be recorded, transcribed, and attached to the job file. For commercial fence work where you need documentation of what was agreed on during a site walk, that's legitimately useful when a dispute comes up three months later.</p>

<p>The invoicing is functional but secondary to the scheduling features. It handles the basics, but it doesn't have the estimate-to-invoice workflow polish that Jobber has. If billing is your primary headache and your scheduling is already running fine, Workiz probably isn't your best pick.</p>

<h3>5. Invoice Ninja - Best for Solo Fence Operators</h3>

<p>Pricing: Free (up to 20 clients), Pro $12/month, Enterprise $16/month.</p>

<p>Invoice Ninja is not a field service app. No scheduling, no crew management, no GPS. What it has is clean, professional invoicing and estimates at a price that is either free or essentially free. For a one-person fence operation where you're scheduling in Google Calendar and doing your own bookkeeping in a spreadsheet, Invoice Ninja lets you send professional PDF invoices and estimates without paying $100+/month for features you're not using yet.</p>

<p>The Stripe and PayPal integrations work cleanly. Clients pay directly from the invoice link, and payment status updates in real time. It's not going to replace a full FSM platform as you grow, but as a starting point for a fence contractor who just went independent, it's a respectable tool. If you're evaluating similar lightweight invoicing options, the <a href="/guides/best-tree-service-invoice-software-2026">best tree service invoice software</a> guide covers a few more options in this simpler-invoicing category that fence operators might also find useful.</p>

<h2>How to Choose Fence Contractor Invoice Software</h2>

<p><strong>Match the software to your crew size and your biggest operational headache. Solo fence contractors running under 10 jobs a month need clean invoicing more than scheduling features. A 3-5 person crew doing $500K or more a year needs scheduling, QuickBooks sync, and automated follow-ups to stop losing money to administrative gaps.</strong></p>

<p>Start with the honest question: what is actually costing you money right now?</p>

<p>If the answer is "customers take forever to pay and I'm chasing invoices every week," Jobber's automatic payment reminders and online payment links address that directly. A lot of fence contractors see their average days-to-pay drop from 28+ days to under 10 after switching to software with automated follow-up built in. That alone can justify the subscription cost in the first month.</p>

<p>If the answer is "I'm losing track of which crew is where and jobs are getting missed," Workiz or Housecall Pro's dispatch features are what you need. Jobber's scheduling is good, but Workiz's dispatch board is built specifically for operations where crew assignments change throughout the day.</p>

<p>If the answer is "I spend two hours every Sunday matching invoices to bank deposits in QuickBooks," any of the top four options solve that with direct sync. The <a href="/guides/best-software-for-small-contractors">best software for small contractors guide</a> covers that accounting integration question in more depth if that's the main driver in your decision.</p>

<p>Commercial fence work adds complexity worth thinking through before you pick a platform. Large commercial jobs often require progress billing (30% upfront, 40% at milestone, 30% at completion). Not every platform handles this well at the lower price tiers. Jobber handles progress billing on Connect and Grow plans. Housecall Pro handles it on Essentials and above. FieldPulse has partial billing functionality but it's less polished. If you're doing significant commercial volume, that difference matters.</p>

<p>Also worth considering: are you primarily residential or commercial? Residential fence is high volume, lower average ticket, and the priority is customer communication, online payment convenience, and fast estimate turnaround. Commercial fence is lower volume, higher ticket, and the priority shifts to job costing, documentation, and lien waiver management. Some contractors doing heavy commercial work end up outgrowing FSM software entirely and moving toward construction project management tools like Buildertrend or CoConstruct. That's a different conversation, but it's worth knowing the boundary exists.</p>

<h2>Our Recommendation</h2>

<p><strong>For most fence contractors in 2026, Jobber's Connect plan at $149/month is the right call. It covers the full estimate-to-invoice workflow, QuickBooks sync, scheduling, and automated payment follow-up without a steep learning curve. Solo operators or startups should trial FieldPulse at $99/month flat before committing to anything higher. Avoid enterprise platforms like ServiceTitan unless you're running a multi-crew commercial fence operation billing over $1 million annually.</strong></p>

<p>Jobber is the most complete option for fence contractors who are past the startup phase and running a real operation. It's not cheap, but $149/month disappears quickly when it saves you 5+ hours of admin work per week and cuts your average collection time in half. The mobile app reliability alone is worth the premium over lower-cost alternatives if your crews are in the field every day.</p>

<p>FieldPulse is the underrated pick on this list. The flat-rate pricing removes the billing anxiety that comes with per-user platforms when you're adding seasonal crew each spring. And the fact that QuickBooks sync is included on all plans (not locked behind a higher tier like it is with Jobber) is a practical advantage that doesn't get enough attention in most software comparisons.</p>

<p>If you're a solo fence contractor doing 15-20 jobs a month, start with Invoice Ninja's free plan. Use it until the absence of scheduling and job management starts costing you more than $99-$149/month in missed efficiency. You'll know when that point arrives. Then move up.</p>

<p>One thing worth saying plainly: don't pick software based on the demo video. Pick it based on the mobile app. The demo is always clean and polished. The mobile app is what your crew actually uses at 7 AM in a muddy backyard. Test the app yourself in real conditions before you commit. Most of these platforms offer 14-30 day free trials, which is more than enough time to find out whether your guys will actually use it or just go back to paper tickets the first week you turn your back.</p>
<h2>Frequently Asked Questions</h2>

<h3>Q: How much does fence contractor invoice software typically cost?</h3>
<p>Most fence contractor invoice software ranges from $49 to $289 per month, with options like Jobber at $49–$249/month and FieldPulse at a flat $99/month for smaller teams. The cost depends on features (field estimates, photo documentation, QuickBooks sync) and user seat count; entry-level plans focus on invoicing and estimates, while premium tiers add crew scheduling, payment processing, and advanced reporting.</p>

<h3>Q: Can fence contractor invoice software handle change orders and mid-job modifications?</h3>
<p>Yes, dedicated platforms like Jobber and Housecall Pro include built-in change order workflows that let you document material or labor additions on-site without rebuilding the entire invoice. This is critical in fencing because scope changes—extra gates, upgraded materials, unforeseen site conditions—are common and need to be tracked separately for clear billing and customer approval.</p>

<h3>Q: Does fence contractor invoice software sync with QuickBooks or Xero?</h3>
<p>Most platforms, including Jobber and Housecall Pro, offer direct QuickBooks and Xero integration that automatically pushes invoices, payments, and job costs without manual re-entry. This eliminates weeks of bookkeeper data entry and prevents cost reconciliation errors between field software and your accounting system.</p>

<h3>Q: What's the cheapest fence contractor invoice software with an offline mobile app?</h3>
<p>FieldPulse is one of the most affordable at $99/month flat, with a mobile app that caches estimates and invoices for offline work in areas with spotty cell coverage—essential for job sites far from town. Jobber's lowest tier at $49/month also works offline but syncs data less frequently than paid tiers.</p>
