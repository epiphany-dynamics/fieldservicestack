Swept ($40-$200/month based on headcount) is purpose-built for janitorial teams and worth a serious look. Housecall Pro works well if you're mixing residential and commercial work. <h2>What to Look For in Commercial Cleaning Invoice Software</h2>

<p><strong>Commercial cleaning invoicing has different demands than residential work. You need recurring billing that runs automatically, net-30 payment terms configurable per client, multi-building client management, and an audit trail your commercial accounts can actually use. Mobile crew access and QuickBooks sync are the baseline here, not bonus features.</strong></p>

<p>Running a commercial cleaning operation is not like running a residential maid service. Your clients are office managers, property management companies, and facilities directors who want invoices that look professional, arrive on a predictable schedule, and match purchase orders when applicable. If your software can't handle that, you're manually processing invoices and chasing payments in ways that quietly kill your margins.</p>

<p>Here's what you actually need to evaluate before picking a platform:</p>

<ul>
  <li>Recurring invoice automation — you're cleaning the same buildings on the same schedule, and generating invoices manually every month is time you don't have</li>
  <li>Per-client payment terms — one client pays on receipt, another is net-30, a third is net-45 because they're a property management company and that's just how it works; your software should handle all three without workarounds</li>
  <li>Multi-location client management — if you're cleaning 12 floors across 3 buildings for one company, that should be one client record with multiple locations, not 3 separate accounts</li>
  <li>Mobile app quality for crew leads — whoever is finishing the job at 11pm needs to mark it done and trigger an invoice without calling the office</li>
  <li>Real QuickBooks or Xero integration (not a CSV export dressed up as a sync)</li>
  <li>Digital signatures and work order confirmations — commercial clients often require sign-off before they'll process payment</li>
  <li>Time tracking for hourly contracts — accurate logs are how you justify invoice amounts when a facilities director questions them</li>
</ul>

For this article, the focus is on which platforms do invoicing well enough to replace your spreadsheet system or basic accounting setup.</p>

<h2>Top Commercial Cleaning Invoice Software in 2026</h2>

<p><strong>Jobber leads the field for commercial cleaning invoicing in 2026, with Swept as the specialist alternative for dedicated janitorial teams. Housecall Pro is a solid third for mixed operations, Workiz adds strong communication tools, ServiceM8 works well for iOS-only shops, and Janitorial Manager handles large enterprise facilities accounts that the other platforms aren't built for.</strong></p>

<h3>1. Jobber — Best All-Around Pick</h3>

<p>Jobber handles commercial cleaning invoicing better than most software that doesn't specifically brand itself as cleaning software. The recurring billing setup takes about 10 minutes to configure per client, and once it's running, invoices go out automatically on whatever schedule you set. Net-30 terms, custom invoice numbers, line items for different service types, client-specific pricing — it's all there and it actually works without a support ticket.</p>

<p>The QuickBooks Online integration is the best in this category. It's a real two-way sync, not just a data export. Payments recorded in Jobber show up in QuickBooks automatically, which eliminates the manual reconciliation headaches that come with managing multiple commercial accounts.</p>

<p>For a 5-truck commercial cleaning crew, the Connect plan at $129/month covers up to 5 users and includes the full invoicing suite. That's a real cost for a small operation, but if you're doing $30,000/month in commercial contracts and you're still emailing PDF invoices manually, you're leaving money on the table through delayed payments and chasing net-30 clients who "didn't receive it." Jobber's client portal for online payment typically cuts average days-to-payment noticeably, which matters more than the software cost.</p>

<p>The weakness is that Jobber wasn't built for cleaning specifically, so it lacks features that janitorial-specific platforms include, like quality inspection checklists tied directly to invoices or per-room service tracking. For most commercial cleaning operations that aren't doing large-scale facilities management, that tradeoff is acceptable.</p>

<p>Pricing: Lite $19/month (1 user), Core $49/month (1 user), Connect $129/month (5 users), Grow $249/month (15 users).</p>

<h3>2. Swept — Best for Dedicated Janitorial Teams</h3>

<p>Swept is the only software on this list built specifically for commercial cleaning and janitorial operations from day one. It shows. Location-based clock-in verification (confirming your crew is actually in the building), per-location inspection checklists, and client-facing QR code access make it genuinely useful in ways that general field service platforms aren't.</p>

<p>The invoicing itself is solid but not exceptional. It handles recurring billing, generates professional invoices, and integrates with QuickBooks. What separates Swept is the operational layer underneath the invoice. You can tie inspections to job completion, meaning your invoice only goes out after the shift supervisor confirms the checklist is complete. For commercial clients who demand accountability and documentation, that's a real differentiator when you're up against competitors quoting lower prices.</p>

<p>Swept's pricing is headcount-based, which scales faster than you'd expect. A team of 10 employees runs around $60-80/month. A team of 30 can push $150-200/month depending on features. The mobile app is well-designed and crew-friendly, which matters when your workforce isn't particularly tech-forward. Simple beats feature-rich when the people using it are finishing a shift at midnight.</p>

<p>Pricing: Starts around $40/month for small teams; scales with employee count. Request a quote for teams over 20.</p>

<h3>3. Housecall Pro — Best for Mixed Operations</h3>

<p>If you're running commercial cleaning alongside residential work, Housecall Pro is the most natural fit. It handles both workflows without requiring two separate software subscriptions, and the invoicing is clean enough that commercial clients don't push back on the format.</p>

<p>Recurring billing works well. The client portal for online payment is competitive with Jobber's. Where Housecall Pro falls behind on the commercial side is multi-location client management and the absence of inspection or compliance features that dedicated cleaning platforms include.</p>

<p>The pricing has also crept up. The Basic plan at $59/month covers only 1 user, which isn't enough for most commercial operations. You're realistically looking at the Essentials plan at $149/month to get meaningful team functionality. The <a href="/reviews/housecall-pro-pricing-2026">Housecall Pro pricing breakdown</a> is worth reading before you commit, because the add-on costs can surprise you.</p>

<p>Pricing: Basic $59/month (1 user), Essentials $149/month (up to 5 users), MAX pricing on request.</p>

<h3>4. Workiz — Best for Communication-Heavy Teams</h3>

<p>Workiz does invoicing competently and adds one feature set that commercial cleaning operations often undervalue: built-in two-way texting and a client communication hub. If you're managing multiple commercial accounts and you spend a significant part of your week chasing invoice approvals and answering calls from property managers, Workiz's communication tools reduce that overhead in a way that the other platforms don't.</p>

<p>The invoicing module handles recurring billing, custom line items, and payment processing. QuickBooks integration is available. It's not the most sophisticated invoicing setup in this category, but it covers the essentials. The real strength is the full workflow from booking through to payment collection, especially for operations that deal with a high volume of inbound requests from commercial property management companies.</p>

<p>Our <a href="/comparisons/workiz-vs-jobber-2026">Workiz vs Jobber comparison</a> goes into more detail on where each platform wins if you're deciding between the two.</p>

<p>Pricing: Starter plans around $65/month; Growth around $110/month; Scale is custom. Confirm current pricing directly as it changes periodically.</p>

<h3>5. ServiceM8 — Best for iPad and iPhone-Based Shops</h3>

<p>ServiceM8 is a genuinely good invoicing platform with one caveat that disqualifies it for some operations: it's iOS-only. If your crew leads are all on iPhones and iPads, that's not a problem. If you have Android users in the field, look elsewhere.</p>

<p>The invoicing quality is high. Templates are professional, the client-facing experience is clean, and recurring billing setup is straightforward. Pricing is job-based rather than per-user, which is a different model than most competitors. The Premium plan at $149/month covers up to 500 jobs per month, realistic for a mid-sized commercial cleaning operation.</p>

<p>QuickBooks and Xero both integrate well. For a smaller operation that wants clean invoicing without a lot of operational overhead, and whose team is all on Apple devices, ServiceM8 is a legitimate contender.</p>

<p>Pricing: Free (20 jobs/month), Starter $29/month (50 jobs), Growing $79/month (150 jobs), Premium $149/month (500 jobs), Premium Plus $349/month (unlimited).</p>

<h3>6. Janitorial Manager — Best for Large Facilities Operations</h3>

<p>Janitorial Manager is enterprise-grade software built for commercial cleaning companies with 50 or more employees managing large facilities contracts. Production rate tracking (how fast should your team clean X square feet?), quality inspection workflows tied to SLA compliance, and billing structures built for facilities management contracts are things the other platforms simply don't offer.</p>

<p>The invoicing handles complex billing arrangements that large commercial accounts require. The problem is price and implementation complexity. This isn't software you set up in a weekend. For smaller operations, it's overkill, and the onboarding cost alone will outweigh the benefits for at least the first several months.</p>

<p>Pricing: Custom quotes. Generally starts around $200-300/month for smaller implementations and scales significantly from there.</p>

<h2>How to Choose the Right Software for Your Cleaning Business</h2>

<p><strong>The right pick depends on your operation type, team size, and whether you're doing commercial-only or mixed work. Under 5 employees doing commercial-only: start with Swept or Jobber Core. 5-15 employees with a mix of account types: Jobber Connect. 50+ employees on facilities contracts: get a Janitorial Manager demo before signing anything else.</strong></p>

<p>A few scenarios worth mapping out before you pick:</p>

<p>If you're a solo operator running 8-10 commercial accounts per week, you don't need a $129/month platform. Jobber Core at $49/month or ServiceM8's Growing plan at $79/month covers your invoicing needs without the overhead of a full crew management suite. Paying for 5-user access when you're the only user is just wasted money.</p>

<p>If you're running a 3-crew commercial cleaning operation with 15 or more employees, the calculus changes. You need multi-user access, time tracking that ties to invoices, and probably a location verification feature to confirm crews are on-site. That points to Jobber Connect, Swept, or Workiz in the $100-150/month range.</p>

<p>If your commercial clients are sending purchase orders and requiring that your invoices reference PO numbers, make sure your platform supports custom fields on invoices. Jobber and Swept both do this. It sounds minor until a facilities manager rejects your invoice because the PO number isn't on it and delays payment by 30 days.</p>

<p>The QuickBooks question comes up constantly. If you're already running QuickBooks Online, prioritize platforms with a genuine two-way sync. One-way exports where you download a CSV and manually import it are not integrations. They're extra work dressed up as a feature. Test the QuickBooks sync during your free trial with real data before committing to any platform.</p>

<p>If budget is the primary constraint, our guide to <a href="/guides/cheapest-field-service-software">the cheapest field service software that actually works</a> covers lower-cost options worth evaluating before committing to a higher-tier plan.</p>

<h2>Our Recommendation</h2>

<p><strong>For most commercial cleaning businesses in 2026, start with Jobber. It's not cleaning-specific, but the invoicing quality, QuickBooks integration, and mobile app cover 90% of what commercial clients need. If you're running a dedicated janitorial team and need inspection workflows tied directly to billing, Swept is worth the switch despite the higher per-headcount cost as you grow.</strong></p>

<p>The honest answer is that the best software depends on what's actually breaking in your current workflow. If invoices go out late or clients dispute amounts, Jobber fixes that. If your crews aren't being verified on-site and commercial clients are complaining about accountability, Swept's location-based verification and inspection checklists fix that. If you're spending three hours a week on the phone with property managers about scheduling changes and payment status, Workiz's communication tools are probably the answer.</p>

<p>Start with free trials on Jobber and Swept. Both offer trials without requiring a credit card upfront. Run your actual invoices through the system — not a demo scenario with fake clients. That's the only real test. A platform that looks perfect in a sales demo and falls apart when you try to set up your real net-30 clients is not actually a good platform for you.</p>

<p>One last thing: whatever you choose, get the QuickBooks integration working before you commit to a paid plan. That single integration tends to determine whether a platform actually saves time or just moves the manual work somewhere else.</p>
<h2>Frequently Asked Questions</h2>
<h3>Q: How much does commercial cleaning invoice software cost per month?</h3>
<p>Top commercial cleaning software ranges from $40–$249 monthly depending on crew size, with Jobber ($49–$249) and Swept ($40–$200) being the most popular options in the small-to-midsize market. Pricing typically scales by number of team members rather than client count, so you're not penalized for handling multiple locations under one account.</p>
<h3>Q: Do commercial cleaning invoice platforms integrate with QuickBooks for accounting?</h3>
<p>Yes, both Jobber and Swept offer direct QuickBooks Online integration that syncs invoices, payments, and expense data automatically, eliminating manual double-entry work for your accounting team. This integration is considered table-stakes functionality—you shouldn't have to manually upload data to your accounting system.</p>
<h3>Q: Can software handle different payment terms (net-30, net-45, net-60) for different clients?</h3>
<p>Yes—quality commercial cleaning software lets you configure payment terms on a per-client basis, which is essential since office managers often require net-30 while property management companies demand net-45 or net-60. This flexibility prevents billing disputes and gives you accurate cash flow visibility across your client mix.</p>
<h3>Q: Is a mobile app for crew access essential in commercial cleaning invoice software?</h3>
<p>Yes, a mobile app where crew leads can mark jobs complete, upload photos, and trigger automatic client notifications is baseline functionality in modern commercial software—it eliminates paper timesheets and ensures real-time billing accuracy. Without mobile job completion, you lose visibility into daily operations and create delays in invoice generation and payment collection.</p>
