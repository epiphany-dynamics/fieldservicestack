<p><strong>Jobber is field service management software priced at $49-$249/month (annual billing), built for small-to-mid home service businesses. It covers scheduling, dispatch, quoting, invoicing, two-way texting, and client payment tools in one platform. Best fit for HVAC, plumbing, landscaping, and cleaning crews with 1-15 employees. Larger multi-location shops will outgrow it.</strong></p>

<h2>What to Look For in Field Service Management Software</h2>

<p><strong>Good FSM software should handle the complete job lifecycle from first call to collected payment without forcing your dispatcher to toggle between four different tools. Scheduling that works on a phone, invoicing that gets paid fast, and clean QuickBooks sync are the baseline requirements. Get those right before thinking about anything else.</strong></p>

<p>Before you spend money on anything, run the platform against real-world conditions. A lot of software looks great in a sales demo and falls apart the second your tech tries to log in from a job site parking lot with two bars of signal.</p>

<p>Scheduling and dispatch is the obvious starting point. Your office should be able to see all active jobs, drag and drop rescheduled work, and handle a last-minute cancellation without calling three people. If figuring out who's available takes more than 30 seconds, the UI is failing you.</p>

<p>Mobile app quality gets underestimated consistently. The best office-side platform is useless if your techs won't touch it. Slow loads, crashes on older Android devices, or an interface that requires a training session will kill adoption within six months regardless of what you paid for it.</p>

<p>QuickBooks integration matters more than most owners realize until they're staring at two months of manually double-entered invoices. Check whether it's a genuine two-way sync or just an export button with a QuickBooks logo on it. Those are very different things, and the distinction rarely shows up in a demo.</p>

<p>Customer communication tools are what separate the platforms doing real work from glorified scheduling calendars. Automated appointment reminders cut no-shows. Two-way texting keeps jobs moving without your techs fielding calls on their personal phones. A client payment portal moves money faster than chasing checks.</p>

<p>Reporting rounds it out. You need to see which jobs are profitable, which techs are closing, and where you're losing hours. Without that visibility, you're running the business on gut feel.</p>

<h2>Jobber Features: What You're Actually Getting</h2>

<p><strong>Jobber's feature set covers scheduling, quoting with online approvals, invoicing, two-way texting, a client self-service hub, basic GPS tracking, automated appointment reminders, and online booking. Most features are gated by plan tier. The mobile app is one of the stronger ones in this price range, stable on iOS and Android for daily field use.</strong></p>

<p>The scheduling calendar is clean and functional. Color-code by crew member, view by day or week, and jobs update in real time when something changes in the field. It's not the most visually elaborate dispatch board on the market, but it's fast and the learning curve is short enough that a non-technical owner can figure it out inside a week.</p>

<p>Quoting is where Jobber earns consistent praise from contractors. Build a professional quote, send it by text or email, and the customer approves online. That digital approval creates a paper trail that removes the "I never agreed to that price" conversation. If you've done residential service work for any amount of time, you know how often that comes up and how hard it is to win without documentation.</p>

<p>The client hub is a self-service portal where customers view job history, approve quotes, and pay invoices. It's not the most sophisticated portal in this space, but customers actually use it without needing instructions. That matters more than an impressive feature list nobody adopts.</p>

<p>Invoicing is fast. Send from the job site, collect payment by card on the spot, or shoot a payment link. Jobber Payments processes cards at 2.9% + $0.30 for card-not-present transactions, which is standard market-rate pricing. High-volume shops should compare that against their existing processor rates before defaulting to it.</p>

<p>Two-way texting is available on Connect and Grow. Your tech sends texts from a Jobber number, not their personal cell. Replies land in the app instead of a private text thread nobody else can see. This sounds minor until you've had a tech go dark for two hours handling customer messages on his own phone while your dispatcher has no idea what's going on.</p>

<p>GPS tracking is included on Connect and Grow, but it's basic. Arrival timestamps, real-time location visibility, that kind of thing. If you're running 10 or more trucks and need mileage reports, geofence alerts, or vehicle diagnostics, Jobber's GPS won't cut it. For a closer look at what each plan delivers, the <a href="/reviews/jobber-review-2026">Jobber Review 2026</a> covers the details that don't show up on the pricing page.</p>

<p>Core is adequate for a solo operator who needs basic structure and wants to stop running jobs out of a text thread. The honest issue: no automated reminders, no two-way texting, no QuickBooks sync. For any business doing real volume, Core is a stepping stone, not a destination. You'll hit its ceiling faster than you expect.</p>

<p>Connect at $129/month is where Jobber starts making clear financial sense for a growing operation. Automated reminders alone save real time every single week. Add two-way texting and QuickBooks sync and you have an actual operations platform, not just a nicer-looking calendar. A 3-truck HVAC crew or a solo plumber with a couple of helpers will be comfortable on Connect for a long time.</p>

<p>Grow makes sense once you're past 5-7 people and need reporting that shows where the business actually stands. The jump from $129 to $249 is steep for what changes between plans. A lot of owners land on Connect and stay there for years, which is a completely reasonable decision. If you've been wondering <a href="/guides/why-is-jobber-so-expensive">why Jobber costs what it does</a>, the answer is that Connect and Grow have genuine depth in the features that move money, not cosmetic upgrades.</p>

<h2>Jobber Pros and Cons</h2>

<p><strong>Jobber's real strengths are its clean UI, polished quoting workflow, stable mobile app, and customer support that actually responds. The real weaknesses are basic GPS tools, thin reporting below Grow, a price gap between Connect and Grow that doesn't always feel proportional, and no native inventory management for parts-heavy shops.</strong></p>

<p><strong>Pros:</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li>Clean interface with a short learning curve, even for non-technical owners</li>
  <li>Quoting and online approval workflow is among the best in this price range</li>
  <li>Client hub and payment portal reduce time spent chasing invoices</li>
  <li>Mobile app is stable and reliable on both iOS and Android</li>
  <li>Two-way texting keeps communication in the system instead of scattered across personal phones</li>
  <li>14-day free trial with no credit card requirement</li>
  <li>Customer support is responsive, which is not something you can say about every platform in this space</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Cons:</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li>GPS tracking is too basic for real fleet management needs</li>
  <li>Reporting is limited on Core and Connect; you need Grow for meaningful business analytics</li>
  <li>No built-in marketing automation (Housecall Pro has a clear advantage here)</li>
  <li>The $120/month jump from Connect to Grow doesn't always match the feature gain for mid-size crews</li>
  <li>Offline mode has real limitations in low-signal or no-signal environments</li>
  <li>No native inventory management for shops that track parts and materials job-to-job</li>
</ul>

<h2>How Jobber Compares to the Alternatives</h2>

<p><strong>Housecall Pro is the closest direct competitor at similar pricing with stronger built-in marketing tools. ServiceTitan is in a different tier entirely, built for larger operations at a much higher price. Service Fusion offers solid mid-market depth with flat-rate pricing that scales better for larger crews. Workiz is a lean option for solo operators and very small operations.</strong></p>

<h3>Housecall Pro</h3>
<p>Housecall Pro starts at $79/month for 1 user (annual) and goes to $189/month on the Essentials plan for up to 5 users. The marketing tools are better than Jobber's out of the box: review request automation, postcard campaigns, and more built-in customer re-engagement features come standard. The trade-off is Jobber has a cleaner quoting workflow and generally a smoother mobile experience. If your biggest operational gap is getting reviews and repeat bookings, Housecall Pro has the edge. If it's getting quotes approved and invoices paid faster, Jobber wins that comparison. The full breakdown is in our <a href="/comparisons/jobber-vs-housecall-pro">Jobber vs Housecall Pro head-to-head</a>.</p>

<h3>ServiceTitan</h3>
<p>ServiceTitan is a different weight class. Pricing typically starts around $125-$250 per user per month, plus onboarding costs that can run $1,000-$5,000 depending on the package. It's built for larger operations: multi-location HVAC companies, plumbing businesses running 20 or more trucks, shops that need real revenue reporting and advanced dispatch optimization. If you're running a 3-truck crew, you'll be paying for capabilities you can't use for years. If you're running 15-plus trucks and hitting your current software's ceiling, it's worth the serious evaluation. The <a href="/comparisons/servicetitan-vs-jobber-2026">ServiceTitan vs Jobber comparison</a> covers exactly where that line sits.</p>

<h3>Service Fusion</h3>
<p>Service Fusion prices at $149/month flat for the Starter tier, with unlimited users. That model is a notable difference from per-seat pricing: once you're running more than 3-4 users, the effective per-seat cost drops well below Jobber's. The interface is older-looking than Jobber's, but the feature depth for HVAC and plumbing shops is solid. Inventory management, flat-rate pricing catalogs, and GPS fleet tracking are all included at the entry level. The mobile app is where it loses ground to Jobber. If your operation is office-heavy and your techs can tolerate a less polished app, Service Fusion is worth a serious look as your crew grows.</p>

<h3>Workiz</h3>
<p>Workiz starts around $45/month and targets smaller operations. Scheduling, invoicing, and basic communication are covered. The platform is lighter than Jobber overall, but that's not a problem if you're a solo locksmith or appliance repair tech who just needs structure without a steep learning curve. It also handles inbound call tracking natively, which Jobber doesn't. For a solo plumber doing 8-10 calls a day who doesn't need GPS tracking or advanced reporting, Workiz is a legitimate option at a lower price point.</p>

<h2>How to Choose the Right FSM Software for Your Business</h2>

<p><strong>Match the software to where your business actually is today, not your three-year projection. A solo electrician doing 6-8 calls daily has completely different needs than a 12-truck HVAC company managing multiple dispatchers. Paying for features you can't use is just a slow monthly tax on your operation.</strong></p>

<p>If you're running one truck by yourself, Jobber Core at $49/month or Workiz at $45/month are both worth testing on a free trial. The goal at this stage is structure: no more jobs falling through text threads, invoices sent same day, and a paper trail on every quote. Don't pay for Connect or Grow until the volume justifies it.</p>

<p>For a small crew of 2-5 people, Jobber Connect at $129/month hits the right balance for most HVAC, plumbing, and landscaping operations. Two-way texting, automated reminders, GPS, and QuickBooks sync all come online. If your biggest gap is marketing and repeat business, Housecall Pro Essentials at $189/month for 5 users is worth comparing before you commit to anything.</p>

<p>Mid-size operations running 6-15 people should look at Jobber Grow or Service Fusion's mid-tier plans. At this size, reporting isn't optional anymore. You need to know which job types are margin-positive and which are eating your labor budget. If you're running an HVAC shop with a flat-rate pricing book, Service Fusion's built-in pricing catalog is worth knowing about before you default to Jobber.</p>

<p>Past 15 people or across multiple locations, start your evaluation with ServiceTitan before locking into Grow. The upfront cost and onboarding time are real, but Jobber Grow will show its ceiling sooner than you expect at that scale. Landscaping and cleaning businesses should note that both Jobber and Housecall Pro handle recurring job management well, while route optimization for large crews often requires a dedicated add-on regardless of which platform you choose.</p>

<h2>Our Recommendation</h2>

<p><strong>For most home service businesses with 2-7 people, Jobber Connect at $129/month is the right call. Solo operators can start on Core at $49 and upgrade when volume demands it. Past 10 trucks or across multiple locations, evaluate ServiceTitan before committing to Grow. The 14-day trial costs nothing and tests your actual workflow under real conditions.</strong></p>

<p>Jobber is a well-built product that does what it says. The quoting workflow is polished, the mobile app works in the field, and the payment tools genuinely move money faster than chasing checks. For a residential service operation with a small-to-mid crew, that's real operational value at a price that makes sense.</p>

<p>The limitations are real. GPS is basic, inventory management doesn't exist natively, and reporting below Grow is thin. If you're running a larger shop and supplementing Jobber with separate fleet and inventory tools, factor that combined monthly cost into the comparison before you decide.</p>

<h2>Frequently Asked Questions</h2>
<h3>Q: Is Jobber worth it for a solo contractor or two-person team?</h3>
<p>For small crews, Jobber's $49/month base tier pays for itself quickly—automated scheduling eliminates phone tag, digital invoicing accelerates payment collection, and QuickBooks integration removes manual double-entry work that typically costs 3–5 hours per month in administrative overhead.</p>
<h3>Q: What features are included in each of Jobber's pricing tiers?</h3>
<p>Jobber's $49/month tier includes scheduling, dispatch, and basic invoicing; mid-tier ($99) adds advanced reporting and custom fields; and the top tier ($249) includes team management features and premium integrations—two-way texting and the mobile app are available on all plans.</p>
<h3>Q: Does Jobber's mobile app actually work on job sites with poor cell signal?</h3>
<p>Jobber's app is designed for field conditions and syncs data when signal returns, but it lacks true offline mode, so jobs in areas with persistent dead zones may experience delayed updates—it's more reliable than generic cloud apps but less robust than platforms with built-in offline capabilities.</p>
<h3>Q: How does Jobber handle dispatch for multi-location or larger teams?</h3>
<p>Jobber works best for single-location operations or small teams sharing one dispatch queue; companies needing independent management across 3+ locations or teams exceeding 15 people should evaluate platforms with native multi-location features like ServiceTitan or Housecall Pro.</p>
