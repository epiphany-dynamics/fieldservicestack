<p><strong>The best electrical contractor scheduling software in 2026 depends on your crew size. ServiceTitan is the most powerful option but runs $125-$398/tech/month with no public pricing. Jobber ($49-$349/month) covers most shops with 1-15 techs effectively. FieldEdge is the strongest trade-specific pick if flat-rate price books and commercial service agreements are central to how you work.</strong></p>

<h2>What to Look For in Electrical Contractor Scheduling Software</h2>

<p><strong>Electrical contractors need software that handles permit-driven job timelines, multi-tech dispatch, recurring maintenance agreements, and invoicing that covers both flat-rate and time-and-material work. The non-negotiables: a usable dispatch board, a mobile app that works offline in panels and attics, and QuickBooks sync that does not produce duplicate journal entries.</strong></p>

<p>Running an electrical business is not like running a cleaning service. You're dealing with permit timelines, material lists that change mid-job, multiple techs on the same commercial site, and customers who expect a two-hour arrival window. Before you demo anything, check for these:</p>

<ul>
<li>The dispatch board needs to show every tech's availability at a glance. If it's clunky or slow, your dispatcher is back to the whiteboard within a month.</li>
<li>The mobile app has to work offline. Techs are in attics, panels, and crawl spaces. An app that requires signal is useless in half the places you actually work.</li>
<li>Invoicing needs to handle both flat-rate and time-and-material, sometimes on the same job. Electrical shops use both, and the software has to keep up.</li>
<li>Recurring job automation matters if you have commercial customers on maintenance agreements. Manual scheduling is a liability once you get past a handful of accounts.</li>
<li>Customer communication tools (automated arrival texts, review requests after close) should be built in, not an add-on tier.</li>
<li>QuickBooks sync should be two-way. If you're on QuickBooks Desktop rather than Online, verify compatibility before signing anything. Several platforms here only support QBO.</li>
</ul>

<h2>Top 5 Electrical Contractor Scheduling Software Picks for 2026</h2>

<p><strong>The five platforms worth your time in 2026 are ServiceTitan, Jobber, Housecall Pro, FieldEdge, and FieldPulse. ServiceTitan has the deepest feature set and the highest price. Jobber is the most practical choice for shops under 15 techs. FieldEdge has the best trade-specific features for electrical work. All five offer mobile apps and QuickBooks integration.</strong></p>

<h3>1. ServiceTitan: Best for Multi-Truck Electrical Operations</h3>

<p>ServiceTitan is the strongest platform in field service software, and it's not close. The problem is the price. Most contractors report paying $125-$398 per tech per month depending on plan tier, plus an onboarding fee that can run several thousand dollars upfront. For a 20-tech operation doing commercial and residential electrical work, that investment is defensible. For a 3-truck shop, it will probably break your budget and overwhelm your office staff.</p>

<p>The dispatch board is the best on this list. Real-time tech GPS, color-coded job status, automated scheduling recommendations based on proximity and skill set. The flat-rate price book is genuinely excellent for electrical contractors: techs present it on a tablet, customers see a clean total, and approval rates go up. Shop owners who've switched to this workflow consistently report average ticket increases of 20-25%.</p>

<p>The QuickBooks integration needs setup time to avoid duplicate entries, but once configured, it's solid. For an unfiltered look at whether the investment pays off for your company size, the <a href="/reviews/is-servicetitan-worth-it-2026">honest ServiceTitan cost-versus-value breakdown</a> covers real contractor feedback directly. Best fit for operations with 10+ techs, commercial work, or multiple locations.</p>

<h3>2. Jobber: Best for Small to Midsize Electrical Shops</h3>

<p>Jobber is the right answer for most electrical contractors reading this. Core is $49/month for one user, Connect is $149/month for up to 5 users, and Grow is $349/month for up to 15 users. Pricing is transparent, there are no surprise fees, and the feature set covers the full job cycle from estimate to payment collection.</p>

<p>The scheduling tools work well. Drag-and-drop dispatch calendar, route optimization, automated customer reminders, and an online booking portal that fills schedule gaps without phone calls. The gap is flat-rate pricing: Jobber is built more around T&M and custom quoting than pre-built price books. If your shop sells flat-rate electrical packages (and you should, they convert better), you'll build that structure yourself.</p>

<p>The mobile app is clean, works offline, and lets techs pull up job details, upload photos, collect signatures, and invoice at the door. QuickBooks Online sync is reliable and two-way. We cover how Jobber fits electrical-specific workflows in detail in the <a href="/guides/jobber-for-electricians-2026">Jobber for electricians 2026 guide</a>. Best for residential and light commercial shops with 1-15 techs.</p>

<h3>3. FieldEdge: Best Trade-Specific Features for Electricians</h3>

<p>FieldEdge doesn't get as much press as Jobber or ServiceTitan, but for electrical contractors specifically, it deserves a serious look. It was built for the trades, and the feature set reflects that. Flat-rate pricing is already structured for skilled trades work. The service agreement module tracks recurring visits per property, not just per customer, which matters when you're managing commercial buildings with multiple electrical systems. Service history is organized the way an electrical shop actually thinks about a job site.</p>

<p>Pricing runs approximately $150-$200+ per user per month with a custom quote process, which is annoying. You won't get a number off their website without talking to sales. Implementation is more involved than Jobber, but the support through onboarding is solid. The reporting depth is better than Jobber's out of the box: revenue per tech, close rates on estimates, profitability by job type. QuickBooks sync is two-way and reliable.</p>

<p>Our <a href="/reviews/fieldedge-review-2026">full FieldEdge 2026 review</a> goes deeper on the feature set if you want specifics before booking a demo. Best for commercial-heavy electrical operations and shops with 5-20+ techs who want trade-specific features without ServiceTitan's price.</p>

<h3>4. Housecall Pro: Best for Residential Electrical Contractors</h3>

<p>Housecall Pro makes sense if you're primarily doing residential electrical work: panel upgrades, rewires, circuit additions, troubleshooting calls. Basic is $79/month for small shops and solo operators. Essentials runs $189/month and adds dispatching tools, online booking, and expanded automation.</p>

<p>The customer experience features are where it earns its price. Automated text sequences (booking confirmation, tech en route, review request after job close) run without anyone in your office managing them. Consistently getting Google reviews is one of the highest-leverage things a residential electrical shop can do, and Housecall Pro automates that process better than most platforms on this list.</p>

<p>The built-in consumer financing integration is underrated. For big residential jobs like panel replacements or whole-home rewires ($5,000-$15,000+), having financing in the proposal closes jobs that would otherwise stall. The scheduling board handles 3-5 tech residential operations well, but for complex commercial dispatch or multi-stage jobs, it runs into real limits. If you're deciding between Housecall Pro and Jobber, the <a href="/comparisons/jobber-vs-housecall-pro">Jobber vs Housecall Pro 2026 comparison</a> lays out the differences without the marketing spin. Best for residential shops with 1-8 techs.</p>

<h3>5. FieldPulse: Best Flat-Rate Option for Small Shops</h3>

<p>FieldPulse positions itself as the "everything included" option at a flat rate. Solo operators start around $99/month, small teams run $149-$199+/month. Unlike Jobber or Housecall Pro, you don't unlock features by upgrading tiers. You get the full feature set from day one, which removes a common frustration with tiered pricing.</p>

<p>The scheduling tools work, the estimate-to-invoice flow is clean, and the flat-rate price book tools are more developed than Jobber's out of the box. The reporting doesn't match FieldEdge or ServiceTitan in depth, and the dispatch board isn't as sophisticated for multi-tech routing. For a lean electrical operation that wants everything handled at a predictable monthly cost, it's a solid pick that doesn't feel like a compromise. QuickBooks integration is available, and the mobile app handles daily field workflows without friction.</p>

<h2>How to Choose the Right Software for Your Electrical Business</h2>

<p><strong>Match the platform to your crew size and job mix. Solo or 1-2 helpers: Jobber Core ($49/mo) or Housecall Pro Basic ($79/mo). Three to eight techs on residential: Jobber Connect. Five to fifteen techs with commercial accounts: FieldEdge. Fifteen-plus techs or multiple locations: ServiceTitan. The most common mistake contractors make in this category is paying for features they will never use.</strong></p>

<p>Before you lock anything in, run your actual dispatch scenario through the free trial. Take a Monday morning with 8 jobs on the board and a tech who just called in sick. See how the dispatch board handles reassignment and rescheduling. That test tells you more than any sales demo ever will.</p>

<p>Two things that specifically trip up electrical contractors when switching software: First, verify whether the platform supports QuickBooks Desktop or only QuickBooks Online. If you're on QBD, migrating to QBO is a real project you'll need to plan separately, and some shops have gotten stuck mid-transition. Second, evaluate the platform based on what a new hire with basic computer skills could learn in two weeks. Software that only your veteran office manager understands is a liability when you're growing and onboarding new people.</p>

<p>If you're still deciding between the two most common options at the 5-15 tech range, the <a href="/comparisons/servicetitan-vs-jobber-2026">ServiceTitan vs Jobber 2026 comparison</a> breaks down exactly where each platform wins and loses by company size and job type.</p>

<h2>Our Recommendation</h2>

<p><strong>Jobber is the right starting point for most electrical contractors in 2026. The pricing is fair, the setup is manageable without an implementation team, and the mobile app works in the field without complaints. Move to FieldEdge if commercial service agreements are a big part of your business. Move to ServiceTitan when you're past 15 techs and need dispatch visibility and reporting depth. Don't overbuy.</strong></p>

<p>There's no universal winner here. A 2-truck residential shop and a 25-tech commercial electrical contractor have almost nothing in common when it comes to software requirements. What I'd tell any electrical contractor going through this evaluation: pick the 1-2 platforms that match your size, put your actual dispatch scenarios through the free trials, and make the call based on that. Not on feature comparison PDFs. Not on a sales demo. On whether it actually handles your Monday morning.</p>

<p>The shops that get the most out of scheduling software aren't the ones with the most features. They're the ones actually using the automation: reminders, review requests, recurring job scheduling, auto-invoicing. Those things compound over time. The right platform is the one your team opens every morning without being asked to.</p>
