I've spent time in both of these platforms. Neither one is perfect, and depending on your team size, one of them is going to cost you significantly more money than the other. That's the short version. Here's the longer one.

Housecall Pro and Service Fusion are two of the more popular field service management platforms right now, and they show up in almost every comparison thread on Reddit and Facebook groups for HVAC, plumbing, and electrical contractors. Both do scheduling, dispatching, invoicing, and payments. Both sync with QuickBooks. Both have mobile apps for your techs.

But they are built for different businesses, and the pricing model difference is not minor. One charges per user, one doesn't. Over 12 months with a 10-tech team, that gap can run into thousands of dollars.

## Who Each Platform Is Actually Built For

**Housecall Pro targets small to mid-sized residential service companies that want a polished, all-in-one experience with strong automation and marketing tools. Service Fusion targets growing SMBs and larger field teams that need unlimited-user pricing and heavier dispatch and GPS capabilities. Pick based on team size and which features you actually use daily.**

Housecall Pro's DNA is residential home service. The whole platform feels like it was designed around a 1-8 tech shop running HVAC tune-ups, plumbing calls, and electrician visits. Online booking, automated review requests, pay-by-text, marketing follow-ups. That's the core value prop.

Service Fusion is built more around the operational side: a large dispatch board, GPS tracking for fleets, route optimization, and inventory tracking. It's not as pretty. The interface shows its age in some areas. But for a shop with 15-20 techs, the unlimited-user flat rate is a massive advantage.

If you have 3 techs, Housecall Pro is probably the better product. If you have 12 techs and you're growing, Service Fusion is probably the smarter financial move.

## Feature-by-Feature Comparison

**Both platforms cover the core field service management basics: scheduling, dispatch, invoicing, mobile apps, CRM, and QuickBooks integration. Where they split is depth of automation (Housecall Pro wins), GPS and fleet management (Service Fusion wins), and per-plan feature availability. Service Fusion includes reporting in all tiers; Housecall Pro gates it to the MAX plan.**

Here's how they stack up across the features that matter most:

### Scheduling and Dispatch

Both have drag-and-drop calendars. Both show real-time job status. Housecall Pro's calendar is generally easier for office staff to learn. Service Fusion's dispatch board handles larger teams better, especially when you're managing 10 or more techs with overlapping jobs across multiple zones.

### GPS and Route Optimization

This is where Service Fusion has a clear edge. Built-in route optimization and comprehensive GPS tracking across the fleet are core features, not add-ons. Housecall Pro has GPS functionality, but it's not the focus of the product. If you run a large fleet and are routing techs every morning, this matters every single day.

### Automation and Marketing

Housecall Pro wins here, and it's not especially close. Automated follow-up texts, review request campaigns, pay-by-text payment collection, and pipeline automations are baked into the upper tiers. Service Fusion handles the basics but doesn't match Housecall Pro's depth in customer communication tools.

### Mobile App

I've seen contractors discuss this in Facebook groups more than almost anything else. Housecall Pro's mobile app gets consistent praise. Techs can capture photos, collect signatures, take payments, and update job status without jumping through hoops. Service Fusion's app works, but "works" is about the ceiling of the compliments it gets.

## Pricing Breakdown 2026

**Housecall Pro runs $79-$329/month across three tiers with per-user pricing beyond included seats. Service Fusion runs $245-$627/month with unlimited users on every plan. For small teams, Housecall Pro is cheaper upfront. For teams of 8 or more, Service Fusion's flat rate usually wins on total cost over 12 months.**

> Pricing note: vendors adjust rates and offer promotional pricing regularly. Verify current figures directly with each vendor before making a decision.

### Housecall Pro Pricing

- Basic: $79/month for 1 user. Core scheduling, dispatching, quotes, invoicing, and payments.
- Essentials: $189/month for 1-5 users. Adds automation, GPS features, and more advanced tools.
- MAX: $329/month for up to 8 users. Adds AI features, API access, advanced reporting, and dedicated support.

Additional users beyond the included seats run roughly $35/user/month. That adds up fast. A 10-tech team on the MAX plan would push past $400/month before you factor in any add-ons.

### Service Fusion Pricing

- Starter: $245/month, unlimited users.
- Plus: $382/month, unlimited users.
- Pro: $627/month, unlimited users.

The unlimited-user model is the whole pitch. At $245/month, a 15-tech company pays the same as a 2-tech company. That math is hard to argue with once your team gets big enough.

For a 10-tech team, Housecall Pro MAX with 2 overage users at $35 each would run about $399/month. Service Fusion Starter would be $245/month. That's $1,848 per year in difference. It adds up fast when you're already watching margins.

The breakeven point lands somewhere around 5-7 techs, depending on which tiers you're comparing.

## User Reviews and Real-World Sentiment

**Housecall Pro holds a 4.7/5 average across nearly 2,739 reviews on Capterra. Service Fusion sits at 4.3/5 with around 308 reviews. Housecall Pro has nearly 10 times the review volume and more consistently positive sentiment. Service Fusion reviews are more mixed, with more frequent complaints about the interface and support responsiveness.**

### What Housecall Pro Users Say

Common positive themes in Housecall Pro reviews:

- The mobile app is the most consistently praised feature. Techs actually use it without complaining, which matters more than people realize when adoption is the real barrier to getting ROI from any software.
- Automated reminders and follow-ups reduce no-shows without adding admin work.
- Pay-by-text is a genuine payment accelerator. People get paid faster.
- Onboarding is fast. Teams get up to speed without weeks of painful setup.

Common complaints:

- It gets expensive as you grow. Multiple reviewers use almost exactly that phrase, independently of each other.
- Some sync issues between mobile and desktop, where invoices don't fully update until reopened on the desktop side.
- Document handling for commercial work creates friction: invoices with image attachments send as multiple PDFs, which is annoying at high volume.

### What Service Fusion Users Say

Common positive themes in Service Fusion reviews:

- Unlimited users at a flat rate is praised repeatedly by teams that have used per-user tools and felt the pain of scaling costs.
- GPS and dispatch capabilities get strong marks from field managers running larger teams.
- Reporting being included across all plans is noticed and appreciated by budget-conscious owners.

Common complaints:

- The interface feels dated compared to newer platforms. This shows up consistently enough across reviews that it's a real pattern, not a fringe opinion from one frustrated user.
- Customer support quality is more polarizing than with Housecall Pro.
- Some users report unexpected costs around setup and third-party integrations.

## Which One Should You Choose?

**For teams under 6-7 techs doing mostly residential service work, Housecall Pro is the better product in 2026: cleaner interface, stronger mobile app, and better marketing automation. For teams of 8 or more, or shops growing fast where per-user costs are starting to hurt, Service Fusion's flat-rate model makes more financial sense.**

Here's where I land after looking at both platforms:

Choose Housecall Pro if:
- You have fewer than 6-7 techs and per-user cost isn't a budget issue yet.
- Residential service is your primary market.
- You want the best mobile app experience for field techs.
- Marketing automation and customer communication tools matter to how you grow.
- You want AI features now or want a platform that's investing in them (MAX plan).

Choose Service Fusion if:
- You have 8 or more techs and per-user pricing is eating into margins.
- You run commercial jobs with multi-stage billing or progress invoicing.
- GPS tracking and route optimization for a larger fleet is a daily operational need.
- You want reporting without paying for the top-tier plan.
- You can live with a less polished interface in exchange for flat-rate pricing.

One thing worth adding: Housecall Pro is clearly investing more aggressively in automation and AI-driven features based on what the MAX plan shipped in 2026. If you're building toward a business where software handles more of the customer communication and scheduling coordination, that platform is moving in that direction faster. Service Fusion is investing in scaling field operations. Two different visions of what FSM software should be. Neither is wrong.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is Housecall Pro more expensive than Service Fusion?**

It depends on team size. Housecall Pro starts at $79/month for 1 user; Service Fusion starts at $245/month with unlimited users. For a solo operator or a 2-3 tech team, Housecall Pro is cheaper. For a team of 8 or more, Service Fusion typically wins on total monthly cost because there is no per-user charge. The breakeven sits around 5-7 techs depending on which tiers you compare.

**Does Service Fusion have route optimization?**

Yes. Route optimization is built into Service Fusion as a core feature, not an add-on. This is one area where Service Fusion clearly outpaces Housecall Pro, which has GPS functionality but does not lead with route optimization as a priority. For companies managing larger fleets with multiple daily dispatches, this is a meaningful difference.

**Which platform has better user reviews?**

Housecall Pro holds a 4.7/5 average across nearly 2,739 Capterra reviews. Service Fusion sits at 4.3/5 with around 308 reviews. Housecall Pro has almost 10 times the review volume and more consistently positive sentiment. Service Fusion reviews are more mixed, with more frequent complaints about the interface and support experience.

**Can Housecall Pro handle commercial work?**

It can, but there are friction points worth knowing about. Several users specifically note that document handling for commercial jobs creates headaches: invoices with image attachments output as multiple PDFs, which gets frustrating at volume. Service Fusion handles progress invoicing for multi-stage commercial work better. If commercial accounts are your primary market rather than residential service calls, Service Fusion has a structural advantage there.
