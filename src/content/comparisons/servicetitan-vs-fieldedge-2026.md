I've watched a lot of field service businesses buy the wrong software. Usually it's not because they didn't research enough. It's because they were comparing the wrong things. ServiceTitan and FieldEdge both target HVAC, plumbing, and electrical shops. They both handle scheduling and dispatch. They both claim to solve your operational problems. But they're built for different business stages, and picking the wrong one costs you time, money, and a lot of frustrated techs.

Here's what the data actually says in 2026, without the vendor spin.

## Who Each Platform Is Actually Built For

**ServiceTitan is built for growth-stage and larger field service companies that want a deep, all-in-one operating system. FieldEdge is built for smaller HVAC, plumbing, and electrical shops that want solid scheduling, dispatch, and QuickBooks-friendly workflows without the complexity or cost of a platform like ServiceTitan.**

If your business has 10 or more technicians, you're tracking marketing spend seriously, and you want customer portals, two-way texting, and online self-scheduling baked into your software, ServiceTitan is the more natural fit. The platform goes deep on reporting, customer engagement, and analytics in ways FieldEdge doesn't.

FieldEdge is a better match for smaller operations. If you've got a tight team, your office manager lives in QuickBooks, and you mostly need solid scheduling and dispatch with a mobile app your techs won't hate, FieldEdge gets the job done at a lower entry cost and with less onboarding friction.

I'll be direct: the size of your business and your relationship with QuickBooks are probably the two biggest factors in this decision.

## Core Features: Where They Overlap and Where They Don't

**Both platforms cover scheduling, dispatch, invoicing, payments, and mobile access. The gap shows up in customer engagement tools, reporting depth, and integration breadth. ServiceTitan goes considerably further on analytics and CRM features, while FieldEdge keeps things simpler and more focused on day-to-day job management.**

Here's how they stack up across the key feature areas:

| Feature Area | ServiceTitan | FieldEdge |
|---|---|---|
| Scheduling and dispatch | Strong, with advanced tools | Strong, core selling point |
| CRM and customer comms | Two-way texting, customer portals, online self-scheduling | CRM included, less expansive |
| Mobile app | Job details, scheduling, comms; desktop parity issues noted | Accessible, field-team friendly |
| Payments and invoicing | Full end-to-end workflow | Supported |
| QuickBooks integration | Available; users report glitches and duplicate entries | Cleaner alignment; a primary selling point |
| Reporting and analytics | Deep dashboards; a major differentiator | Supported but lighter |
| Third-party integrations | Broader | More limited |

The QuickBooks situation is worth calling out specifically. ServiceTitan has the integration, but user reviews flag glitches and duplicate entries as recurring pain. FieldEdge was built with QuickBooks-centric shops in mind, and that alignment shows in how the accounting workflow is structured. If your bookkeeper is going to be annoyed by sync errors, that matters.

ServiceTitan's edge in reporting is real. If you want to track technician performance, marketing ROI, revenue by job type, or customer lifetime value with actual dashboard depth, ServiceTitan is the stronger tool. FieldEdge handles reporting, but comparison sources consistently score ServiceTitan higher on analytics complexity.

The mobile app situation on the ServiceTitan side is worth noting. Users report the mobile experience doesn't fully match desktop feature parity, which can frustrate techs who need to do things in the field that only work cleanly on a desktop.

## Pricing: What You'll Actually Pay

**ServiceTitan starts at $398 per month as a platform subscription. FieldEdge starts at $100 per user per month. These pricing models are structured differently, so the comparison isn't straightforward. For very small teams, FieldEdge can be cheaper. For larger teams, ServiceTitan may close the gap depending on quote structure and add-ons.**

These numbers come from SelectHub's 2026 comparison data. Neither vendor publishes clean, complete pricing publicly, so exact setup fees, annual contract requirements, and add-on costs aren't easy to pin down without getting a quote.

Here's the practical math. At $100 per user per month with FieldEdge, a team of five techs plus an office manager runs $600/month. That's $202 more per month than ServiceTitan's $398 starting price. Scale to 10 users and you're looking at $1,000/month for FieldEdge against a ServiceTitan price that may not change much depending on the tier.

The per-user model benefits very small shops. A one or two-person operation could run FieldEdge for $100-200/month. ServiceTitan's $398 floor doesn't make much sense at that scale.

ServiceTitan also carries a reputation for significant implementation costs and setup fees that don't always get mentioned in the headline pricing. Get a full all-in quote from both vendors before making any assumptions about total year-one cost.

## What Real Users Say

**ServiceTitan scores 4.3 out of 5 from 325 reviews on Software Advice and carries a 90% user satisfaction rate from SelectHub's analysis of 208 reviews. FieldEdge scores 4.2 out of 5 from 304 reviews on Software Advice, with an 85% satisfaction rate from SelectHub's pool of 596 reviews. Both have strong review bases; ServiceTitan's scores edge slightly higher.**

### ServiceTitan: The Honest Review Picture

The praise centers on feature depth and the platform's fit for home service businesses. Users describe the interface as intuitive once you're past onboarding. Support staff get called out positively in some review summaries as quick and knowledgeable.

The complaints are consistent: steep learning curve, rough onboarding experience, and QuickBooks integration problems. Users also flag the system as rigid for businesses that need heavy custom workflows. ServiceTitan's SelectHub analyst rating is 85 out of 100, with overall sentiment rated "excellent."

A few patterns I notice in the reviews: the negative feedback tends to cluster around implementation and early adoption. Users who get through that phase seem generally happy. The ones who struggle often say they wish setup support was more hands-on.

### FieldEdge: The Honest Review Picture

FieldEdge users consistently describe the platform as simple and straightforward. That's not a backhanded compliment. For a smaller shop that doesn't want a six-month onboarding process, simplicity is a feature. Scheduling, dispatch, and QuickBooks integration are repeatedly called out as strengths.

The complaints: limited third-party integrations and occasional performance lag during peak times. Users who need connections to tools outside the QuickBooks ecosystem run into more friction. FieldEdge's SelectHub analyst rating is 75 out of 100, with sentiment rated "great."

The integration limitation is a real constraint if your business runs other software that needs to talk to your field service platform. FieldEdge works best when QuickBooks is your accounting hub and you're not depending on a lot of other connected tools.

## Which One Should You Choose?

**For most small-to-mid-sized HVAC, plumbing, or electrical shops that are QuickBooks-centered and want simpler operations, FieldEdge is the more practical choice. ServiceTitan makes more sense for growth-focused companies with larger teams, complex operations, and a budget to match. The wrong answer is picking based on marketing materials instead of actual team size and workflow needs.**

My honest take: ServiceTitan is a genuinely impressive platform and earns its higher ratings. But it's overkill for a lot of the shops that get sold into it. The learning curve and implementation friction are real costs. If your operation doesn't have the admin bandwidth to absorb a complicated rollout, you'll spend the first year fighting the software instead of running your business.

FieldEdge's simpler value proposition is legitimate for the right business. It's not a consolation prize. If you don't need two-way customer texting, deep analytics dashboards, or a broad integration ecosystem, paying for those things in a ServiceTitan subscription doesn't make sense.

Here's a quick framework for the decision:

**Choose ServiceTitan if:**
- You have 10 or more technicians, or are actively scaling toward that
- You want advanced customer engagement tools: portals, two-way texting, online booking
- Deep reporting and analytics are a priority for your operations
- You have the implementation budget and admin support to handle onboarding

**Choose FieldEdge if:**
- You're running a smaller shop with a tighter team
- Your accounting workflow is built around QuickBooks and you want clean alignment
- You want core scheduling, dispatch, and invoicing without a steep adoption curve
- You're cost-conscious and the per-user pricing math works in your favor at your team size

Neither one is a perfect product. Both have real complaints in their review base. The goal is matching the platform to your actual operation, not buying the one with the more impressive demo.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is ServiceTitan worth the cost for a small HVAC company?**

Probably not. ServiceTitan starts at $398 per month and carries a reputation for significant implementation costs on top of that. For a small HVAC shop with a handful of techs, FieldEdge's $100 per user per month model is likely to be cheaper and easier to adopt. ServiceTitan's feature depth pays off at larger team sizes where you actually use what you're paying for.

**How do ServiceTitan and FieldEdge compare on user ratings?**

ServiceTitan scores 4.3 out of 5 from 325 reviews on Software Advice, with a 90% user satisfaction rate from SelectHub's analysis of 208 reviews. FieldEdge scores 4.2 out of 5 from 304 reviews on Software Advice, with an 85% satisfaction rate from SelectHub's pool of 596 reviews. ServiceTitan edges slightly higher, but both are solid.

**Which platform integrates better with QuickBooks?**

FieldEdge. ServiceTitan has a QuickBooks integration, but user reviews across multiple platforms consistently flag sync glitches and duplicate entries as recurring problems. That complaint shows up across ServiceTitan's 325+ Software Advice reviews and is one of the most cited negatives in comparison summaries. FieldEdge was built with QuickBooks-centric operations in mind, and review data positions it as the cleaner option for shops where the bookkeeper lives in QuickBooks.

**Can FieldEdge handle a growing field service business?**

It depends on how fast you're growing and what you need. FieldEdge handles scheduling, dispatch, payments, invoicing, and basic reporting well. But comparison sources consistently describe it as less capable than ServiceTitan for complex analytics, customer engagement at scale, and connecting to a broad ecosystem of third-party tools. If you're scaling past 15-20 techs and want deeper operational visibility, ServiceTitan or a comparable platform is likely the better long-term fit.
