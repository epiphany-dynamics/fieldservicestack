I've talked to enough contractors running Zuper and Housecall Pro to have a real opinion on this. Both tools can run a field service business. But they're not interchangeable, and the wrong pick will cost you weeks of painful re-onboarding. Here's what actually matters in 2026.

## What Each Platform Is Actually Built For

**Zuper targets operations-heavy service businesses: think multi-crew HVAC companies, commercial maintenance teams, facilities contractors, and anyone managing SLAs or equipment histories. Housecall Pro targets residential home service contractors who want scheduling, online booking, invoicing, and marketing in one place without heavy configuration work. The buyer profiles barely overlap.**

That distinction matters more than any individual feature comparison. I've seen solo plumbers try Zuper and bounce off it within a month because the setup time wasn't worth it for their call volume. On the flip side, a 12-tech HVAC company running Housecall Pro can hit a ceiling fast once they need asset tracking or multi-crew SLA management.

Zuper covers service, installation, and maintenance businesses across HVAC, plumbing, electrical, solar, facilities, and telecom. It's built for teams with complex job types, crew assignments, and reporting needs that go beyond basic scheduling.

Housecall Pro is shaped around speed. Book the job, send the estimate, collect the payment, request the review. That's the loop it optimizes for residential contractors.

G2's head-to-head data backs this up. Users rate both platforms about equally on ease of use. Housecall Pro wins on setup speed. Zuper wins on what G2 calls "ease of doing business," which reflects flexibility and support responsiveness.

## Feature Breakdown: Where They Actually Differ

**Zuper has stronger operations infrastructure: asset tracking, contract management, configurable status workflows, SLA reporting, and deeper CRM/ERP integrations. Housecall Pro has stronger customer-facing tools: online self-booking, built-in email marketing, review automation, and a Client Hub where customers approve quotes and pay invoices without a phone call.**

### Scheduling and Dispatch

Both platforms have drag-and-drop scheduling. Both handle recurring jobs and technician assignments.

Zuper goes further with skill-based dispatch, multi-day jobs, crew assignments, and GPS stamping for time tracking. The workflow engine lets you enforce steps, like requiring a checklist before a job status can change. Useful for teams running structured service SOPs.

Housecall Pro's scheduling is faster to learn. The customer self-booking widget is a real differentiator at this price point. You embed it on your website and customers schedule their own appointments. For a residential HVAC or cleaning company, that feature alone can justify the subscription cost.

### Mobile App

Both apps cover the basics: job details, photos, notes, customer signatures, and payments.

Zuper's app does GPS stamping and time tracking. There's a recurring complaint in user reviews though: photo uploads can be slow, and the app locks the screen during uploads. That's genuinely annoying when a tech is trying to move between jobs.

Housecall Pro's app gets consistent praise for being quick. Techs can check in, finish, invoice, and collect payment without much friction. For residential routes with high job volume, that speed compounds over a full week.

### Quotes, Invoices, and Payments

Both handle estimates, job conversion, and digital payments.

Housecall Pro makes the billing loop tight. One-click conversion from estimate to job, instant invoicing, multiple payment methods including tap-to-pay. The Client Hub lets customers review and approve quotes without anyone being on the phone. That shortens the cash cycle for residential service calls.

Zuper's configurable rate cards are better for commercial or industrial pricing structures where you need more flexibility in how jobs get quoted.

### Customer Communication and Marketing

This is where Housecall Pro separates from most FSM tools at its price tier.

Built-in email marketing, automated review requests, "on my way" texts, job reminders, and follow-up sequences. Those features typically cost extra in competing platforms, or don't exist at all. For a small residential service company trying to compete with bigger operations, having this built in is a real advantage.

Zuper doesn't lead with marketing tools. The focus is operations infrastructure. If marketing automation is a priority, Housecall Pro is the clear choice.

### Asset, Contract, and Inventory Management

Zuper has equipment and asset tracking, maintenance contracts, and service histories. That's important for HVAC companies managing commercial equipment or facilities contractors with ongoing maintenance agreements.

Housecall Pro has no built-in inventory management. Worth noting: Zuper doesn't have native inventory management either. Neither tool solves parts tracking. If inventory is central to how you run jobs, you're adding a third-party integration regardless of which platform you choose.

### Integrations

Zuper connects to HubSpot, Zoho, QuickBooks, Xero, and other tools via API. For teams already running a sales CRM or ERP alongside field operations, this matters.

Housecall Pro integrates with QuickBooks, which covers most residential contractors. One notable issue from 2026 reviews: the QuickBooks sync is one-way only, pushing data from Housecall Pro to QuickBooks but not back. That's a real limitation worth knowing before you commit.

## 2026 Pricing: What You'll Actually Pay

**Housecall Pro starts at $59-$79 per month for one user, scales to $149-$189 per month for up to 5 users on Essentials, and tops out around $300-$329 per month on MAX. Zuper uses quote-based, per-user pricing that isn't publicly listed; most 2026 reviewers describe it as more expensive than Housecall Pro once you add technicians.**

### Housecall Pro Pricing (2026)

| Plan | Monthly Cost (Annual Billing) | Users |
|------|-------------------------------|-------|
| Basic | $59-$79/month | 1 user |
| Essentials | $149-$189/month | Up to 5 users |
| MAX | $300-$329/month | More users + advanced features |

The variance in Basic plan pricing ($59 vs $79) shows up across different sources, so confirm current rates directly with Housecall Pro before budgeting.

Add-ons that push the real monthly cost significantly higher:

- Price Book powered by Profit Rhino: $149/month
- Vehicle GPS tracking: $20/vehicle/month
- Sales Proposals (if not on MAX): $40/month

Housecall Pro markets itself as flexible with no long-term contracts. That's largely accurate for the base plan. The add-ons stack up fast if you're on a lower tier and need full functionality.

### Zuper Pricing (2026)

Zuper doesn't publish pricing publicly. They structure it by edition, roughly Starter through Enterprise, with per-user, per-month billing and minimum user counts. You need a sales conversation to get actual numbers.

Independent review sites consistently describe Zuper as the heavier-duty, higher-cost option compared to lightweight residential tools. If you're running under five techs doing residential work, the cost difference is hard to justify given what Zuper adds at that scale.

## Pros and Cons

**Zuper wins on operational depth: configurable workflows, asset and contract management, SLA tracking, and enterprise integrations. Housecall Pro wins on ease of use, setup speed, built-in marketing, and customer communication. Neither is the wrong answer. They're just built for different operations.**

### Zuper

Pros:
- Strong multi-crew scheduling with skill-based dispatch and GPS time tracking
- Asset and contract management for equipment-heavy service businesses
- Configurable job workflows with enforced checklists and status controls
- Integrations with CRMs and accounting platforms including QuickBooks and Xero
- Rated well for support quality and flexibility on G2

Cons:
- No transparent pricing; requires a sales conversation to get real numbers
- Photo uploads can be slow and lock the app screen in the field
- Navigation bugs reported, including address lookups pulling incorrect locations
- Can feel like overkill for small residential teams
- Some workflow customization requests reportedly difficult to get implemented

### Housecall Pro

Pros:
- Fast setup; most users are operational within a day or two
- Built-in email marketing and review automation at no extra cost on most plans
- Online self-booking widget is a genuine differentiator for residential contractors
- Client Hub streamlines quote approval and payment collection
- No long-term contracts required

Cons:
- No native route optimization; requires a third-party tool like Beline Routes
- QuickBooks sync is one-way only as of 2026 review coverage
- No built-in inventory management
- Add-ons for Price Book ($149/month), GPS ($20/vehicle/month), and Sales Proposals ($40/month) raise the real monthly cost substantially

## Which One Should You Actually Choose?

**Pick Zuper if you run multi-crew operations, manage commercial service contracts, need SLA tracking, or are integrating with a CRM or ERP. Pick Housecall Pro if you're a residential contractor who wants fast setup, built-in marketing, and a polished customer experience. The gap between these tools is a feature gap, not a quality gap.**

My honest take: if you're running a 1-5 tech residential service company, Housecall Pro is the easier answer. Setup is fast, the customer communication tools are genuinely good, and the pricing is predictable. The one-way QuickBooks sync and missing route optimization are real annoyances, but they're workable.

If you're running a larger team with commercial contracts, equipment service histories, or crews that need SLA accountability, Zuper's operational depth is worth the higher cost and longer setup. Just go in knowing you'll need a sales conversation to get real pricing, and plan for actual implementation time.

One more thing worth saying before you decide: neither tool handles inventory natively. If parts tracking is central to your operations, you're adding a third-party solution no matter which direction you go. Factor that into your total cost comparison.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is Housecall Pro cheaper than Zuper?**

Yes, in most cases. Housecall Pro's Basic plan starts at $59-$79 per month for one user, and the Essentials plan covers up to 5 users for $149-$189 per month. Zuper uses quote-based pricing that most 2026 reviewers describe as coming in higher than lightweight residential tools once you scale past a few technicians. The gap widens when you account for Zuper's minimum user counts and implementation requirements.

**Does Housecall Pro integrate with QuickBooks?**

Yes, but as of 2026 review coverage, the sync is one-way only. Data pushes from Housecall Pro to QuickBooks, not back the other direction. If you need full two-way sync, you'll need a workaround or a platform that supports bi-directional QuickBooks integration out of the box.

**Does Zuper have a free trial?**

Zuper does not offer a publicly available free trial; pricing and onboarding go through a sales conversation. Housecall Pro offers a 14-day free trial, primarily on higher-tier plans. If you want to test the product hands-on before committing, Housecall Pro gives you that option without a sales call first.

**Which is better for HVAC companies?**

It depends on your operation size and customer mix. Residential HVAC contractors with 1-5 techs will generally find Housecall Pro easier to set up and better suited to their customer communication needs. HVAC companies managing commercial accounts, equipment maintenance contracts, SLA requirements, or larger crews will get more out of Zuper's asset tracking and operational depth.
