If you're running a field service business and trying to pick between Connecteam and Housecall Pro, I get it. Both are popular. Both have vocal fans. But they're built for different problems, and picking the wrong one will cost you time and money. I've spent enough time inside both platforms to have a real opinion here.

The short version: Connecteam is a workforce management tool that happens to work well for field teams. Housecall Pro is a field service business platform that includes workforce tools. That distinction matters more than any feature comparison.

## What Each Tool Is Actually Built For

**Connecteam is an employee management platform designed for deskless workers, handling time tracking, scheduling, communication, and task management across mobile teams. Housecall Pro is a business operations platform for trades businesses, covering scheduling, invoicing, payments, CRM, and marketing. The overlap is real, but the core focus is completely different.**

Connecteam started as a solution for non-desk teams across industries. Think retail, hospitality, construction, cleaning. The core product is built around keeping employees organized and accountable. GPS time tracking, digital forms, team chats, shift scheduling. It's genuinely good at all of this.

Housecall Pro has a narrower target: HVAC, plumbing, electrical, pest control, landscaping. The whole product is shaped around the job lifecycle. Customer books a job, tech gets dispatched, tech does the work, invoice gets sent, payment gets collected. That loop is where Housecall Pro shines.

Neither is a bad choice. They just solve different headaches.

## Core Features: Where Each Platform Wins

**Connecteam leads on workforce tools, earning a 4.84-5.0 rating for time tracking and forms, while Housecall Pro wins on business operations with a 5.0 rating for customer history and billing. Housecall Pro has more total features (around 164-300 vs. Connecteam's 144), but Connecteam's workforce tools go deeper where it counts for team management.**

### Connecteam's Strongest Features

The time tracking here is legitimately solid. GPS clock-in, automated timesheets, exportable reports for payroll. If you're tired of paper timesheets or employees padding their hours, this fixes it. The geofence feature on the Expert plan lets you set location boundaries so employees can only clock in when they're actually on site.

Task management and forms are also a real strength. You can build custom checklists, job forms, inspection sheets. Employees fill them out on mobile, and the data flows back to you in real time. For businesses doing compliance-heavy work, this is valuable.

Communication tools cover in-app chat, surveys, and a knowledge base. I've seen small businesses basically throw out their group texts and switch entirely to Connecteam's chat. It's not Slack, but for a 15-person field crew, it works.

### Housecall Pro's Strongest Features

Scheduling and dispatching is the core of this product. The drag-and-drop calendar, real-time technician tracking, and automated customer notifications (the 'your tech is on the way' texts) work well out of the box.

The billing and payments integration is what really separates it from workforce-only tools. Invoices go out from the field, customers pay electronically, and everything syncs to QuickBooks. For a service business processing dozens of jobs per week, this alone is worth the price.

CRM functionality is genuinely useful. Full customer history, job notes, equipment records. When a customer calls back six months later, your tech knows what was done last time without a phone tag marathon.

The marketing tools are a bonus that becomes meaningful as you grow: email campaigns, review requests, online booking. Connecteam has none of this.

## Pricing: The Real Numbers

**Connecteam starts at $29 per month for up to 30 users, while Housecall Pro starts at $79 per month (or $59 annually) for a single user. For small crews, Connecteam is significantly cheaper. For solo operators focused on billing efficiency, Housecall Pro's higher cost is often justified by the time saved on invoicing and payment collection.**

Here's the full breakdown:

| Plan | Connecteam | Housecall Pro |
|------|-----------|---------------|
| Entry tier | $29/mo (up to 30 users) | $79/mo ($59 annual) |
| Mid tier | $49/mo | $189/mo ($149 annual) |
| Top tier | $99/mo | Custom (MAX plan) |
| Per-user pricing | Yes | No (flat tiers) |

Connecteam's pricing model is a meaningful advantage for larger teams. If you're running 20 technicians, a flat $29/month is a serious bargain. Housecall Pro's Essentials plan at $189/month hits harder but covers more of your operational stack.

One thing the pricing tables don't show: you might end up paying for both. A business with 12 techs might use Housecall Pro for billing and CRM while running Connecteam for time tracking and team chat. It's not ideal, but it happens because neither platform fully covers both needs.

## Pros and Cons: What Real Users Actually Say

**Both platforms score 9.4/10 for small business fit, but Connecteam pulls ahead for medium-sized companies at 9.5 vs. Housecall Pro's 8.4. Housecall Pro holds a 4.7/5 rating on Capterra across 2,739 reviews. Connecteam users consistently praise its communication tools and UX, while Housecall Pro users frequently flag the pricing jump as team size grows.**

### Connecteam

What users love: The interface is accessible to non-tech workers. I've seen 60-year-old HVAC techs learn it in an afternoon. The scheduling and time tracking tools consolidate what used to take three separate apps. Support responsiveness is frequently cited as above average on G2.

The real complaints: No invoicing, no payments, no CRM. If billing is central to your operations, this is a genuine gap, not a minor inconvenience. There are also reported issues with the time clock specifically: employees forgetting to log out, which can inflate recorded hours by 20-30 hours in extreme cases. That's a real problem for payroll accuracy.

### Housecall Pro

What users love: The end-to-end job flow. Customer in, invoice out, payment received, all within one system. The mobile app is consistently rated highly by field technicians who aren't especially tech-forward. The customer management tools genuinely change how businesses handle repeat clients.

The real complaints: Pricing is a sticking point as you add team members. Moving from Basic to Essentials is a $110/month jump. And despite having GPS tracking, the workforce management depth doesn't match Connecteam for detailed timesheets or internal communication.

## Integrations

**Both Connecteam and Housecall Pro connect with QuickBooks and Google Calendar. Housecall Pro adds Mailchimp and Gusto, making it stronger for businesses that combine service operations with email marketing or Gusto-based payroll. Connecteam focuses more on HR and payroll platform connections that support workforce management workflows.**

The Mailchimp connection in Housecall Pro is worth calling out. It turns your customer database into an email marketing list without any manual export work. If you're running seasonal promotions or trying to drive repeat business, that's a real time-saver.

Connecteam's integrations lean toward payroll accuracy rather than marketing reach. If you're already running a specific payroll provider, check compatibility before committing.

## Who Should Choose Which

**If your biggest operational pain points are scheduling, invoicing, and customer management, choose Housecall Pro. If your biggest pain points are time tracking, employee accountability, and team communication, choose Connecteam. For most trades businesses billing dozens of jobs per week, Housecall Pro's integrated revenue tools will drive more immediate ROI.**

Here's how I actually think about the decision:

Choose Connecteam if:
- You have 10+ field employees and need solid time tracking with GPS
- You're already handling billing through QuickBooks or another tool and just need workforce management
- Budget matters and you're managing a large crew (the flat $29/month entry plan scales well)
- Internal communication and team scheduling is your current chaos

Choose Housecall Pro if:
- You're in HVAC, plumbing, electrical, pest control, or a similar trade
- You want scheduling, invoicing, payments, and CRM in one place
- You're growing and want marketing tools built into your operations platform
- You're a solo operator or small team where one platform handling everything makes operational sense

One edge case worth noting: cleaning companies and property maintenance businesses with large hourly workforces might actually be better served by Connecteam, even though Housecall Pro markets broadly to home services.

## The Bottom Line

**Connecteam and Housecall Pro solve different problems. Connecteam wins on workforce management at a better price point for larger teams. Housecall Pro wins on revenue operations for trades businesses. The right choice comes down to whether your biggest daily friction is managing employees or managing the full job lifecycle from booking to payment.**

I don't think there's a universally correct answer here. Don't evaluate these tools against each other based on feature count. Evaluate them against your actual daily problems.

For a 3-person HVAC shop trying to get invoices out faster and collect payment on site, Housecall Pro at $79/month solves a real revenue problem. For a 25-person pest control operation struggling with timesheet accuracy and employee communication, Connecteam at $29/month for the whole team is a no-brainer.

If you're somewhere in the middle, with billing complexity and workforce complexity, you might end up trialing both. Both have free trials. Actually run a real week of work through whichever you're considering before you commit. A demo is not the same as your real workflow.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can Connecteam replace Housecall Pro for a plumbing business?**

Probably not fully. Connecteam doesn't have invoicing, payments, or CRM built in, which are core to how most plumbing businesses operate. Housecall Pro's Capterra score is 4.7/5 across 2,739 reviews specifically from trades businesses, reflecting how well it fits that use case. Connecteam would still cover time tracking and scheduling, but you'd need a separate billing tool alongside it.

**What's the actual price difference for a team of 15 technicians?**

At 15 technicians, Connecteam's Basic plan covers the team at $29/month flat (it handles up to 30 users). Housecall Pro's Essentials plan runs $149/month on an annual billing cycle. The gap is around $120/month, which adds up to over $1,400/year in base platform costs. Housecall Pro covers more operational ground for that price, but for pure workforce management, Connecteam is the clear budget winner.

**Which platform has better mobile app reviews?**

Both rate well, but Housecall Pro consistently gets praised for its mobile usability by field technicians specifically. It holds a 4.6/5 ease-of-use score on Capterra. Connecteam's mobile app handles communication and time tracking well but isn't designed around the job dispatch workflow that Housecall Pro centers on. For technicians running from job to job, Housecall Pro's field-facing UX tends to feel more natural.

**Does Housecall Pro have GPS tracking like Connecteam?**

Yes, Housecall Pro includes GPS tracking for technicians, available starting on the Essentials plan ($149/month annually). However, Connecteam's GPS time clock is more granular for workforce management, with geofencing available on its Expert plan at $99/month. For technician location tracking tied to specific jobs, Housecall Pro works well. For detailed workforce accountability with location-based clock-in enforcement, Connecteam goes meaningfully deeper.
