<p><strong>Service Autopilot starts at $47/month and Jobber starts at $49/month, but price barely separates them. The real difference is workflow. Service Autopilot is built for automation-heavy recurring service businesses: lawn care, cleaning, pest control. Jobber wins on mobile quality, ease of use, and fast setup. For most field service teams under 10 people, Jobber is the easier and safer pick.</strong></p>

<h2>What Each Platform Actually Does</h2>

<p><strong>Service Autopilot and Jobber both handle scheduling, invoicing, and dispatching, but they are designed for different operating modes. Service Autopilot leans into automated workflows built for recurring route businesses. Jobber focuses on clean job management for variable-schedule trades. They overlap heavily in features but differ sharply in complexity, setup time, and who they're built for.</strong></p>

<p>These two tools come up constantly in lawn care and cleaning Facebook groups, and the debate usually comes down to one question: how much automation do you actually need right now?</p>

<p>Service Autopilot built its reputation on automations. The software can send a follow-up email three days after a quote, flag lapsed customers for re-engagement, and auto-generate invoices the moment a job closes. It was built for businesses that run the same clients week after week, route after route. Lawn care operations with 80-plus weekly stops, cleaning businesses with a locked-in recurring client list, pest control routes on a quarterly cycle. That's SA's sweet spot, and it genuinely delivers there.</p>

<p>Jobber built its reputation on being the one tool your whole crew can use without a training seminar. The dispatch board is clean. The mobile app doesn't fight you. The client portal is something you can actually send to customers without apologizing for how it looks. Jobber skews toward HVAC, plumbing, electrical, and general contracting, where jobs vary in scope and rarely follow a predictable weekly pattern. That said, plenty of smaller lawn care and cleaning shops run on Jobber just fine, especially under 50 active clients.</p>

<h2>Mobile App, QuickBooks, and Day-to-Day Use</h2>

<p><strong>Jobber's mobile app is consistently rated higher in app stores and generates far fewer complaints from field techs. Service Autopilot's mobile app works but has a steeper curve and requires more upfront configuration. Both integrate with QuickBooks Online, but Jobber's sync is generally cleaner, with fewer duplicate records and less manual cleanup required on the accounting side.</strong></p>

<p>This is where the decision gets practical. If you have a tech who struggles with smartphones, Jobber's mobile app is what you want. The interface is clean, jobs are easy to find, and the flow from check-in to close-out takes about five minutes to learn. I've heard directly from landscaping and cleaning crews who switched off SA specifically because their field guys were fighting the app and using it wrong, which meant the office was spending time fixing data instead of running the business.</p>

<p>Service Autopilot's mobile app is more powerful in theory. You can access automations, client history, and job notes on the go. The problem is the UI isn't as forgiving, and first-week adoption is rougher. That's not a dealbreaker if you have patient crew members willing to learn. It is a real problem if your techs are experienced tradespeople who want to get in, close the job, and get to the next one.</p>

<p>On QuickBooks: Jobber's integration has a better reputation for cleanliness, with fewer double-entry issues and clearer invoice mapping. SA's integration works but has more moving parts, and users report needing to watch the sync more carefully. Neither platform completely eliminates manual cleanup by tax time. That's just the reality of syncing job management software with accounting software.</p>

<h2>Who Should Pick Which</h2>

<p><strong>Service Autopilot fits lawn care, cleaning, and pest control businesses running 50-plus recurring accounts per week where automation is the priority. Jobber fits HVAC, plumbing, electrical, and smaller service businesses where mobile usability and quick deployment matter more than deep automation. Most businesses under 3 trucks and 40 recurring clients will be better served by Jobber.</strong></p>

<h3>Pick Service Autopilot If...</h3>

<p>You're running a lawn care or cleaning operation with 50 or more weekly accounts and you're spending real time every week chasing invoices and sending follow-ups by hand. SA's automation engine solves that specific problem well. Configure it once and the software handles follow-up sequences, re-engages inactive clients, and closes invoices automatically when a job wraps.</p>

<p>The <a href="/guides/best-landscaping-business-software-2026">best landscaping business software guide</a> breaks down how SA stacks up against the other tools in that category. SA consistently ranks near the top for landscaping specifically because of the route tools and recurring billing setup.</p>

<p>Pick SA if chemical tracking is a compliance requirement. If you're a licensed pesticide applicator, Jobber doesn't offer that. SA does.</p>

<p>The honest threshold here: if you're below 3 trucks or under 40 recurring accounts, SA's setup overhead is probably more than you need. You'll spend weeks getting it configured and then underuse half of what you're paying for. Scale into it.</p>

<h3>Pick Jobber If...</h3>

<p>You're in HVAC, plumbing, electrical, roofing, or general contracting. Your jobs vary in scope, your schedule isn't a repeating weekly route, and you need something your techs can use on day one. Jobber fits that operating model without asking much of you upfront.</p>

<p>Pick Jobber if you're a solo operator or a 2-3 person shop that wants clean, reliable software without overbuilding your tech stack. The Core plan at $49/month gives you scheduling and invoicing that actually work. Upgrade to Connect when you add a second truck and need the online booking and estimate e-sign features.</p>

<p>Jobber also wins on the client-facing side. The client portal and booking page look professional on mobile. That matters when you're trying to convert leads who are comparing you to a competitor. The first impression from your software isn't nothing.</p>

<h2>Final Verdict</h2>

<p><strong>Jobber wins for most field service businesses under 10 people. It deploys faster, handles non-technical crews better, and the mobile app holds up in the field. Service Autopilot wins for automation-heavy recurring service operations past the 3-4 truck mark. Both are legitimate tools. The mistake most operators make is buying Service Autopilot before they're actually ready for it.</strong></p>

<p>The honest truth is that most field service operators would be better served by starting with Jobber. You'll be running real jobs inside a week. SA has a genuine payoff, but that payoff shows up at scale. If you're doing 100 lawn care stops per week and burning hours on invoicing and re-engagement emails, SA's automation engine is worth every dollar of setup time. If you're running a 5-person HVAC crew doing 8-12 calls per day, Jobber covers everything you need at a lower total cost and with less operational friction.</p>

<p>Don't get caught up in which tool has more features. The question is which one matches your actual operating model today. Service Autopilot is a purpose-built power tool for a specific kind of business. Jobber is a reliable everyday platform that scales reasonably well without demanding much of you.</p>

<p>Both offer free trials. SA has a demo program. Jobber runs a 14-day free trial with no credit card required. Run your real workflow through each one before you commit.</p>

Jobber accommodates recurring work but wasn't designed with route-heavy, repeat-client automation in mind, so it works better for variable-scope trades like HVAC and plumbing.</p>

<p>Service Autopilot can send follow-up emails days after quotes, automatically flag inactive clients for re-engagement, and generate invoices the moment jobs close—all without manual intervention. Jobber handles the core job-to-invoice flow but lacks these multi-step automation workflows, requiring team members to manually trigger follow-ups and reminders.</p>
<h3>Q: Why is Jobber better for small crews under 10 people?</h3>
<p>Jobber's clean dispatch board and field-ready mobile app require minimal training and setup time, letting small teams deploy it immediately without weeks of configuration. Service Autopilot's power comes from extensive automation setup, which demands more initial investment—better suited for teams large enough to leverage complex workflows and dedicated administrators.</p>
